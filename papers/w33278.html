Non-Profit Hospital Governance, Conduct, and CEO Pay

非营利医院治理、行为与CEO薪酬

Daniel Kessler, William Wygal

环境与健康 未指明区域

Abstract

We investigate whether the membership of a non-profit hospital’s CEO on its board of directors is associated with CEO pay and several measures of hospital performance, including price, operating margin, quality, and service to low-income patients. Although the consequences of CEO board membership for for-profit firms have been studied extensively, the consequences for non-profits in general, and non-profit hospitals in particular, have received less attention. Because most hospitals are non-profit and non-profit hospital prices have increased rapidly over the past 20 years, this gap is important. We find a strong positive association between CEO board membership and non-profit hospital prices, operating margins, and CEO pay, with a weaker positive (negative) association between CEO board membership and quality (service to low-income patients). We conclude that non-profit hospitals’ CEO board membership is likely associated with increases in agency costs due to a lack of separation between management and control.

中文摘要

我们研究了非营利医院CEO担任董事会成员是否与CEO薪酬以及多项医院绩效指标（包括价格、营业利润率、质量和对低收入患者的服务）相关。尽管CEO担任董事会成员对营利性公司的影响已被广泛研究，但其对非营利组织（尤其是非营利医院）的影响受到的关注较少。由于大多数医院是非营利性的，且非营利医院的价格在过去20年中迅速上涨，这一研究空白至关重要。我们发现，CEO担任董事会成员与非营利医院的价格、营业利润率和CEO薪酬之间存在强烈的正相关关系，而与质量（对低收入患者的服务）之间存在较弱的正（负）相关关系。我们得出结论，非营利医院CEO担任董事会成员很可能因管理层与控制权缺乏分离而导致代理成本增加。
