Trends in Health Inequalities among Spanish Retirees

西班牙退休人员健康不平等趋势

Cristina Bellés-Obrero, Manuel Flores Mallo, Pilar García-Gómez, Sergi Jimenez-Martin, Judit Vall Castelló

环境与健康 全球/多国

Abstract

Spain, with one of the highest life expectancies globally and a rapidly ageing population, faces growing challenges in sustaining its pension, healthcare, and long-term care systems. This study examines trends in health inequalities among retired Spaniards from 2004 to 2022, using eight waves of the Survey of Health, Ageing and Retirement in Europe (SHARE). We analyse five health outcomes—limitations in daily and instrumental activities, number of chronic conditions, a composite health deficiency index, mental health (EURO-D scale), and cognitive performance—and use linear regression to assess income-related gradients, adjusted for age and sex. We also compute a catch-up time measure—the number of years a poorer individual would need to reach the same level of health as a richer individual—and concentration indices of bad health. We then examine how these inequalities change over time, allowing us to explore the potential influence of pension reforms within the context of Spain’s Beveridge-style healthcare system and tax-funded long-term care provision. Our results show no clear evidence that health inequality has increased from 2004 to 2022. These findings contribute to understanding how income disparities interact with social protection systems in ageing societies and inform the design of equitable health, long-term care, and pension policies.

中文摘要

西班牙拥有全球最高的预期寿命之一且人口迅速老龄化，在维持其养老金、医疗保健和长期护理体系方面面临日益严峻的挑战。本研究利用欧洲健康、老龄化和退休调查的八轮数据，考察了2004年至2022年间西班牙退休人员健康不平等的趋势。我们分析了五项健康结果——日常活动限制和工具性活动限制、慢性病数量、综合健康缺陷指数、心理健康（EURO-D量表）和认知表现——并使用线性回归评估经年龄和性别调整后的收入相关梯度。我们还计算了一个追赶时间指标——一个较贫困个体需要多少年才能达到与较富裕个体相同的健康水平——以及不良健康的集中指数。然后，我们考察了这些不平等如何随时间变化，从而得以探索在西班牙贝弗里奇式医疗体系和税收资助的长期护理提供背景下养老金改革的潜在影响。我们的结果显示，没有明确证据表明健康不平等在2004年至2022年间有所加剧。这些发现有助于理解在老龄化社会中收入差距如何与社会保护体系相互作用，并为设计公平的健康、长期护理和养老金政策提供信息。
