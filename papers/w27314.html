Does Poverty Change Labor Supply? Evidence from Multiple Income Effects and 115,579 Bags

贫困会改变劳动力供给吗？来自多重收入效应和115,579个袋子的证据

Abhijit Banerjee, Dean Karlan, Hannah Trachtman, Christopher R. Udry

理论与方法 未指明区域

Abstract

The income elasticity of labor supply is a central parameter of many economic models. We test the response of labor supply to exogenous changes in income using data from a randomized evaluation of a multi-faceted grant program in northern Ghana combined with a researcher-implemented bag-making operation. We find a non-negative "income effect" on labor supply. We argue that simple models with either labor or capital market frictions cannot explain the results, whereas a model that allows for positive physiological or psychological productivity effects from higher income fits with our findings.

中文摘要

劳动力供给的收入弹性是许多经济模型的核心参数。我们利用加纳北部一项多层面赠款项目的随机评估数据，结合研究者实施的制袋操作，检验了劳动力供给对外生收入变化的反应。我们发现劳动力供给存在非负的“收入效应”。我们认为，仅包含劳动力或资本市场摩擦的简单模型无法解释这一结果，而允许更高收入带来正向生理或心理生产力效应的模型则与我们的发现相符。
