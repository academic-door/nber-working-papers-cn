Reassessing the Role of Standardized Tests in University Admissions

重新评估标准化考试在大学招生中的作用

Johann D. Gaebler, Calvin Isley, Christopher Avery, Sharad Goel

教育与劳动 美国

Abstract

There is a long-running debate over using standardized test scores to inform college and graduate admissions decisions, with some arguing that test scores are an important signal of academic strength and others arguing that they are biased and exclusionary. Here we revisit this issue by analyzing a novel dataset of more than 13,000 applications over roughly a decade to a large public policy master’s program in the United States. Consistent with past work, we find that GRE scores substantially improve predictions of first-year grades relative to predictions based on GPA alone. However, when these predictions are used to inform admissions decisions, we find that test scores only modestly improve the expected academic quality of admitted students. The gap shrinks further when we augment the test-aware and test-blind predictive models with more fine-grained information available in student transcripts and other application materials. Specifically, we estimate that incorporating standardized test scores in our setting would result in admitting students who perform, on average, only 0.03 grade points better. We show—both empirically and theoretically—that this pattern stems from a subtle distinction between predictions and decisions. Even with improved predictions, the downstream admissions decisions are often the same; and where there are differences, they often involve selecting between similarly qualified applicants. Our results indicate that standardized test scores may be less important for university admissions than previously suggested.

中文摘要

关于是否使用标准化考试成绩来指导大学和研究生招生决策存在长期争论，一些人认为考试成绩是学术实力的重要信号，另一些人则认为它们具有偏见性和排斥性。在此，我们通过分析一个新颖的数据集重新审视这一问题，该数据集包含美国一个大型公共政策硕士项目在大约十年间收到的超过13,000份申请。与以往研究一致，我们发现相对于仅基于GPA的预测，GRE成绩显著改善了对一年级成绩的预测。然而，当这些预测被用于指导招生决策时，我们发现考试成绩仅适度提高了录取学生的预期学术质量。当我们用学生成绩单和其他申请材料中可获得的更精细信息来增强考虑考试和不考虑考试的预测模型时，这一差距进一步缩小。具体而言，我们估计在我们的情境中纳入标准化考试成绩，平均只会导致录取学生的成绩提高0.03分。我们——通过实证和理论——表明，这种模式源于预测与决策之间的微妙区别。即使预测得到改善，下游的招生决策往往相同；而在存在差异的地方，它们通常涉及在资质相似的申请者之间进行选择。我们的结果表明，标准化考试成绩对大学招生的影响可能比之前认为的要小。
