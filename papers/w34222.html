Missing Markets for Innovation: Evidence from New Uses for Existing Drugs

创新的缺失市场：来自现有药物新用途的证据

Eric Budish, Maya M. Durvasula, Benjamin N. Roin, Heidi L. Williams

金融与资产定价 未指明区域

Abstract

We investigate missing markets for innovation: contexts where intellectual property rights necessary to incentivize research investment are either unavailable or unenforceable. A simple theoretical model formalizes the conditions that yield missing investment. We identify an empirical setting—research into new uses for existing drugs— with sharp variation in the enforceability of intellectual property rights on comparable inventions. We show that when intellectual property rights become unenforceable, research investment and commercialization nearly cease. Estimates of the value of this missing investment are in the trillions of dollars. We discuss the implications of these findings for the design of innovation policy.

中文摘要

我们研究了创新的缺失市场：即激励研究投资所需的知识产权要么不可用、要么不可执行的情境。一个简单的理论模型形式化了导致投资缺失的条件。我们确定了一个实证环境——对现有药物新用途的研究——其中可比发明的知识产权可执行性存在显著差异。我们表明，当知识产权变得不可执行时，研究投资和商业化几乎停止。对这种缺失投资价值的估计高达数万亿美元。我们讨论了这些发现对创新政策设计的启示。
