Economic Incentives and Organ Procurement: Evidence from a U.S. Reform

经济激励与器官获取：来自美国改革的证据

Erkut Y. Ozbay, Ariel Rava, Sergio S. Urzúa, Emanuel Zur

公共财政与税收 美国

Abstract

In 2019, the United States implemented new regulations for Organ Procurement Organizations (OPOs) aimed at increasing transparency and strengthening accountability. These rules introduced stronger performance incentives designed to improve organ procurement outcomes. This paper examines the impact of the reform on organ donations, transplant activity, and associated system costs, with a particular focus on kidneys, given their clinical characteristics and the structure of the reform’s organizational incentives. We estimate that the new policy led to an average increase of 7.13 kidneys procured per OPO per month, relative to a baseline mean of 24.78. The largest effects are observed among top-performing OPOs prior to the reform, with similar patterns for transplanted organs. Consistent with these findings, we document an overall rise in total OPO costs, with statistically significant increases concentrated among low-performing OPOs. Finally, we present a back-of-the-envelope calculation estimating the fiscal impact of the reform.

中文摘要

2019年，美国对器官获取组织（OPO）实施了旨在提高透明度和加强问责制的新规定。这些规则引入了更强的绩效激励，旨在改善器官获取结果。本文考察了该改革对器官捐赠、移植活动及相关系统成本的影响，特别关注肾脏，这是基于其临床特征以及改革组织激励的结构。我们估计，相对于改革前每月平均24.78个肾脏的基线水平，新政策导致每个OPO每月平均多获取7.13个肾脏。改革前表现最佳的OPO效果最大，移植器官也呈现类似模式。与这些发现一致，我们记录了OPO总成本的总体上升，其中统计上显著的增加集中在表现不佳的OPO。最后，我们通过一个粗略计算来估算改革的财政影响。
