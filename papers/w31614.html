Cooking, health, and daily exposure to pollution spikes

烹饪、健康与日常污染峰值暴露

Susanna B. Berkouwer, Joshua T. Dean

环境与健康 肯尼亚

Abstract

Many routine daily activities—such as cooking and commuting—cause large recurring pollution spikes that may impact health without significantly affecting average exposure. We study pollution spikes by combining experimental variation in cooking technology with high-frequency data on individual pollution exposure and time-use in Kenya. Improved cookstoves reduce PM2.5 spikes while cooking by 51.3μg/m³ (41%) and cause a 0.24 standard deviation reduction in self-reported respiratory symptoms. However, even after more than three years of daily use, we find no clinical health improvements, possibly because we detect no impact on average exposure. Clinical health improvements may require reductions in ambient concentrations.\n

中文摘要

许多日常活动——例如烹饪和通勤——会导致频繁出现的重大污染峰值，这些峰值可能在不显著影响平均暴露水平的情况下影响健康。我们通过结合烹饪技术的实验性变化与肯尼亚个人污染暴露和时间使用的高频数据，研究了污染峰值。改进的炉灶在烹饪期间将PM2.5峰值降低了51.3μg/m³（41%），并使自我报告的呼吸道症状减少了0.24个标准差。然而，即使在日常使用超过三年后，我们未发现临床健康改善，这可能是因为我们未检测到对平均暴露水平的影响。临床健康改善可能需要降低环境浓度。
