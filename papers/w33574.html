Human Capital at Home: Evidence from a Randomized Evaluation in the Philippines

家庭中的人力资本：来自菲律宾随机评估的证据

Noam Angrist, Sarah Kabay, Dean Karlan, Lincoln Lau, Kevin Wong

教育与劳动 未指明区域

Abstract

Children spend most of their time at home in their early years, yet efforts to promote human capital at home in many low- and middle-income settings remain limited. We conduct a randomized controlled trial to evaluate an intervention which encourages parents and caregivers to foster human capital accumulation among their children between ages 3 and 5, with a focus on math and phonics skills. Children gain 0.52 and 0.51 standard deviations relative to the control group on math and phonics tests, respectively (p<0.001). A year later effects persist, but math gains dissipate to 0.15 (p=0.06) and phonics to 0.13 (p=0.12). We also measure impacts on parents, including both fathers and mothers. Effects appear to be mediated largely through instructional support by parents and not other parent investment mechanisms, such as more positive parent-child interactions or additional time spent on education at home beyond the intervention. We also do not find any crowd-out effects on labor market outcomes, likely since the approach tested is efficient, delivering learning gains in a short period of time.

中文摘要

儿童在早期大部分时间都在家中度过，然而在许多中低收入环境中，促进家庭人力资本的努力仍然有限。我们进行了一项随机对照试验，以评估一项鼓励父母和照顾者在3至5岁儿童中促进人力资本积累的干预措施，重点关注数学和语音技能。与对照组相比，儿童在数学和语音测试中分别获得了0.52和0.51个标准差的提升（p<0.001）。一年后，效果仍然存在，但数学收益降至0.15（p=0.06），语音收益降至0.13（p=0.12）。我们还测量了对父母（包括父亲和母亲）的影响。效果似乎主要通过父母的教学支持来中介，而非其他父母投资机制，例如更积极的亲子互动或超出干预范围的在家庭教育上投入的额外时间。我们也没有发现对劳动力市场结果的任何挤出效应，这可能是因为所测试的方法效率较高，能在短时间内带来学习收益。
