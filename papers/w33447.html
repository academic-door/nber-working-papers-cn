The Hidden Toll of Airborne Lead: Infant Mortality Impacts of Industrial Lead Pollution

空气中铅的隐性代价：工业铅污染对婴儿死亡率的影响

Karen Clay, Edson R. Severnini, Xiao Wang

环境与健康 美国

Abstract

This paper uses U.S. Toxic Release Inventory data on industrial air lead emissions to provide IV estimates of the effects of air lead concentration on infant mortality. The causal effect of lead on infant mortality is identified by annual variation in air fugitive lead emissions interacted with wind speed near reporting plants, which together determine local ambient lead concentration. Unlike stack emissions, which occur routinely and may prompt avoidance behavior, fugitive emissions are intermittent and influenced by both historical and current factors, such as wind speed variation, making them difficult to avoid. The paper has two main findings. First, higher air lead concentration causes higher infant mortality in the first month and in the first year, suggesting that both in utero and environmental exposures matter. Second, higher lead concentration increases deaths from low birthweight, sudden unexplained infant death (SUID), and respiratory and nervous system causes, which is consistent with findings from animal studies, even when accounting for behavioral responses. Back of the envelope calculations indicate that declines in industrial air lead emissions prevented more than 300 infant deaths per year, generating benefots of at least $\$3.5$ billion annually in 2023 dollars.

中文摘要

本文利用美国有毒物质释放清单中关于工业空气铅排放的数据，提供了空气铅浓度对婴儿死亡率影响的工具变量估计。铅对婴儿死亡率的因果效应是通过报告工厂附近年度空气无组织铅排放量与风速的交互作用来识别的，这两者共同决定了当地的环境铅浓度。与常规排放（可能引发规避行为）不同，无组织排放是间歇性的，并受历史和当前因素（如风速变化）的影响，因此难以规避。本文有两个主要发现。首先，较高的空气铅浓度会导致第一个月和第一年内更高的婴儿死亡率，这表明宫内暴露和环境暴露都很重要。其次，较高的铅浓度会增加因低出生体重、婴儿猝死综合征（SUID）以及呼吸和神经系统原因导致的死亡，这与动物研究的发现一致，即使在考虑了行为反应后也是如此。粗略计算表明，工业空气铅排放的减少每年防止了超过300例婴儿死亡，按2023年美元计算，每年产生至少35亿美元的收益。
