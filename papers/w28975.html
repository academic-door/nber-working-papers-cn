What Drives Demand for State-Run Lotteries? Evidence and Welfare Implications

州营彩票需求驱动因素：证据与福利影响

Benjamin Lockwood, Hunt Allcott, Dmitry Taubinsky, Afras Y. Sial

理论与方法 未指明区域

Abstract

We use natural experiments embedded in state-run lotteries and a new nationally representative survey to provide reduced-form and structural estimates of risk preferences and behavioral biases in lottery demand. We ﬁnd that sales respond more to the expected value of the jackpot than to price, but are unresponsive to variation in the second prize—a pattern that implies probability weighting but is inconsistent with standard parameterizations. In the survey, we ﬁnd that lottery spending decreases modestly with income and is strongly associated with measures of innumeracy, poor statistical reasoning, and other proxies for behavioral bias. These bias proxies decline with income and statistically account for 43 percent of lottery purchases, suggesting that at least some of lottery demand is due to behavioral bias, not just anticipatory utility or entertainment value. We use these empirical moments to estimate a model of socially optimal lottery design. In the model, current multi-state lottery designs increase welfare but may harm heavy spenders.

中文摘要

我们利用州营彩票中的自然实验以及一项新的全国代表性调查，对彩票需求中的风险偏好和行为偏差提供了简化形式和结构性估计。研究发现，销售额对头奖预期价值的反应大于对价格的敏感度，但对二等奖的变化不敏感——这一模式意味着概率加权，但与标准参数化不一致。在调查中，我们发现彩票支出随收入增加而适度下降，并与计算能力不足、统计推理能力差以及其他行为偏差指标密切相关。这些偏差指标随收入增加而下降，并在统计上解释了43%的彩票购买行为，这表明至少部分彩票需求源于行为偏差，而不仅仅是预期效用或娱乐价值。我们利用这些实证矩来估计一个社会最优彩票设计模型。在该模型中，当前的多州彩票设计提高了福利，但可能损害高支出者利益。
