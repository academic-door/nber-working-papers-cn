Deterrence Through Response Curves: An Empirical Analysis of the Gaza-Israel Conflict

通过响应曲线进行威慑：加沙-以色列冲突的实证分析

Eli Berman, Prabin B. Khadka, Danny Klinenberg, Esteban Klor

理论与方法 未指明区域

Abstract

We use response curves in a repeated game to analyze key aspects of mutual deterrence: escalation, de-escalation, incomplete deterrence, and deterrence by denial. In this approach, episodes of violence are due to interacting response curves, which disincentivize opponents from attacking through both deterrence and compellence. Both sides punish attacks to maintain credibility in future episodes, disincentivizing larger attacks and yielding nonviolent lulls. We empirically estimate those curves using detailed incident data from the Israel-Gaza conflict between 2007 and 2017. Our estimates match the dynamics of the raw data: very frequent episodes of low lethality violent exchange. Response curves are stable and probably Markovian. They exhibit a posture consistent with incomplete deterrence: i.e., episodes de-escalate, but to a violent equilibrium. Israeli missile defense shifts the Gazan response curve to a less aggressive posture, as predicted by theory.

中文摘要

我们在重复博弈中使用响应曲线来分析相互威慑的关键方面：升级、降级、不完全威慑和拒止威慑。在这种方法中，暴力事件源于相互作用的响应曲线，这些曲线通过威慑和强制手段阻止对手发动攻击。双方惩罚攻击以维持未来事件中的可信度，从而阻止更大规模的攻击并产生非暴力的平静期。我们利用2007年至2017年以色列-加沙冲突的详细事件数据，实证估计了这些曲线。我们的估计与原始数据的动态相符：非常频繁的低致死率暴力交换事件。响应曲线是稳定的，并且可能是马尔可夫性的。它们表现出与不完全威慑一致的状态：即事件降级，但达到一个暴力均衡。正如理论所预测的那样，以色列导弹防御系统将加沙的响应曲线转变为一种不那么激进的姿态。
