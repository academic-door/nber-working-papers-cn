What Good Are Treatment Effects without Treatment? Mental Health and the Reluctance to Use Talk Therapy

没有治疗的治疗效果有何用？心理健康与谈话疗法使用意愿不足

Christopher J. Cronin, Matthew P. Forsstrom, Nicholas W. Papageorge

环境与健康 未指明区域

Abstract

Evidence across disciplines suggests that talk therapy is more curative than antidepressants for mild-to-moderate depression and anxiety. Yet, few patients use it. We develop a dynamic choice model to analyze patient demand for the treatment of depression and anxiety. The model incorporates myriad potential impediments to therapy use along with links between mental health improvements and earnings. The estimated model reveals that mental health improvements are valuable, directly through utility and indirectly through earnings. However, patient reluctance to use therapy is nearly impervious to reasonable counterfactual policies (e.g., lowering prices or removing other costs). Patient behavior might reflect stigma, biases in beliefs about the effectiveness of therapy, or a distaste for discussing personal or painful issues with a stranger. More broadly, the benefits of therapy estimated in randomized trials tell only half the story. If patients do not use treatments outside of an experimental setting—and we fail to understand why or how to get them to—estimated treatment effects cannot be leveraged.

中文摘要

跨学科证据表明，对于轻中度抑郁和焦虑，谈话疗法比抗抑郁药更具治愈效果。然而，很少有患者使用它。我们开发了一个动态选择模型来分析患者对抑郁和焦虑治疗的需求。该模型纳入了众多可能阻碍治疗使用的因素，以及心理健康改善与收入之间的联系。估计模型显示，心理健康改善是有价值的，既直接通过效用，也间接通过收入。然而，患者对使用疗法的抵触情绪几乎不受合理的反事实政策（例如降低价格或消除其他成本）的影响。患者行为可能反映了污名化、对疗法有效性的信念偏差，或不愿与陌生人讨论个人或痛苦问题。更广泛地说，随机试验中估计的疗法益处只讲述了故事的一半。如果患者在实验环境之外不使用治疗——而我们又不理解原因或如何促使他们使用——那么估计的治疗效果就无法被利用。
