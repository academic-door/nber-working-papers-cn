Competitive Exposure and Entrepreneurial Experimentation

竞争暴露与创业实验

Joshua S. Gans

理论与方法 未指明区域

Abstract

Entrepreneurs learn by experimenting, but experiment choices are often public. A closed beta, private pilot, or public launch not only generates evidence; it also reveals what kind of entrepreneur would choose that action. We develop a dynamic model in which a founder chooses between stealthy and public experiments while potential entrants infer from both actions and outcomes. Public outcomes are modelled as garblings of the founder's private experimental evidence, so public leakage informs outsiders without giving the founder information beyond the private signal already observed. The key state variable is competitive exposure: the public runway before entry becomes attractive. Exposure is depleted by two forces, leakage burn from public outcomes, and action burn from public inference about experiment choice. This distinction implies that competition can distort experiment design without forcing earlier scale: lower-confidence founders choose stealthier tests, while higher-confidence founders spend exposure to obtain faster private learning through more public tests. Scale is accelerated only when exposure reduces the value of waiting more than it reduces the value of scaling. Finally, exogenous funding gates make observable scale more selective and, therefore, more informative to entrants. The analysis shows that entrepreneurial experimentation is not merely private learning under uncertainty; it is public action under competitive inference.

中文摘要

企业家通过实验来学习，但实验选择往往是公开的。封闭测试、私人试点或公开发布不仅产生证据；它还揭示了什么样的企业家会选择该行动。我们开发了一个动态模型，其中创始人在隐秘实验和公开实验之间进行选择，而潜在进入者则从行动和结果中进行推断。公开结果被建模为创始人私人实验证据的加噪版本，因此公开泄露告知了外部人士，但并未给创始人提供超出已观察到的私人信号之外的信息。关键状态变量是竞争暴露：即进入变得有吸引力之前的公开跑道。暴露被两种力量消耗：来自公开结果的泄露消耗，以及来自关于实验选择的公开推断的行动消耗。这种区别意味着竞争可以扭曲实验设计而不强制更早的规模化：信心较低的创始人选择更隐秘的测试，而信心较高的创始人则通过更多公开测试消耗暴露以获取更快的私人学习。只有当暴露降低等待价值的幅度大于降低规模化价值的幅度时，规模化才会加速。最后，外生融资门槛使可观察的规模化更具选择性，因此对进入者更具信息量。分析表明，创业实验不仅仅是不确定性下的私人学习；它是在竞争性推断下的公开行动。
