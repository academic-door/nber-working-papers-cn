Dynamic Preference “Reversals” and Time Inconsistency

动态偏好“逆转”与时间不一致性

Philipp Strack, Dmitry Taubinsky

其他主题 未指明区域

Abstract

We study whether it’s possible to identify time-inconsistent preferences in empirical designs where preferences are elicited in advance at time 0, and then again at time 1, after the agent receives additional information. For single-peaked preferences, time-consistency is rejected only when the time-1 ranking between a pair of alternatives is always the reverse of the time-0 ranking. We establish variations and generalizations of this result. Since such stark reversals are rarely observed, choice-revision designs require stronger identification assumptions than perhaps previously appreciated. But we show that time-inconsistency is identifiable in environments where preferences over alternatives can be “priced-out.”

中文摘要

我们研究在实证设计中，当偏好在时间0提前被引出，然后在时间1（在代理人获得额外信息后）再次被引出时，是否可能识别出时间不一致偏好。对于单峰偏好，只有当时间1对一对备选方案的排序总是与时间0的排序相反时，时间一致性才被拒绝。我们建立了这一结果的变体和推广。由于这种明显的逆转很少被观察到，选择修正设计可能需要比之前可能认识到的更强的识别假设。但我们表明，在可以对备选方案偏好进行“定价”的环境中，时间不一致性是可以识别的。
