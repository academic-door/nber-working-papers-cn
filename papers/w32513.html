Trust, Intangible Assets, and Productivity

信任、无形资产与生产率

Gilbert Cette, Jimmy Lopez, Jacques Mairesse, Giuseppe Nicoletti

环境与健康 全球/多国

Abstract

Business environments dominated by information flows and autonomous tasks, typical of knowledge-intensive industries, are likely to require enough social capital to be viable and productive. In this paper, we use new EUKLEMS-INTANProd industry-level data (Bontadini et al., 2023a) covering a panel of 19 countries and 20 industries over the 1995-2018 period to investigate the influence of a key element of social capital – trust – on labour productivity in intangible-intensive industries, controlling for hiring and firing regulations that can constrain the ability of managers to implement best practices productively. We find that in such industries, productivity gains from high levels of trust are stronger than elsewhere, while too strict hiring and firing regulations are more damaging for productivity. Using a more limited sample for which data on management quality are available, we show that the positive impact of high trust on productivity in intangible-intensive industries is channeled by the ability to benefit from good management, a key element of organizational capital. Productivity gains from relatively high levels of trust in knowledge-rich environments are estimated to be sizeable and our estimates survive a number of robustness checks.

中文摘要

以信息流和自主任务为主导的商业环境（典型于知识密集型行业）可能需要足够的社会资本才能维持其可行性和生产率。在本文中，我们利用覆盖1995-2018年期间19个国家和20个行业面板数据的新版EUKLEMS-INTANProd行业层面数据（Bontadini等人，2023a），研究了社会资本的一个关键要素——信任——对无形资产密集型行业劳动生产率的影响，同时控制了可能限制管理者有效实施最佳实践的雇佣和解雇法规。我们发现，在此类行业中，高信任水平带来的生产率提升比其他行业更为显著，而过于严格的雇佣和解雇法规对生产率的损害则更大。通过使用一个可获得管理质量数据的有限样本，我们表明，在无形资产密集型行业中，高信任对生产率的积极影响是通过受益于良好管理（组织资本的关键要素）的能力来传导的。据估计，在知识密集型环境中，相对较高的信任水平所带来的生产率提升是相当可观的，并且我们的估计结果通过了多项稳健性检验。
