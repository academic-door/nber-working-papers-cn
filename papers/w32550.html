Sleep: Educational Impact and Habit Formation

睡眠：教育影响与习惯养成

Osea Giuntella, Silvia Saccardo, Sally Sadoff

教育与劳动 美国

Abstract

There is growing evidence on the importance of sleep for productivity, but little is known about the impact of interventions targeting sleep. In a field experiment among U.S. university students, we show that incentives for sleep increase both sleep and academic performance. Motivated by theories of cue-based habit formation, our primary intervention couples personalized bedtime reminders with morning feedback and immediate rewards for sleeping at least seven hours on weeknights. The intervention increases the share of nights with at least seven hours of sleep by 26 percent and average weeknight sleep by an estimated 19 minutes during a four-week treatment period, with persistent effects of about eight minutes per night during a one to five-week post-treatment period. Comparisons to secondary treatments show that immediate incentives have larger impacts on sleep than delayed incentives or reminders and feedback alone during the treatment period, but do not have statistically distinguishable impacts on longer-term sleep habits in the post-treatment period. We estimate that immediate incentives im- prove average semester course performance by 0.075 - 0.089 grade points, a 0.10 - 0.11 standard deviation increase. Our results demonstrate that incentives to sleep can be a cost-effective tool for improving educational outcomes.

中文摘要

越来越多的证据表明睡眠对生产力至关重要，但针对睡眠的干预措施的影响却鲜为人知。在一项针对美国大学生的实地实验中，我们表明，为睡眠提供激励既能增加睡眠时间，也能提高学业成绩。受基于线索的习惯养成理论的启发，我们的主要干预措施将个性化的就寝提醒与早晨反馈以及在工作日夜晚至少睡七小时的即时奖励相结合。在为期四周的干预期内，该干预措施使至少睡七小时的夜晚比例提高了26%，并将平均工作日夜晚睡眠时间估计增加了19分钟；在干预期结束后的一至五周内，每晚仍有约八分钟的持续效应。与次要干预措施的比较表明，在干预期内，即时激励对睡眠的影响大于延迟激励或仅提供提醒和反馈，但在干预期后的长期睡眠习惯方面，这些干预措施没有统计学上可区分的差异。我们估计，即时激励能使学期平均课程成绩提高0.075至0.089个绩点，即增加0.10至0.11个标准差。我们的结果表明，睡眠激励可以成为改善教育成果的一种具有成本效益的工具。
