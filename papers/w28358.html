Living and Dying in America: An Essay on Deaths of Despair and the Future of Capitalism

生与死在美利坚：评绝望之死与资本主义的未来

Christopher J. Ruhm

环境与健康 美国

Abstract

This essay reviews <i>Deaths of Despair and the Future of Capitalism</i> (DEATHS), by Anne Case and Angus Deaton, a fascinating account of life and death in the United States during the late 20th and early 21st centuries. While primarily targeted towards a popular audience, the volume will be of interest to many economists and other social scientists. It postulates how public and private policies currently practiced in the United States, combined with and partly causing the declining economic and social circumstances of less educated, have led to increased mortality from drugs, suicide, and chronic liver disease. After describing the material in DEATHS in considerable detail, I suggest a variety of research questions that need to be answered to confirm or refute Case and Deaton’s arguments and describe challenges to their key hypotheses. Among the latter are the ability of the postulated relationships to explain the sharply differing mortality trajectories of non-Hispanic whites, compared with other groups, and the timing of the observed mortality changes. Along the way, I raise doubts about the usefulness of the “deaths of despair” conceptualization, with its strong implications about causality.

中文摘要

本文评述了安妮·凯斯和安格斯·迪顿所著的《绝望之死与资本主义的未来》（DEATHS），该书对20世纪末和21世纪初美国人的生与死进行了引人入胜的记述。虽然主要面向大众读者，但这本书也会引起许多经济学家和其他社会科学家的兴趣。该书假设，美国当前实行的公共和私人政策，加上并部分导致了受教育程度较低人群经济和社会状况的恶化，导致了因毒品、自杀和慢性肝病导致的死亡率上升。在详细描述了DEATHS一书的内容之后，我提出了一系列需要回答的研究问题，以证实或反驳凯斯和迪顿的论点，并描述了他们关键假设所面临的挑战。后者包括所假设的关系能否解释非西班牙裔白人与其他群体相比截然不同的死亡率轨迹，以及观察到的死亡率变化的时间点。在此过程中，我对“绝望之死”这一概念化框架的有用性提出了质疑，因为它对因果关系有强烈的暗示。
