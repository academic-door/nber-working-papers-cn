Unbalanced Financial Globalization

不平衡的金融全球化

Damien Capelle, Bruno Pellegrino

金融与资产定价 全球/多国

Abstract

We use a dynamic spatial general equilibrium model of international investment and production to investigate the real implications of the last five decades of financial globalization. We introduce a wedge accounting framework to estimate country- and time-varying measures of outward and inward Revealed Financial Openness (RFO). These wedges are meant to capture all impediments to cross-border investment, rather than explicit policy measures alone. We show how to identify these wedges for a large panel of countries using limited publicly available data on national accounts and external asset and liability positions since the 1970s. Our analysis reveals striking cross-country differences in the pace and direction of financial opening: wealthier countries have become relatively more open to foreign capital inflows, while poorer countries have become relatively more open to capital outflows, a phenomenon we call “Unbalanced Financial Globalization.” Counterfactual simulations show that this unbalanced financial globalization has worsened capital allocation, resulting in a 5.9% decrease inworld GDP, a 3.4% rise in cross-country income inequality, lower wages in poorer countries, and a decline in rates of return on capital in richer countries. In contrast, if financial integration had been uniform, the improved allocation of capital would have reduced income inequality, and increased global GDP. These findings underscore the crucial role of spatial heterogeneity in shaping the real impact of international capital markets integration.

中文摘要

我们使用一个国际投资与生产的动态空间一般均衡模型，来研究过去五十年金融全球化的实际影响。我们引入了一个楔子核算框架，以估计各国随时间变化的对外和对内“显性金融开放度”指标。这些楔子旨在捕捉跨境投资的所有障碍，而不仅仅是明确的政策措施。我们展示了如何利用自1970年代以来有限的公开国民账户和外部资产与负债头寸数据，为大量国家识别这些楔子。我们的分析揭示了各国在金融开放速度和方向上的显著差异：较富裕国家对外资流入相对更加开放，而较贫穷国家则对资本流出相对更加开放，我们将这一现象称为“不平衡的金融全球化”。反事实模拟表明，这种不平衡的金融全球化恶化了资本配置，导致全球GDP下降5.9%，跨国收入不平等加剧3.4%，较贫穷国家工资降低，以及较富裕国家资本回报率下降。相比之下，如果金融一体化是均匀的，资本配置的改善将减少收入不平等并增加全球GDP。这些发现强调了空间异质性在塑造国际资本市场一体化实际影响中的关键作用。
