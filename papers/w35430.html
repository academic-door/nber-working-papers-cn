Retrieval Failures and Consumption Smoothing: A Field Experiment on Seasonal Poverty

检索失败与消费平滑：关于季节性贫困的实地实验

Ned Augenblick, B. Kelsey Jack, Supreet Kaur, Felix Masiye, Nicholas Swanson

环境与健康 未指明区域

Abstract

Individuals may fail to recall and use information they already know when making decisions. We empirically investigate whether such “retrieval failures” distort consumption smoothing behavior among Zambian farmers, who derive their income from one annual harvest and then spend it down over the course of the year. We document that individuals underestimate upcoming spending by 50%, creating scope for under-saving. In order to improve recall, we randomize an intervention that prompts individuals to think through their future expenses associatively in categories—without providing any external information or guidance. Treated individuals increase “remembered” expenses by 36-60%; as predicted by the memory literature, effects are concentrated among small, irregular, and stochastic items. Immediate spending drops and, six weeks after the intervention, treated households hold 15% higher savings. They subsequently enter the hungry season—the final months of the year when consumption typically declines sharply—with one additional month of savings, leading to a flatter spending profile over the year. Households use the increased savings to self-finance additional farm investment, resulting in a 9% increase in the next year’s crop revenue. We replicate the intervention’s impact on beliefs among low-income Americans, suggesting that retrieval failures generalize across settings and populations.

中文摘要

个体在决策时可能无法回忆和使用已知信息。我们通过实证研究这种“检索失败”是否扭曲了赞比亚农民的消费平滑行为——这些农民的收入来自每年一次收获，并在全年逐步消耗。我们记录到，个体低估未来支出达50%，这为储蓄不足创造了空间。为改善回忆，我们随机实施了一项干预措施，促使个体按类别联想性地思考未来支出——不提供任何外部信息或指导。受干预个体的“回忆”支出增加了36-60%；正如记忆文献所预测，效应集中于小额、非定期和随机项目。即时支出下降，干预六周后，受干预家庭持有高出15%的储蓄。随后他们进入饥饿季节（一年中最后几个月，消费通常急剧下降），此时多出一个月的储蓄，导致全年支出曲线更平坦。家庭利用增加的储蓄自我融资进行额外农业投资，使次年作物收入增加9%。我们在低收入美国人中复制了该干预对信念的影响，表明检索失败在不同环境和人群中具有普遍性。
