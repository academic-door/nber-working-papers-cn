E-Cigarette Taxation and Queer Youth

电子烟税收与性少数青少年

Anthony Chuo, Chad D. Cotti, Charles J. Courtemanche, Johanna Catherine Maclean, Erik T. Nesson, Joseph J. Sabia

公共财政与税收 未指明区域

Abstract

Electronic nicotine delivery systems (ENDS) use among lesbian, gay, bisexual, and questioning (LGBQ) teenagers is over 30 percent higher than among their heterosexual counterparts. Yet little is known about how recent efforts to curb nicotine vaping through ENDS taxes impact sexual minorities. This study explores this question using data from the 2015-2023 State Youth Behavior Surveys and a generalized difference-in-differences identification strategy. We find that a one-dollar (in 2023$) per mL of e-liquid increase in ENDS taxes reduces the likelihood of any prior-month ENDS use among heterosexual teens by about three percentage points and habitual vaping (as measured by frequent and everyday use) by about one percentage point. In sharp contrast, we find no evidence that ENDS taxes reduce any of the vaping measures for queer youths. The differences in the effects between heterosexual and LGBQ youth are statistically significant in most specifications. We also find that ENDS minimum legal sales ages affect heterosexual but not LGBQ teens, while flavor bans influence heterosexual teens more quickly. The relatively weak effects of ENDS policies on queer youth appear to be driven by those who suffer from persistent sadness, bullying, or suicidal ideation. Overall, our results are consistent with LGBQ youths exhibiting dependence on e-cigarettes to cope with unique stress-related psychological challenges.

中文摘要

女同性恋、男同性恋、双性恋和质疑性取向（LGBQ）青少年使用电子尼古丁传送系统（ENDS）的比例比其异性恋同龄人高出30%以上。然而，关于近期通过ENDS税收遏制尼古丁电子烟的努力如何影响性少数群体的研究甚少。本研究利用2015-2023年州青少年行为调查数据以及广义双重差分识别策略探讨了这一问题。我们发现，每毫升烟油增加1美元（按2023年美元计）的ENDS税，会使异性恋青少年过去一个月内使用ENDS的可能性降低约三个百分点，并使习惯性电子烟使用（以频繁和日常使用衡量）降低约一个百分点。与此形成鲜明对比的是，我们没有发现证据表明ENDS税能减少性少数青少年的任何电子烟使用指标。在大多数设定中，异性恋与LGBQ青少年之间的效应差异具有统计显著性。我们还发现，ENDS最低法定销售年龄影响异性恋青少年，但不影响LGBQ青少年，而风味禁令对异性恋青少年的影响更为迅速。ENDS政策对性少数青少年相对较弱的效果似乎是由那些遭受持续悲伤、欺凌或自杀意念的青少年所驱动。总体而言，我们的结果与LGBQ青少年依赖电子烟来应对与压力相关的独特心理挑战这一观点一致。
