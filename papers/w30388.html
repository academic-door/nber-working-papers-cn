Does Occupational Licensing Reduce Value Creation on Digital Platforms?

职业许可会减少数字平台的价值创造吗？

Peter Q. Blair, Mischa Fisher

AI 与数字经济 未指明区域

Abstract

We test whether occupational licensing undercuts a key goal of digital marketplaces— to increase social surplus by increasing the effectiveness of customer search. Our setting is a large online marketplace in the $\$500$B home services industry where a platform converts customer search into sales leads that are accepted for purchase by service providers on the platform. For each of the 21 million observations in our data set, we observe task-level variation in the state licensing requirements that service providers must meet to operate on the platform. Exploiting two natural experiments, we find that licensing reduces the accept rate of sales leads by an average of 25 percent. The accept rate drops because licensing reduces the aggregate labor supply of workers on the platform and not because licensing increases the volume of customer search. We develop a model and derive analytic expressions for the impact of licensing on the welfare of consumers, service providers and the platform as a function of seven sufficient statistics which we estimate from the data. We find that licensing a task reduces service provider surplus and platform surplus without increasing consumer surplus.

中文摘要

我们检验职业许可是否削弱了数字市场的一个关键目标——通过提高客户搜索的有效性来增加社会剩余。我们的背景是一个价值5000亿美元的家居服务行业的大型在线市场，在该市场中，平台将客户搜索转化为销售线索，由平台上的服务提供商接受购买。在我们的数据集的2100万个观测值中，每个观测值都观察到服务提供商在平台上运营必须满足的州许可要求的任务层面差异。利用两个自然实验，我们发现许可使销售线索的接受率平均降低了25%。接受率下降是因为许可减少了平台上工人的总劳动力供给，而不是因为许可增加了客户搜索量。我们开发了一个模型，并推导出许可对消费者、服务提供商和平台福利影响的解析表达式，该表达式是七个充分统计量的函数，我们从数据中估计了这些统计量。我们发现，许可一项任务会减少服务提供商剩余和平台剩余，而不会增加消费者剩余。
