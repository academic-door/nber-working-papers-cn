Tariffs and the Term Structure of Inflation Expectations

关税与通胀预期的期限结构

Stéphane Auray, Michael B. Devereux, Anthony M. Diercks, Aurélien Eyquem, Joon Kim

宏观与货币财政 未指明区域

Abstract

Inflation expectations derived from financial markets exhibited unprecedented dynamics in 2025: the correlation between one-year inflation swaps and one-year-ahead one-year forward rates turned significantly negative for the first time on record. We show that this decoupling occurred primarily on days when tariff news dominated market pricing, using a two-stage event classification validated by Bloomberg news trends. Standard small open-economy New Keynesian models in which tariffs generate a one-time price-level increase imply positive comovement across horizons and cannot explain this pattern. \nWe explain these occurrences through the lens of an amended small open-economy New Keynesian model. Three ingredients prove critical for reproducing the observed negative conditional correlation between spot and forward inflation after tariff shocks: targeting year-on-year inflation, substantial interest-rate inertia, and persistent tariffs. Under empirically plausible calibrations, the model generates a negative correlation conditional on tariff shocks while preserving a positive unconditional correlation, suggesting that the 2025 twist in the term structure reflects expectations of a persistent policy response to trade shocks.

中文摘要

从金融市场得出的通胀预期在2025年表现出前所未有的动态：一年期通胀互换与一年后的一年期远期利率之间的相关性首次出现显著负值。我们利用经彭博新闻趋势验证的两阶段事件分类法，表明这种脱钩主要发生在关税新闻主导市场定价的日子里。标准的、关税导致一次性价格水平上升的小型开放经济体新凯恩斯主义模型意味着各期限之间存在正联动，无法解释这一模式。我们通过一个修正的小型开放经济体新凯恩斯主义模型的视角来解释这些现象。对于在关税冲击后重现观察到的即期与远期通胀之间的负条件相关性，三个要素至关重要：以同比通胀率为目标、显著的利率惯性以及持续的关税。在经验上合理的校准下，该模型在关税冲击条件下产生负相关性，同时保持正的无条件相关性，这表明2025年期限结构的扭曲反映了市场预期政策将对贸易冲击做出持续性反应。
