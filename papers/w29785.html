The Unemployment-Inflation Trade-off Revisited: The Phillips Curve in COVID Times

失业与通胀权衡再审视：新冠疫情时期的菲利普斯曲线

Richard K. Crump, Stefano Eusepi, Marc Giannoni, Ayşegül Şahin

宏观与货币财政 未指明区域

Abstract

Using a New Keynesian Phillips curve, we document the rapid and persistent increase in the natural rate of unemployment, u*ₜ, in the aftermath of the pandemic and characterize its implications for inflation dynamics. While the bulk of the inflation surge is attributed to temporary supply factors, we also find an important role for current and expected negative unemployment gaps. Through the lens of the model, the 2022–2023 disinflation was driven by the expectation that the unemployment gap will close through a progressive decline in u*ₜ and a rise in the unemployment rate. This implies that convergence to long-run price stability depends critically on expectations about labor market tightness. Using a variety of cross-sectional data sources we provide corroborating evidence of unusually tight labor market conditions, consistent with our estimated rise in u*ₜ.

中文摘要

利用新凯恩斯菲利普斯曲线，我们记录了疫情后自然失业率u*ₜ的快速且持续上升，并刻画了其对通胀动态的影响。虽然通胀飙升主要归因于暂时性供给因素，但我们发现当前和预期的负失业缺口也发挥了重要作用。根据模型视角，2022-2023年的通胀下降是由以下预期驱动的：失业缺口将通过u*ₜ的逐步下降和失业率的上升而闭合。这意味着向长期价格稳定的收敛关键取决于对劳动力市场紧张程度的预期。利用多种横截面数据来源，我们提供了劳动力市场异常紧张的佐证证据，这与我们估计的u*ₜ上升相一致。
