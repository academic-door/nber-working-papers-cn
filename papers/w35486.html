Learning and the Efficiency of Expert Referrals

学习与专家转诊的效率

Ian McCarthy, Seth Richards-Shubik

环境与健康 未指明区域

Abstract

Buyers of complex goods and services often rely on the advice of expert intermediaries who are themselves imperfectly informed about the quality of the available options. How well do these intermediaries learn about that quality and act on it? We study this question in the setting of referrals from primary care physicians (PCPs) to specialists, using data on 4.5 million joint replacement surgeries for Medicare beneficiaries. We first document substantial heterogeneity in specialist quality and costs within geographic markets, and we present design-based evidence showing that PCPs adjust their referrals specifically based on the outcomes of their own patients. We then employ a structural learning model of PCP referral choices to quantify the losses from informational frictions and to simulate possible reallocations with improved information. Beyond learning, the model also accounts for limitations on possible reallocations due to habit persistence and capacity constraints. We find that about one-quarter of patients would be referred to a different specialist in the absence of informational frictions, with small but meaningful improvements in patient outcomes.

中文摘要

复杂商品和服务的购买者通常依赖专家中介的建议，而这些中介自身对可用选项的质量了解并不完全。这些中介在多大程度上能够学习并据此采取行动？我们利用医疗保险受益人的450万例关节置换手术数据，在初级保健医生 (PCP) 向专科医生转诊的背景下研究了这一问题。我们首先记录了地理市场内专科医生质量和成本的显著异质性，并提供了基于设计的证据，表明PCP会根据其自身患者的治疗结果来调整转诊。然后，我们采用PCP转诊选择的结构性学习模型来量化信息摩擦造成的损失，并模拟在信息改善情况下可能的资源重新配置。除了学习因素外，该模型还考虑了因习惯持续性和产能限制而对可能重新配置施加的限制。我们发现，在没有信息摩擦的情况下，大约四分之一的患者会被转诊给不同的专科医生，患者治疗结果会有微小但有意义的改善。
