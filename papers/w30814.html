Disparities in Pollution Capitalization Rates: The Role of Direct & Systemic Discrimination

污染资本化率的差异：直接歧视与系统性歧视的作用

Joshua S. Graff Zivin, Gregor Singer

环境与健康 未指明区域

Abstract

We examine how exogenous changes in exposure to air pollution over the past two decades have altered the disparities in home values between Black and White homeowners. We find that air quality capitalization rates are significantly lower for Black homeowners. In fact, they are so much lower that, despite secular reductions in the Black-White pollution exposure gap, disparities in housing values have increased during this period. An exploration of mechanisms suggests that roughly 60% of this difference is the result of direct discrimination while the remaining 40% can be attributed to systemic discrimination.

中文摘要

我们研究了过去二十年空气污染暴露的外生变化如何改变了黑人和白人房主之间房屋价值的差异。我们发现，黑人房主的空气质量资本化率显著较低。事实上，这一差异如此之大，以至于尽管黑人与白人之间的污染暴露差距在长期内有所缩小，但住房价值的差距在此期间反而扩大了。对机制的研究表明，这种差异中约60%源于直接歧视，其余40%可归因于系统性歧视。
