Air Pollution, Wildfire Smoke, and Worker Health

空气污染、野火烟雾与工人健康

Marika Cabral, Marcus Dillender

环境与健康 未指明区域

Abstract

Despite growing concerns about the impact of pollution on workers, little is known about how pollution impacts worker health and workplace safety. This paper leverages high-frequency, plausibly exogenous variation in wildfire smoke to estimate the impact of pollution on workplace injuries. Our analysis draws on unique data we construct through linking information on smoke plumes and pollution to comprehensive administrative data on workers’ compensation injury claims from Texas. We first document that wildfire smoke increases ambient air pollution—with our estimates indicating that a day of smoke coverage is associated with an average increase in PM2.5 of 18.6%.We find that an additional day of smoke coverage leads to a 2.8% increase in workplace injury claims. Similar percent increases in workplace injuries are found across different types of injuries and workers. However, because of large variation in baseline injury risk, the incidence of these pollution-induced injuries is concentrated among workers in high-risk occupations, and supplemental analysis illustrates potential opportunities for improving the targeting of costly mitigation. Our estimates indicate that pollution—and wildfire smoke in particular—substantially harms worker health, even at pollution levels well below current and proposed regulatory standards. Moreover, the implied costs associated with increased workplace injuries are in the same range as prior estimates of the costs of pollution from reduced worker productivity or increased health care utilization among children or the elderly. Overall, our findings suggest workers face unique risks from pollution and provide insights for policy aiming to address these risks.

中文摘要

尽管人们越来越关注污染对工人的影响，但关于污染如何影响工人健康和工作场所安全，我们知之甚少。本文利用高频、看似外生的野火烟雾变化来估计污染对工伤的影响。我们的分析基于独特的数据集，该数据集通过将烟雾羽流和污染信息与来自德克萨斯州的工人赔偿工伤索赔的全面行政数据相关联而构建。我们首先证明，野火烟雾增加了环境空气污染——我们的估计表明，一天的烟雾覆盖与PM2.5平均增加18.6%相关。我们发现，额外一天的烟雾覆盖导致工伤索赔增加2.8%。在不同类型的伤害和工人中，工伤的百分比增长相似。然而，由于基线工伤风险存在巨大差异，这些污染引发的伤害主要集中在高风险职业的工人中，补充分析说明了改善昂贵缓解措施针对性的潜在机会。我们的估计表明，污染——尤其是野火烟雾——会严重损害工人健康，即使在远低于当前和拟议监管标准的污染水平下也是如此。此外，与工伤增加相关的隐含成本，与先前关于污染导致工人生产率下降或儿童或老年人医疗保健利用增加的成本估计范围相同。总体而言，我们的研究结果表明，工人面临独特的污染风险，并为旨在应对这些风险的政策提供了见解。
