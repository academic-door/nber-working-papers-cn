Estimating the Impact of the Age of Criminal Majority: Decomposing Multiple Treatments in a Regression Discontinuity Framework

估算刑事责任年龄的影响：在断点回归框架中分解多重处理

Michael G. Mueller-Smith, Benjamin Pyle, Caroline Walker

公共财政与税收 未指明区域

Abstract

This paper studies the impact of adult prosecution on recidivism and employment trajectories for adolescent, first-time felony defendants. We use extensive linked Criminal Justice Admin- istrative Record System and socio-economic data from Wayne County, Michigan (Detroit). Using the discrete age of majority rule and a regression discontinuity design, we find that adult prosecution reduces future criminal charges over 5 years by 0.48 felony cases (↓ 20%) while also worsening labor market outcomes: 0.76 fewer employers (↓ 19%) and $\$674$ fewer earnings (↓ 21%) per year. We develop a novel econometric framework that combines standard regression discontinuity methods with predictive machine learning models to identify mechanism-specific treatment effects that underpin the overall impact of adult prosecution. We leverage these estimates to consider four policy counterfactuals: (1) raising the age of majority, (2) increasing adult dismissals to match the juvenile disposition rates, (3) eliminating adult incarceration, and (4) expanding juvenile record sealing opportunities to teenage adult defendants. All four scenarios generate positive returns for government budgets. When accounting for impacts to defendants as well as victim costs borne by society stemming from increases in recidivism, we find positive social returns for juvenile record sealing expansions and dismissing marginal adult charges; raising the age of majority breaks even. Eliminating prison for first-time adult felony defendants, however, increases net social costs. Policymakers may still find this attractive if they are willing to value beneficiaries (taxpayers and defendants) slightly higher (124%) than potential victims.

中文摘要

本文研究了成人起诉对青少年初犯重罪被告人的累犯和就业轨迹的影响。我们使用了来自密歇根州韦恩县（底特律）的广泛关联的刑事司法行政记录系统和社会经济数据。利用离散的成年年龄规则和断点回归设计，我们发现成人起诉使未来5年内的刑事指控减少了0.48起重罪案件（下降20%），同时恶化了劳动力市场结果：每年雇主数量减少0.76个（下降19%），收入减少674美元（下降21%）。我们开发了一个新颖的计量经济学框架，将标准断点回归方法与预测性机器学习模型相结合，以识别构成成人起诉总体影响基础的机制特定处理效应。我们利用这些估计来考虑四种政策反事实情景：（1）提高成年年龄，（2）增加成人案件驳回率以匹配青少年处置率，（3）消除成人监禁，以及（4）将青少年记录封存机会扩大到青少年成年被告人。所有四种情景都为政府预算带来了正回报。当考虑到对被告人的影响以及因累犯增加而由社会承担的受害者成本时，我们发现扩大青少年记录封存和驳回边缘成人指控能带来正社会回报；提高成年年龄则收支平衡。然而，消除初犯成人重罪被告人的监禁会增加社会净成本。如果政策制定者愿意对受益者（纳税人和被告人）的估值略高于潜在受害者（124%），他们可能仍会觉得这一方案有吸引力。
