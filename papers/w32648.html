Superstars or Supervillains? Large Firms in the South Korean Growth Miracle

超级明星还是超级恶棍？韩国增长奇迹中的大型企业

Jaedo Choi, Andrei A. Levchenko, Dimitrije Ruzic, Younghun Shim

宏观与货币财政 未指明区域

Abstract

We quantify the contribution of the largest firms to South Korea’s economic performance over the period 1972-2011. Using firm-level historical data, we document a novel fact: firm concentration rose substantially during the growth miracle period. To understand whether rising concentration contributed positively or negatively to South Korean real income, we build a quantitative dynamic heterogeneous firm small open economy model. Our framework accommodates a variety of potential causes and consequences of changing firm concentration: productivity, distortions, selection into exporting, scale economies, and oligopolistic and oligopsonistic market power in domestic goods and labor markets. The model is implemented directly on the firm-level data and inverted to recover the drivers of concentration. We find that most of the differential performance of the top firms is attributable to higher productivity growth rather than increasingly favorable distortions. Exceptional performance of the top 3 firms within each sector relative to the average firms contributed 20.8% to the 2011 real GDP and 6.6% to the net present value of welfare over the period 1972-2011. Thus, the largest Korean firms were superstars rather than supervillains.

中文摘要

我们量化了1972-2011年期间韩国最大企业对经济表现的贡献。利用企业层面的历史数据，我们记录了一个新事实：在增长奇迹期间，企业集中度大幅上升。为了理解集中度上升对韩国实际收入是产生了积极还是消极影响，我们构建了一个定量动态异质性企业小型开放经济模型。我们的框架涵盖了企业集中度变化的各种潜在原因和后果：生产率、扭曲、出口选择、规模经济，以及国内商品和劳动力市场中的寡头和寡买方市场势力。该模型直接在企业层面数据上实施，并通过反推来恢复集中度的驱动因素。我们发现，顶级企业的差异化表现大多归因于更高的生产率增长，而非日益有利的扭曲。每个行业内前3大企业相对于平均企业的卓越表现，对2011年实际GDP的贡献为20.8%，对1972-2011年期间福利净现值的贡献为6.6%。因此，韩国最大的企业是超级明星而非超级恶棍。
