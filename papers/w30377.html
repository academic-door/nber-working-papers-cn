The German Model of Industrial Relations: Balancing Flexibility and Collective Action

德国劳资关系模式：平衡灵活性与集体行动

Simon Jäger, Shakked Noy, Benjamin Schoefer

理论与方法 美国、德国

Abstract

We give an overview of the “German model” of industrial relations. We organize our review by focusing on the two pillars of the model: sectoral collective bargaining and firm-level codetermination. Relative to the United States, Germany outsources collective bargaining to the sectoral level, resulting in higher coverage and the avoidance of firm-level distributional conflict. Relative to other European countries, Germany makes it easy for employers to avoid coverage or use flexibility provisions to deviate downwards from collective agreements. The greater flexibility of the German system may reduce unemployment, but may also erode bargaining coverage and increase inequality. Meanwhile, firm-level codetermination through worker board representation and works councils creates cooperative dialogue between employers and workers. Board representation has few direct impacts owing to worker representatives’ minority vote share, but works councils, which hold a range of substantive powers, may be more impactful. Overall, the German model highlights tensions between efficiency-enhancing flexibility and equity-enhancing collective action.\n

中文摘要

我们概述了劳资关系的“德国模式”。我们通过聚焦该模式的两大支柱来组织我们的回顾：行业集体谈判和企业层面的共同决策。相对于美国，德国将集体谈判外包给行业层面，从而提高了覆盖率并避免了企业层面的分配冲突。相对于其他欧洲国家，德国使雇主很容易避免覆盖或利用灵活性条款向下偏离集体协议。德国体系更大的灵活性可能降低失业率，但也可能侵蚀谈判覆盖率并加剧不平等。与此同时，通过工人董事会代表和劳资委员会实现的企业层面共同决策，在雇主和工人之间创造了合作对话。由于工人代表的少数投票权，董事会代表的直接影响很小，但拥有实质性权力的劳资委员会可能更具影响力。总体而言，德国模式凸显了提高效率的灵活性与增强公平的集体行动之间的张力。
