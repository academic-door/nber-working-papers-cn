From Heterogeneous Firms to Heterogeneous Trade Elasticities: The Aggregate Implications of Firm Export Decisions

从异质性企业到异质性贸易弹性：企业出口决策的总体影响

Rodrigo Adão, Costas Arkolakis, Sharat Ganapati

国际贸易 全球/多国

Abstract

We study the consequences of globalization in monopolistic competition models where heterogeneous firms select into export markets. We summarize firm heterogeneity at the extensive and intensive margins of firm exports with two nonparametric elasticity functions that depend only on the share of active firms in a market. Given changes in trade costs, these elasticity functions are sufficient to compute the model’s counterfactual predictions, and their shape generates heterogeneity in welfare responses across countries. We estimate these functions using the model’s semiparametric gravity equations for firm export margins, which yield trade elasticity estimates that vary with the number of exporters in a market and the country’s level of development. Compared to constant-elasticity gravity models, our estimates imply gains from trade that are larger in developed countries but smaller in developing countries, with differences arising mainly due to the entry and selection of heterogeneous firms.

中文摘要

我们研究垄断竞争模型中异质性企业选择进入出口市场时的全球化后果。我们通过两个非参数弹性函数总结企业出口的广延边际和集约边际的异质性，这些函数仅取决于市场中活跃企业的份额。给定贸易成本变化，这些弹性函数足以计算模型的反事实预测，其形状导致各国福利响应的异质性。我们利用模型对企业出口边际的半参数引力方程估计这些函数，得到的贸易弹性估计值随市场中出口商数量和一国发展水平而变化。与恒定弹性引力模型相比，我们的估计意味着发达国家的贸易收益更大，而发展中国家的贸易收益更小，差异主要源于异质性企业的进入和选择。
