Charitable Giving Responses to Education Budgets

对教育预算的慈善捐赠反应

Jonathan Meer, Hedieh Tajali

教育与劳动 全球/多国

Abstract

Do changes in government spending affect voluntary contributions to those recipients? We examine how changes in K-12 education budgets impact donations to teachers using data from DonorsChoose.org, an online crowdfunding platform for public school teachers to raise money. We find a positive correlation between budgets and voluntary contributions when not accounting for their endogeneous relationship. With instrumental variables, we find evidence for crowd-out of private giving, though the magnitudes are small relative to spending and do not meaningfully offset budget changes. These results are driven entirely by teachers’ posting of requests.

中文摘要

政府支出的变化是否会影响对这些受助者的自愿捐款？我们利用DonorsChoose.org（一个为公立学校教师筹集资金的在线众筹平台）的数据，研究了K-12教育预算变化如何影响对教师的捐赠。我们发现，在不考虑其内生关系时，预算与自愿捐款之间存在正相关。使用工具变量，我们找到了私人捐赠被挤出的证据，尽管其规模相对于支出而言较小，且并未显著抵消预算变化。这些结果完全由教师发布请求所驱动。
