Intergovernmental Grants to School Districts and Educational Outcomes During the COVID-19 Pandemic

新冠疫情大流行期间对学区的政府间拨款与教育成果

Jeffrey Clemens, Philip G. Hoxie, Stan Veuger

教育与劳动 未指明区域

Abstract

The federal government appropriated $189.5 billion in pandemic aid to school districts through the Elementary and Secondary School Emergency Relief (ESSER) fund. We evaluate their impact on schools with district poverty shares near 5%, where a threshold-driven increase in funds enables us to implement a difference-in-discontinuities design. Federal funds were passed on to residents through reductions in local revenue collections, including property taxes, and did not result in increased per-pupil expenditures by school districts. We find no evidence that additional funds mitigated the declines in test scores. We provide suggestive evidence that political engagement, especially from parents, increased in districts that qualified for additional funds. This combination of engagement and reduced taxation may explain the greater enrollment and faster reopening we observe in such districts.

中文摘要

联邦政府通过中小学紧急救济基金向学区拨款1895亿美元。我们评估了这些拨款对学区贫困份额接近5%的学校的影响，其中基于阈值的资金增加使我们能够实施断点回归差分设计。联邦资金通过减少地方税收收入（包括财产税）转嫁给居民，并未导致学区生均支出增加。我们没有发现额外资金缓解考试成绩下降的证据。我们提供暗示性证据表明，在获得额外资金的学区中，政治参与（尤其是家长参与）有所增加。这种参与与减税的结合可能解释了我们在这些学区观察到的更高入学率和更快重新开放。
