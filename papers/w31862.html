Are Swedish House Prices Too High? Why the Price-to-Income Ratio Is a Misleading Indicator

瑞典房价过高吗？为什么房价收入比是一个误导性指标

Lars E.O. Svensson

其他主题 全球/多国

Abstract

Appropriate indicators of housing valuation are important for macroprudential policy and assessments of risks to financial stability. Overvalued housing may result in a correction and a fall in house prices, which if large could threaten financial and macroeconomic stability. \nAccording to ECB (2023) and European Systemic Risk Board (2022), Swedish owner-occupied housing (OOH) was overvalued by about 55% in 2021q2, the largest overvaluation in the EU and EEA; according to European Commission (2023c), by about 30% in 2022. These assessments affect warnings and recommendations issued for Swedish economic policy and the shocks in EBA stress tests of Swedish banks. \nBut these assessments are due to the use of misleading indicators: the deviations of price-to-income (PTI) and price-to-rent ratios from their historical averages. They disregard mortgage rates and other housing costs and lack scientific support. According to a large housing literature, it is not the purchase price but the user cost that is the appropriate measure of the cost of living in OOH, the cost of the housing services that the OOH delivers. From this point of view, “Are house prices too high?” is the wrong question. The right question is, “Are user costs too high?” \nNew improved estimates of an upper bound of the user cost are constructed, including an adjustment for a preference shift during the coronavirus crisis in favor of larger and better housing. The upper bound results from a conservative lower bound on house-price expectations below the few existing data points—regular time-series expectations data are not available. \nAccording to the user-cost-to-income (UCTI) ratio, Swedish owner-occupied houses have since 2010 instead become increasingly undervalued (not overvalued), by about 30% in 2019q4. Due to higher mortgage rates, they are less undervalued in 2025q1, but still about 25%. \nFor Sweden, the UCTI and PTI indicators are in fact strongly negatively correlated, with opposite signs. If the UCTI indicator is the right one, the PTI indicator is consistently wrong. \nThe valuation assessments of the ECB, the ESRB, the Commission, the OECD, and the IMF are scrutinized and compared. The problem of misleading indicators and overvaluation assessments—and resulting distorted warnings and recommendations—is not restricted to Sweden but concerns several other countries in the European Union.

中文摘要

适当的住房估值指标对于宏观审慎政策和金融稳定风险评估至关重要。估值过高的住房可能导致价格回调，如果幅度过大，可能威胁金融和宏观经济稳定。根据欧洲央行（2023）和欧洲系统性风险委员会（2022）的数据，2021年第二季度瑞典自住住房估值过高约55%，为欧盟和欧洲经济区最高；根据欧盟委员会（2023c）的数据，2022年估值过高约30%。这些评估影响了针对瑞典经济政策发出的警告和建议，以及欧洲银行管理局对瑞典银行压力测试中的冲击。但这些评估是由于使用了误导性指标：房价收入比和房价租金比与其历史均值的偏离。它们忽略了抵押贷款利率和其他住房成本，缺乏科学依据。根据大量住房文献，衡量自住住房居住成本（即自住住房提供的住房服务成本）的适当指标不是购买价格，而是使用者成本。从这个角度看，“房价过高吗？”是一个错误的问题。正确的问题是，“使用者成本过高吗？”我们构建了使用者成本上限的新改进估计，包括对新冠疫情期间偏好转向更大更好住房的调整。该上限源于对房价预期的保守下限估计，该下限低于现有的少数数据点——常规的时间序列预期数据不可得。根据使用者成本收入比，自2010年以来，瑞典自住住房反而变得越来越被低估（而非高估），2019年第四季度被低估约30%。由于抵押贷款利率上升，2025年第一季度被低估程度有所减轻，但仍约为25%。对于瑞典而言，使用者成本收入比和房价收入比指标实际上呈强烈负相关，符号相反。如果使用者成本收入比指标是正确的，那么房价收入比指标就一直是错误的。我们对欧洲央行、欧洲系统性风险委员会、欧盟委员会、经合组织和国际货币基金组织的估值评估进行了详细审查和比较。误导性指标和高估评估的问题——以及由此产生的扭曲警告和建议——并不仅限于瑞典，还涉及欧盟其他几个国家。
