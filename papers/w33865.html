Job Sorting and the Labor Market Effects of a Criminal Record

工作分类与犯罪记录的劳动力市场效应

Randi Hjalmarsson, Matthew J. Lindquist, Louis-Pierre Lepage, Conrad Miller

教育与劳动 未指明区域

Abstract

We examine the effects of a criminal record on labor market outcomes and the mediating role of job sorting using Swedish register data. Prime-age adults with criminal records earn about 30% less than observably similar adults without records and are concentrated in specific employers and occupations. To estimate the causal effect of a criminal record, we use an event study design that compares outcomes for adults charged with an offense for the first time to matched adults who were suspected of a similar offense but not charged. Acquiring a criminal record reduces months employed by 2% and annual earnings by 5%. These negative effects are: twice as large for more serious or subsequent charges, not driven by job displacement or incapacitation, and not mitigated by automatic record expungement, which typically occurs 5 or 10 years after case disposition. We classify firms by their propensity to hire workers with criminal records, holding suspected offense history fixed. A criminal record reduces employment at firms classified as less likely to hire workers with criminal records, increases employment at other firms, and decreases monthly earnings across all firm types. Firm propensity to hire workers with criminal records varies substantially—even within industries—and is linked to firm size and managers’ prior exposure to people with records. Leveraging manager moves across small firms, we find that when a firm hires a new manager with greater prior exposure to people with criminal records, it hires more people with records, with no detectable effect on productivity.

中文摘要

我们利用瑞典登记数据，研究了犯罪记录对劳动力市场结果的影响以及工作分类的中介作用。有犯罪记录的壮年成年人比观察特征相似的无记录成年人收入低约30%，并且集中在特定的雇主和职业中。为了估计犯罪记录的因果效应，我们采用事件研究设计，将首次被指控犯罪的成年人的结果与匹配的因类似罪行被怀疑但未被指控的成年人进行比较。获得犯罪记录使就业月数减少2%，年收入减少5%。这些负面效应：对于更严重或后续指控，其影响加倍；并非由工作置换或失能效应驱动；也不会因通常在案件处理后5年或10年发生的自动记录消除而减轻。我们根据企业雇佣有犯罪记录工人的倾向对其进行分类，同时固定被怀疑的犯罪历史。犯罪记录减少了在被归类为不太可能雇佣有犯罪记录工人的企业中的就业，增加了在其他企业中的就业，并降低了所有类型企业的月收入。企业雇佣有犯罪记录工人的倾向差异很大——即使在行业内部——并且与企业规模以及管理者之前接触有记录人员的经历有关。利用管理者在小型企业间的流动，我们发现，当一家企业雇佣了一位之前接触有犯罪记录人员更多的新管理者时，它会雇佣更多有记录的人，而对生产率没有可检测的影响。
