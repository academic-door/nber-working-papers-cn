In Sickness and in Health: Retirement Responses to Spousal Health Shocks

疾病与健康：对配偶健康冲击的退休反应

Hyeran Chung, Haena Lee, Sita Slavov

环境与健康 未指明区域

Abstract

Health shocks are a major driver of retirement. However, the existing literature on this topic tends not to consider the marital context in which retirements occur. Using the 1992-2022 Health and Retirement Study and staggered difference-in-differences methods, we investigate how spousal health shocks impact retirement transitions. We also investigate whether these effects differ by gender and health insurance status. We consider multiple definitions of health shocks, including self-reported health declines, additional diagnoses, hospitalizations, increases in out-of-pocket medical spending, and functional deterioration. Our results suggest that the probability of retirement increases by 1.2 to 1.3 percentage points (12 to 13 percent) after a spouse’s new health condition diagnosis or hospitalization. These retirements tend to occur earlier than anticipated. They also tend to be concentrated among women and respondents with employment-based health plans that include retiree coverage for their spouse.

中文摘要

健康冲击是退休的主要驱动因素之一。然而，关于这一主题的现有文献往往不考虑退休发生的婚姻背景。利用1992-2022年健康与退休研究以及交错双重差分方法，我们调查了配偶健康冲击如何影响退休转变。我们还研究了这些影响是否因性别和健康保险状况而异。我们考虑了健康冲击的多种定义，包括自评健康下降、新增诊断、住院、自付医疗支出增加以及功能恶化。我们的结果表明，在配偶新诊断出健康状况或住院后，退休概率增加了1.2至1.3个百分点（12%至13%）。这些退休往往比预期更早发生。它们也往往集中在女性和那些拥有包含配偶退休人员保险的就业型健康计划的受访者中。
