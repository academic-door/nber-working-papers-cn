Quantifying Climate Change Loss and Damage Consistent with a Social Cost of Greenhouse Gases

量化与温室气体社会成本一致的气候变化损失与损害

Marshall Burke, Mustafa Zahid, Noah Diffenbaugh, Solomon M. Hsiang

环境与健康 印度

Abstract

Climate change is generating demonstrable harm around the world. Political and legal efforts have sought to associate climate impacts with specific emissions, including in recent international policy discussion of Loss and Damage (L&D). However, no quantitative definition of L&D exists, nor does there exist a framework for linking specific emissions to specific damages. Here we develop such a framework, linking it explicitly to recent efforts to calculate the social cost of carbon dioxide (SC-CO2), and demonstrate its use in a variety of applications. We calculate that future damages from past emissions, one component of L&D, are at least an order of magnitude larger than historical damages from the same emissions, a more commonly discussed component of L&D: 1 ton of CO2 emitted in 1990 causes $\$4$ in global cumulative discounted damages by 2020 and an additional $\$327$ in discounted damages through 2100 (2% discount rate). These estimates of past and future damages from marginal emissions can be used to calculate L&D for a range of specific emitting activities: for instance, an individual taking one long-haul flight every year for the past decade will generate ~$\$5500$ in damages through 2100, the emissions associated with multiple oil majors between 1988-2015 have already caused $\$50$-200B of cumulative global economic damage by 2020, and CO2 emissions in the US since 1990 have caused ~$\$2$T in global damage through 2020, with India ($\$293$B) and Brazil ($\$167$B) being harmed the most. Carbon removal offers an alternative to transfer payments for settling L&D, but we show that it becomes increasingly ineffective in limiting damages as the delay between emission and recapture increases.

中文摘要

气候变化正在全球范围内造成明显的危害。政治和法律努力试图将气候影响与特定排放联系起来，包括在近期关于损失与损害的国际政策讨论中。然而，目前既不存在损失与损害的定量定义，也不存在将特定排放与特定损害联系起来的框架。在此，我们开发了这样一个框架，将其与近期计算二氧化碳社会成本的努力明确联系起来，并展示了其在多种应用中的用途。我们计算出，过去排放造成的未来损害（损失与损害的一个组成部分）至少比同一排放造成的已发生损害（损失与损害中更常被讨论的组成部分）大一个数量级：1990年排放的1吨二氧化碳到2020年造成全球累计贴现损害4美元，并通过2100年额外造成贴现损害327美元（贴现率为2%）。这些对边际排放造成的过去和未来损害的估计可用于计算一系列特定排放活动的损失与损害：例如，过去十年每年乘坐一次长途航班的个人将在2100年前产生约5500美元的损害；1988年至2015年间多家大型石油公司的相关排放到2020年已造成500亿至2000亿美元的全球累计经济损失；美国自1990年以来的二氧化碳排放到2020年已造成约2万亿美元的全球损害，其中印度（2930亿美元）和巴西（1670亿美元）受害最严重。碳移除为通过转移支付解决损失与损害提供了替代方案，但我们表明，随着排放与重新捕获之间的延迟增加，碳移除在限制损害方面的效果会越来越差。
