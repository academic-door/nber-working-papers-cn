Literature Review and Evidence Aggregation: a Toolkit for Applied Micro

文献综述与证据聚合：应用微观经济学工具箱

Peter Ganong, Avik Garg, Maximilian Kasy

理论与方法 未指明区域

Abstract

Consider an analyst interested in predicting the size of an effect. She has identified a set of prior published studies of similar effects. We provide a toolkit for (i) summarizing the prior literature, (ii) making predictions of effects in new contexts, and (iii) correcting for the bias from selectivity in the prior literature. We illustrate these methods with empirical examples from labor, public, behavioral, environmental, and development economics. Some of the tools are relevant even when only three prior studies are available. We show how it is possible to use covariates to transparently make predictions for a new context by reweighting prior estimates. The mean effect—after correcting for selectivity—is between 12% and 21% of the simple mean in our empirical examples. We conclude with a cookbook for practitioners producing meta-analyses.

中文摘要

假设一位分析者想要预测某个效应的大小。她已经找到了一组关于类似效应的已发表前期研究。我们提供了一个工具箱，用于：(i) 总结前期文献，(ii) 在新情境下进行效应预测，以及 (iii) 纠正前期文献中因选择性而产生的偏差。我们通过来自劳动经济学、公共经济学、行为经济学、环境经济学和发展经济学的实证例子来说明这些方法。即使只有三项前期研究可用，其中一些工具也是相关的。我们展示了如何通过重新加权前期估计值，利用协变量透明地为新情境做出预测。在我们的实证例子中，经过选择性偏差校正后的平均效应，介于简单平均值的12%到21%之间。最后，我们为进行元分析的研究者提供了一份操作指南。
