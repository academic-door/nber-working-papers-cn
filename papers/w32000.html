An IV Hazard Model of Loan Default with an Application to Subprime Mortgage Cohorts

贷款违约的IV风险模型：次级抵押贷款队列的应用

Christopher Palmer

理论与方法 未指明区域

Abstract

This paper develops a control-function methodology accounting for endogenous or mismeasured regressors in hazard models. I provide sufficient identifying assumptions and regularity conditions for the estimator to be consistent and asymptotically normal. Applying the estimator to the subprime mortgage crisis, I quantify what caused the foreclosure rate to triple across the 2003-2007 subprime cohorts. To identify the elasticity of default with respect to housing prices, I use various home-price instruments including historical variation in home-price cyclicality. Loose credit played a significant role in the crisis, but much of the increase in defaults across cohorts was caused by home-price declines unrelated to lending standards, with a 10% decline in home prices increasing subprime mortgage default rates by 50%.

中文摘要

本文开发了一种控制函数方法，用于处理风险模型中内生性或测量误差的回归变量。我提供了充分的识别假设和正则条件，使估计量具有一致性和渐近正态性。将该估计量应用于次贷危机，我量化了导致2003-2007年次级贷款队列止赎率翻三倍的原因。为了识别违约对房价的弹性，我使用了各种房价工具变量，包括房价周期性的历史变化。宽松信贷在危机中发挥了重要作用，但各队列违约率上升的大部分原因是与贷款标准无关的房价下跌，房价下跌10%会使次级抵押贷款违约率增加50%。
