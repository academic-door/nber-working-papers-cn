Do Technology Standards Induce Innovation in Environmental Technologies When Coordination is Important?

当协调至关重要时，技术标准会催生环境技术创新吗？

Myriam Gregoire-Zawilski, David Popp

环境与健康 未指明区域

Abstract

If further decarbonization of electricity systems is to continue, a next generation of innovation in transformative grid modernization and renewables integration technologies will be needed. Few studies have investigated the policy determinants of innovation in this sector to glean insights on how government may support the development and deployment of these technologies. We argue that policies that were successful at supporting the first wave of renewables innovation may not be sufficient to produce similar results in the next wave of green innovation since those face higher coordination bottlenecks. We investigate the effects of interoperability standards - an instrument that may facilitate coordination - on patenting using smart grid as an example of a technology that has high interoperability requirements. We find that standards decrease patenting at the extensive and intensive margins, but these results vary across types of firms. We find that this negative effect is driven by large firms, whereas standards increase entry by firms without prior smart grid innovation experience. We interpret this result as an information effect: standards provide useful information to new entrants and may help diversify the range of players innovating in this space.

中文摘要

如果电力系统要进一步脱碳，就需要下一代变革性的电网现代化和可再生能源整合技术的创新。很少有研究探讨该领域创新的政策决定因素，以洞察政府如何支持这些技术的开发和部署。我们认为，成功支持第一波可再生能源创新的政策，可能不足以在下一波绿色创新中产生类似效果，因为后者面临更高的协调瓶颈。我们以具有高互操作性要求的智能电网技术为例，研究了互操作性标准（一种可能促进协调的工具）对专利活动的影响。我们发现，标准在广延边际和集约边际上都减少了专利活动，但这些结果因企业类型而异。我们发现，这种负面效应是由大型企业驱动的，而标准则促进了此前没有智能电网创新经验的企业进入。我们将这一结果解读为信息效应：标准为新进入者提供了有用信息，并可能有助于使该领域的创新主体更加多元化。
