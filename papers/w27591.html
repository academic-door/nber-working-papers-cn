The Leveraging of Silicon Valley

硅谷的杠杆化

Jesse Davis, Adair Morse, Xinxin Wang

公共财政与税收 未指明区域

Abstract

Early-stage firms utilize venture debt in one-third of financing rounds despite their general lack of cash flow and collateral. In our model, we show how venture debt aligns incentives within a firm. We derive a novel theoretical channel in which runway extension through debt increases firm value while potentially lowering closure. Consistent with the model's mechanism, we find that dilution predicts venture debt issuance. Empirically, treatment with venture debt lowers closure hazard by 1.6-4.4% and increases successful exits by 4.3-5.3%. Back-of-the-envelope calculations suggest $\$41$B, or 9.4% of invested capital, remains productive due to venture debt.

中文摘要

早期阶段的公司在三分之一的融资轮次中会使用风险债务，尽管它们普遍缺乏现金流和抵押品。在我们的模型中，我们展示了风险债务如何协调公司内部的激励。我们推导出一个新颖的理论渠道，即通过债务延长跑道可以增加公司价值，同时可能降低倒闭概率。与该模型的机制一致，我们发现稀释效应可以预测风险债务的发行。实证上，接受风险债务处理使倒闭风险降低1.6%至4.4%，并使成功退出的概率增加4.3%至5.3%。粗略估算表明，由于风险债务的存在，410亿美元（即投资资本的9.4%）仍保持生产力。
