A New Measure of Surviving Children that Sheds Light on Long-term Trends in Fertility

衡量存活子女数的新指标及其对生育率长期趋势的启示

Anup Malani, Ari Jacob

环境与健康 全球/多国

Abstract

The world has experienced a dramatic decline in total fertility rate (TFR) since the Industrial Revolution. Yet the consequences of this decline flow not merely from a reduction in births, but from a reduction in the number of surviving children. We propose a new measure of the number of surviving children per female, which we call the effective fertility rate (EFR). EFR can be approximated as the product of TFR and the probability of survival. Moreover, TFR changes can be decomposed into changes that preserve EFR and those that change EFR. We specialized EFR to measure the number of daughters that survive to reproduce (reproductive EFR) and the number children that survive to become workers (labor EFR). \nWe use three data sets to shed light on EFR over time across locations. First, we use data from 165 countries between 1950-2019 to show that one-third of the global decline in TFR during this period did not change labor EFR, suggesting that a substantial portion of fertility decline merely compensated for higher survival rates. Focusing on the change in labor EFR, at least 40% of variation cannot be explained by economic factors such as income, prices, education levels, structural transformation, an urbanization, leaving room for explanations like cultural change. Second, using historical demographic data on European countries since 1750, we find that there was dramatic fluctuation in labor EFR in Europe around each of the World Wars, a phenomenon that is distinct from the demographic transition. However, prior to that fluctuation, EFRs were remarkably constant, even as European countries were undergoing demographic transitions. Indeed, even when EFRs fell below 2 after 1975, we find that EFRs remained stable rather than continuing to decline. Third, data from the US since 1800 reveal that, despite great differences in mortality rates, Black and White populations have remarkably similar numbers of surviving children over time.

中文摘要

自工业革命以来，世界经历了总和生育率的急剧下降。然而，这种下降的后果不仅源于出生人数的减少，还源于存活子女数的减少。我们提出了一个衡量每位女性存活子女数的新指标，称为有效生育率。有效生育率可以近似为总和生育率与存活概率的乘积。此外，总和生育率的变化可以分解为保持有效生育率不变的变化和改变有效生育率的变化。我们将有效生育率专门化，以衡量存活到生育年龄的女儿数量（生殖有效生育率）和存活到成为劳动者的子女数量（劳动有效生育率）。我们使用三个数据集来揭示不同地区随时间变化的有效生育率。首先，我们利用1950年至2019年间165个国家的数据表明，在此期间全球总和生育率下降的三分之一并未改变劳动有效生育率，这表明相当一部分生育率下降仅仅是对更高存活率的补偿。关注劳动有效生育率的变化，至少40%的变异无法用收入、价格、教育水平、结构转型和城市化等经济因素解释，为文化变迁等解释留下了空间。其次，利用自1750年以来欧洲国家的历史人口数据，我们发现欧洲的劳动有效生育率在两次世界大战前后出现了剧烈波动，这一现象与人口转变截然不同。然而，在此波动之前，即使欧洲国家正在经历人口转变，有效生育率却异常稳定。事实上，即使在1975年后有效生育率降至2以下，我们发现有效生育率保持稳定而非持续下降。第三，自1800年以来美国的数据显示，尽管死亡率存在巨大差异，但黑人和白人群体的存活子女数随时间推移却惊人地相似。
