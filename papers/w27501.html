Going Bankrupt in China

在中国破产

Bo Li, Jacopo Ponticelli

其他主题 中国相关

Abstract

Using a new case-level dataset we document a set of stylized facts on bankruptcy in China and study how the staggered introduction of specialized courts across Chinese cities aﬀects insolvency resolution and the local economy. For identiﬁcation, we compare bankruptcy cases handled by specialized versus traditional civil courts within the same city and ﬁled in the same year. We ﬁnd that specialized courts decrease case duration by 36% relative to traditional civil courts. We provide evidence consistent with court specialization increasing eﬃciency via selection of better trained judges and higher judicial independence from local politicians. We document that cities introducing specialized courts experience a relative reallocation of employment out of zombie-ﬁrms-intensive sectors, as well as faster ﬁrm entry and a larger increase in average capital productivity.

中文摘要

利用一个新的案例层面数据集，我们记录了一组关于中国破产的典型事实，并研究了专业法院在中国各城市分阶段引入如何影响破产解决和当地经济。为了进行识别，我们比较了同一城市内、同一年立案的由专业法院与传统民事法院处理的破产案件。我们发现，相对于传统民事法院，专业法院将案件审理时长缩短了36%。我们提供的证据表明，法院专业化通过选拔训练有素的法官和提高法官相对于地方政治人物的独立性来提高效率。我们记录到，引入专业法院的城市经历了就业从僵尸企业密集型行业向其他行业的相对重新配置，以及更快的企业进入和平均资本生产率的更大提升。
