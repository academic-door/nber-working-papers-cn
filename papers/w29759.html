Group Corruption via Sequential Bargaining in a Hierarchical Organization

层级组织中的群体腐败：通过序贯谈判实现

Fan-chin Kung, Ping Wang, Quan Wen

其他主题 未指明区域

Abstract

We develop a framework of group corruption via back-door negotiations between an outside initiator and an authority of decision-makers in a hierarchical organization. We examine the role played by the architecture of a multi-tier authority and determine under such a structure how bargaining proceeds, in what order, and when it breaks down. We verify that equilibrium bargaining sequence proceeds as a chain through decision-making agents, regardless of the hierarchy of the organization. We prove the existence of a compromised equilibrium, where the decision of the authority is compromised, and establish sufficient conditions under which the most natural bottom-up bargaining configuration arises in equilibrium where a proposer negotiates with an immediately higher ranked respondent, starting with the initiator bargaining with the lowest ranked decision-maker in the organization. We then show the circumstances under which a top-down or a non-monotonic equilibrium configuration may emerge, and those under which the deal may break down. This enables us to capture a rich array of group corruptive configurations as observed. We conclude by investigating the extension to multi-tier authorities with multiple agents of the same rank in each tier, such as in a tree hierarchy.

中文摘要

我们构建了一个群体腐败框架，该框架通过外部发起者与层级组织中的决策者权威之间的幕后谈判来实现。我们研究了多层权威结构所扮演的角色，并确定了在这种结构下谈判如何进行、以何种顺序进行以及何时破裂。我们验证了无论组织层级如何，均衡谈判序列都作为一条链条贯穿决策者。我们证明了妥协均衡的存在，其中权威的决策被妥协，并建立了充分条件，使得最自然的自下而上谈判配置在均衡中出现，即提议者与直接上级回应者谈判，从发起者与组织中最低级别的决策者谈判开始。然后，我们展示了自上而下或非单调均衡配置可能出现的情况，以及交易可能破裂的情况。这使我们能够捕捉到观察到的丰富多样的群体腐败配置。最后，我们研究了扩展到具有多层权威且每层有多个同级代理人的情况，例如树形层级结构。
