Is Online Education Working?

在线教育有效吗？

Duha Tore Altindag, Elif S. Filiz, Erdal Tekin

教育与劳动 未指明区域

Abstract

This study examines the impact of instruction modality on student learning outcomes, with a focus on disparities observed pre- and post-pandemic. Using administrative data from a public university spanning seven pre-pandemic and five post-pandemic semesters, the analysis controls for endogenous sorting using fixed effects. The findings suggest that face-to-face (FtF) instruction results in better student performance, such as higher grades and a lower withdrawal rate. Additionally, students with greater exposure to FtF instruction are less likely to repeat courses, more likely to graduate on time, and achieve higher GPAs. The study also shows that the FtF advantage has been decreasing over time, and the differences are smaller post-pandemic. The results are consistent across student and instructor characteristics, except for Honors and graduate students, where the FtF advantage is either smaller or statistically insignificant.

中文摘要

本研究考察了教学方式对学生学习成果的影响，重点关注疫情前后观察到的差异。利用一所公立大学涵盖七个疫情前学期和五个疫情后学期的行政数据，分析通过固定效应控制了内生性选择。研究结果表明，面对面教学能带来更好的学生表现，例如更高的成绩和更低的退课率。此外，接触更多面对面教学的学生更不容易重修课程，更有可能按时毕业，并获得更高的平均绩点。研究还显示，面对面教学的优势随时间推移而减小，且疫情后差异更小。除荣誉学生和研究生外，这一结果在不同学生和教师特征中保持一致，对于这些群体，面对面教学的优势要么较小，要么在统计上不显著。
