The Effects of Government Licensing on E-commerce: Evidence from Alibaba

政府许可对电子商务的影响：来自阿里巴巴的证据

Ginger Zhe Jin, Zhentong Lu, Xiaolu Zhou, Chunxiao Li

其他主题 中国相关

Abstract

Using proprietary data from Alibaba, we examine how the 2015 Food Safety Law (FSL) affects e-commerce in China. The FSL requires most food sellers on e-commerce platforms to obtain a valid, online license for retail food handling. Because the FSL was rolled out progressively, we have a rare opportunity to observe a gradual transition from voluntary certification to partial licensing and mandatory licensing.\nData summary shows that, conditional on sellers with valid licensing information, those that had a better online reputation and more online food sales before FSL tend to display their FSL license earlier on the platform, and buyers are more willing to transact with a seller after she displays her FSL license. \nTo identify the causal impact of the FSL, we compare food and non-food categories via synthetic control matching. We find the average quality of surviving food sellers has improved after partial and mandatory licensing, partly because those who are unwilling to obtain the FSL license must exit the platform. Despite an increase in seller concentration, the platform's gross merchandise value (GMV) in the food category did not decline post FSL, nor did the average sales price increase significantly one year into full enforcement of the FSL.

中文摘要

利用阿里巴巴的专有数据，我们研究了2015年《食品安全法》如何影响中国的电子商务。《食品安全法》要求电子商务平台上的大多数食品销售者获得有效的在线零售食品经营许可证。由于《食品安全法》是逐步推行的，我们难得有机会观察到从自愿认证到部分许可再到强制许可的渐进过渡。数据总结显示，在拥有有效许可信息的卖家中，那些在《食品安全法》实施前拥有更好在线声誉和更多在线食品销售的卖家更倾向于在平台上更早展示其《食品安全法》许可证，而买家在卖家展示其《食品安全法》许可证后更愿意与其交易。为了识别《食品安全法》的因果影响，我们通过合成控制匹配比较了食品和非食品类别。我们发现，在部分许可和强制许可之后，幸存食品卖家的平均质量有所提高，部分原因是那些不愿获得《食品安全法》许可证的卖家必须退出平台。尽管卖家集中度有所增加，但平台在食品类别的总商品交易额在《食品安全法》实施后并未下降，且在《食品安全法》全面实施一年后，平均销售价格也未显著上涨。
