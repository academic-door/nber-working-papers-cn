Pareto Improvements in the Contest for College Admissions

大学招生竞争中的帕累托改进

Kala Krishna, Sergey Lychagin, Wojciech Olszewski, Ron Siegel, Chloe Tergiman

教育与劳动 全球/多国

Abstract

College admissions in many countries are based on a centrally administered test. Applicants invest a great deal of resources to improve their performance on the test, and there is growing concern about the large costs associated with these activities. We consider modifying such tests by introducing performance-disclosure policies that pool intervals of performance rankings, and investigate how such policies can improve students’ welfare in a Pareto sense. Pooling aﬀects the equilibrium allocation of studentso colleges, which hurts some students and beneﬁts others, but also aﬀects the eﬀort students exert. We characterize the Pareto frontier of Pareto improving policies, and also identify improvements that are robust to the distribution of college seats.\n\nWe illustrate the potential applicability of our results with an empirical estimation that uses data on college admissions in Turkey. We ﬁnd that a policy that pools a large fraction of the lowest performing students leads to a Pareto improvement in a contest based on the estimated parameters. We then conduct a laboratory experiment based on the estimated parameters to examine the eﬀect of such pooling on subjects’ behavior. The ﬁndings generally support our theoretical predictions. Our work suggests that identifying and introducing Pareto improving performance-disclosure policies may be a feasible and practical way to improve college admissions based on centralized tests.

中文摘要

许多国家的大学招生基于统一组织的考试。申请者投入大量资源以提高考试成绩，人们日益担忧这些活动带来的巨大成本。我们考虑通过引入绩效披露政策来修改此类考试，该政策将绩效排名的区间进行合并，并研究此类政策如何能在帕累托意义上改善学生的福利。合并会影响学生与大学之间的均衡分配，这伤害一些学生而惠及其他学生，但也会影响学生付出的努力。我们刻画了帕累托改进政策的帕累托前沿，并识别出对大学名额分布具有稳健性的改进。
