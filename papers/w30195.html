Crash Narratives

崩盘叙事

William N. Goetzmann, Dasol Kim, Robert J. Shiller

理论与方法 未指明区域

Abstract

Narratives can serve as linguistic mechanisms for transmitting knowledge about important historical events. Shiller (2017) argues that narratives are potent mechanisms for the propagation of beliefs about the economy. We use a controlled context approach to test the effect of media narratives about historic stock market crashes on current investor beliefs and choices. Our methodology avoids the problem of large language model leakage that can lead to look-ahead biases. We find that crash narratives propagate broadly once they appear in news articles, and that they predict market volatility. We exploit investor heterogeneity using survey data to distinguish the effects of narrativity from response to fundamental factors. Finally, we develop a measure of pure narrativity to examine when the financial press is more likely to employ narratives.

中文摘要

叙事可以作为传递关于重要历史事件知识的语言机制。Shiller（2017）认为，叙事是传播关于经济信念的有力机制。我们采用受控情境方法来测试关于历史股市崩盘的媒体叙事对当前投资者信念和选择的影响。我们的方法避免了可能导致前瞻性偏差的大型语言模型泄漏问题。我们发现，崩盘叙事一旦出现在新闻文章中就会广泛传播，并且它们能预测市场波动。我们利用调查数据中的投资者异质性来区分叙事效应与对基本面因素的反应。最后，我们开发了一个纯叙事性衡量指标，以考察金融媒体何时更可能使用叙事。
