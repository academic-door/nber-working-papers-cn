Using Mobile Device Activity Data to Study Local Variation in Onsite Work

利用移动设备活动数据研究现场工作的局部变化

Katharine G. Abraham, Mohammad Ashoori, Aref Darzi, Nathalie Gonzalez-Prieto, John C. Haltiwanger, Aliakbar Kabiri, Erkut Y. Ozbay

其他主题 美国

Abstract

Mobile device location data suitable for a variety of research and commercial purposes have become increasingly available. We use these data to provide new evidence on the evolution of onsite work (OSW) following the pandemic. In one analysis, we start with a large sample of individuals who, based on their mobile device activity, had a job at which they worked onsite in February 2020, then track those individuals’ onsite work activity in May and August 2020. We then carry out a parallel analysis for 2019 and compare the persistence in OSW across the two time periods. In a second analysis, we analyze the ratio of measured OSW activity in September 2020, September 2021 and September 2022 to measured OSW activity in February 2020. In both analyses, we work with Census-tract-level estimates, documenting considerable cross-tract variation in OSW outcomes nationally, within states and cities, and even within counties. Observable characteristics such as industry, occupation, and household income in the tract account for much of the observed variation, but there is also substantial unexplained residual variation. Our results imply considerable heterogeneity in how the pandemic affected where the resident populations of U.S. neighborhoods spend their days, a finding that has significant implications for businesses, workers, and policymakers. We use this study of the evolution of OSW following the pandemic to evaluate the strengths and weaknesses of mobile device location data for tracking economic activity.

中文摘要

适用于各种研究和商业目的的移动设备位置数据已变得越来越容易获取。我们利用这些数据，为疫情后现场工作（OSW）的演变提供了新证据。在一项分析中，我们从一个大样本个体开始，这些个体基于其移动设备活动，在2020年2月拥有一份现场工作，然后追踪这些个体在2020年5月和8月的现场工作活动。接着，我们对2019年进行平行分析，并比较两个时间段内现场工作的持续性。在第二项分析中，我们分析了2020年9月、2021年9月和2022年9月测量的现场工作活动与2020年2月测量的现场工作活动之比。在这两项分析中，我们使用人口普查区层面的估计值，记录了全国范围内、各州和城市内、甚至各县内现场工作结果的显著跨区差异。该区可观测的特征，如行业、职业和家庭收入，解释了大部分观测到的变异，但也存在大量无法解释的残差变异。我们的结果表明，疫情如何影响美国社区常住居民日常活动地点的方式存在相当大的异质性，这一发现对企业、工人和政策制定者具有重要影响。我们利用这项关于疫情后现场工作演变的研究，来评估移动设备位置数据在追踪经济活动方面的优势和劣势。
