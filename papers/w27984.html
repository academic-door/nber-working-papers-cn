Communication and Bargaining Breakdown: An Empirical Analysis

沟通与谈判破裂：一项实证分析

Matthew Backus, Thomas Blake, Jett Pettus, Steven Tadelis

国际贸易 德国

Abstract

Bargaining breakdown—whether as delay, conflict, or missing trade—plagues bargaining in environments with incomplete information. Can a bargaining environment that facilitates or restricts communication alleviate these costs? We exploit a unique opportunity to study this question using real market transactions: eBay Germany’s Best Offer platform. On May 23, 2016, the platform introduced unstructured communication allowing buyers and sellers on the desktop version of the site, but not the mobile app, to accompany price offers with a message. Using this natural experiment, our difference-in-differences approach documents a 14% decrease in the the rate of breakdown among compliers. Though adoption is immediate, the effect is not. We show, using text analysis, that the dynamics are consistent with repeat players learning how to use communication in bargaining, and that the messaging strategies of experienced sellers are correlated with successful bargaining.

中文摘要

谈判破裂——无论是表现为延迟、冲突还是交易缺失——困扰着信息不完全环境中的谈判。一个促进或限制沟通的谈判环境能否减轻这些成本？我们利用一个独特的机会，通过真实市场交易来研究这个问题：eBay德国的“最佳报价”平台。2016年5月23日，该平台引入了非结构化沟通，允许桌面版网站（而非移动应用）上的买家和卖家在报价时附带一条消息。利用这一自然实验，我们的双重差分方法记录了在依从者中谈判破裂率下降了14%。尽管采用是即时的，但效果并非如此。我们通过文本分析表明，这种动态与重复参与者学习如何在谈判中使用沟通是一致的，并且经验丰富的卖家的消息传递策略与成功的谈判相关。
