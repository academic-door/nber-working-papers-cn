NBER Working Paper w31851所在周报所在月度合集

Implications of Asset Market Data for Equilibrium Models of Exchange Rates

资产市场数据对汇率均衡模型的含义

Zhengyang Jiang, Arvind Krishnamurthy, Hanno Lustig, Jialu Sun

金融与资产定价 未指明区域

Abstract

We characterize the cyclicality and predictability of exchange rates without committing to a specific model of preferences, endowment or menu of traded assets. When investors can trade home and foreign currency risk-free bonds without frictions, the exchange rate conditionally appreciates in states of the world that are worse for home investors than foreign investors. We show that its unconditional counterpart can only be overturned if the deviations from U.I.P. are large and exchange rates are highly predictable, conditions that do not hold in the data. Thus, without frictions in bond markets, it is impossible to match the empirical exchange rate cyclicality (the Backus- Smith puzzle) and the deviations from U.I.P. (the Fama puzzle) as well as the lack of predictability (the Meese-Rogoff puzzle). To relax this trade-off, we need small Euler equation wedges consistent with a home currency bias, home bond convenience yields, or financial repression, but we do not need market segmentation.

中文摘要

我们在不承诺特定偏好、禀赋或可交易资产菜单模型的情况下，刻画了汇率的周期性和可预测性。当投资者可以在无摩擦条件下交易本币和外币无风险债券时，汇率会在对本币投资者比对外币投资者更差的世界状态下条件性升值。我们表明，只有当无抛补利率平价偏离很大且汇率高度可预测时，其无条件对应关系才可能被推翻，而这些条件在数据中并不成立。因此，在没有债券市场摩擦的情况下，不可能同时匹配经验汇率周期性（巴克斯-史密斯之谜）、无抛补利率平价偏离（法玛之谜）以及缺乏可预测性（米斯-罗戈夫之谜）。为了放松这种权衡，我们需要与本国货币偏好、本国债券便利收益率或金融压抑相一致的小型欧拉方程楔子，但我们不需要市场分割。
