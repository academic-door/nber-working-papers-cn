An Informational Nudge to Shave Peak Demand

削减峰值需求的信息助推

Gilbert E. Metcalf

其他主题 未指明区域

Abstract

Informational nudges to encourage energy conservation or load shifting have been tried in various contexts. This paper studies a program run by a small municipally owned electric utility to reduce demand on certain peak demand days. An email alert is sent out to residential customers who sign up for the alerts. Some recipients of those alerts forward the alerts to other customers or community groups, making it difficult to determine how broadly the alerts are disseminated. The alerts encourage load shifting and energy saving during specific hours on the following day. \nUsing hourly load data for the utility, I estimate the reduction in electricity load caused by the alert emails. Using an instrumental variables approach, estimates suggest that load is reduced by roughly 0.7 MWs per hour during the hours covered by the alert. This works out to a reduction in load on the order of 2 percent. I calculate the cost savings to the municipal utility and discuss social and private benefits of the program. The private benefits of the peak alert program swamp the social benefits.

中文摘要

鼓励节能或负荷转移的信息助推已在多种情境下尝试过。本文研究了一家小型市政电力公司为减少特定峰值需求日的需求而运行的项目。向注册接收警报的居民客户发送电子邮件警报。部分警报接收者将警报转发给其他客户或社区团体，这使得难以确定警报的传播范围。这些警报鼓励在次日的特定时段内进行负荷转移和节能。利用该公用事业公司的每小时负荷数据，我估算了警报电子邮件导致的电力负荷减少量。使用工具变量方法，估计表明在警报覆盖的时段内，负荷每小时减少约0.7兆瓦。这相当于负荷减少约2%。我计算了该市政公用事业公司的成本节约，并讨论了该项目的私人和社会效益。该峰值警报项目的私人效益远超社会效益。
