Immigrants' Legal Status and Firms: Evidence from the 2007 EU Enlargement

移民的法律身份与企业：来自2007年欧盟扩员的证据

Vittoria Dicandia, Silvia Vannutelli

教育与劳动 保加利亚、意大利、罗马尼亚

Abstract

We study how firms and workers adjust when previously restricted migrants gain full and portable work rights in a labor market with substantial informality. We exploit the 2007 EU accession of Bulgaria and Romania, which granted unrestricted work rights to Italy's largest migrant group. Using matched employer–employee administrative data and an IV-DID design, we find that firms suddenly and persistently shift employment composition toward EU07 workers, compressing the native employment share without reducing native hiring or increasing separations. We don't detect any significant change in wages for either natives or EU07 workers. For migrants, this null effect reflects offsetting compositional shifts as newly observed and incumbent EU07 workers enter the formal workforce with different wage trajectories. Consistent with a shift in bargaining power toward workers, EU07 migrants experienced significant gains in job mobility and job security. Overall, the evidence suggests that removing legal restrictions reshaped firms’ personnel choices and altered migrants’ employment relationships, improving their outside options, bargaining position, and access to more secure jobs.

中文摘要

我们研究当此前受限的移民在存在大量非正规就业的劳动力市场中获得完全且可携带的工作权利时，企业和工人如何调整。我们利用2007年保加利亚和罗马尼亚加入欧盟的事件，该事件赋予了意大利最大移民群体不受限制的工作权利。使用匹配的雇主–雇员行政数据和工具变量双重差分设计，我们发现企业突然且持续地将就业构成转向欧盟07工人，压缩了本土工人的就业份额，但并未减少本土工人的招聘或增加离职。我们未检测到本土工人或欧盟07工人的工资发生任何显著变化。对移民而言，这一零效应反映了抵消性的构成变化，即新被观测到的和在岗的欧盟07工人以不同的工资轨迹进入正规劳动力队伍。与工人议价能力向工人方转移相一致，欧盟07移民在工作流动性和工作保障方面获得了显著改善。总体而言，证据表明，取消法律限制重塑了企业的人事选择，改变了移民的雇佣关系，改善了他们的外部选择、议价地位以及获得更稳定工作的机会。
