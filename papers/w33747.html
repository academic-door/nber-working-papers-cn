The Power of Persuasion: Causal Effects of Household Communication on Women's Employment

说服的力量：家庭沟通对女性就业的因果效应

Namrata Kala, Madeline McKelway

教育与劳动 印度

Abstract

In many economic settings, agents lack decision rights but provide input. Intra-household decision-making in contexts with restrictive gender norms is one important example; wives often lack final say over decisions but still give input. Their ability to communicate persuasively while providing input could sway decisions in their favor. We conduct a field experiment in rural India to test whether an effective communication training among married women impacts their labor supply, the most common topic of intra-household disagreement in the sample. The treatment shifted women's communication styles towards the techniques taught in the training. We find positive effects on labor supply and earnings but, consistent with theory, only for women who at baseline were more interested than their husbands in the women working. These effects persist to at least one year following the treatment, leading to a nearly 60% increase in earnings over this period. Mechanisms analyses suggest the changes in labor supply are not due to shifts in bargaining power but rather come from women changing their husbands' preferences about female employment.

中文摘要

在许多经济环境中，行为主体缺乏决策权但提供意见。在性别规范严格的环境中，家庭内部决策是一个重要例子：妻子通常对决策没有最终决定权，但仍会提供意见。她们在提供意见时进行有效沟通的能力可能影响决策结果。我们在印度农村进行了一项实地实验，检验对已婚女性进行有效沟通培训是否会影响她们的劳动供给——这是样本中家庭内部最常见的分歧话题。该干预措施使女性的沟通风格转向培训所教授的技巧。我们发现，培训对劳动供给和收入产生了积极影响，但根据理论，这种影响仅适用于那些在基线时比丈夫更希望自己工作的女性。这些效应在干预后至少持续一年，导致该期间收入增长近60%。机制分析表明，劳动供给的变化并非源于议价能力的转变，而是来自女性改变了丈夫对女性就业的偏好。
