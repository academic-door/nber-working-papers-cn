Bayesian Adaptive Clinical Trials for Anti‐Infective Therapeutics during Epidemic Outbreaks

疫情暴发期间抗感染治疗的贝叶斯自适应临床试验

Shomesh Chaudhuri, Andrew W. Lo, Danying Xiao, Qingyang Xu

教育与劳动 未指明区域

Abstract

In the midst of epidemics such as COVID-19, therapeutic candidates are unlikely to be able to complete the usual multiyear clinical trial and regulatory approval process within the course of an outbreak. We apply a Bayesian adaptive patient-centered model—which minimizes the expected harm of false positives and false negatives—to optimize the clinical trial development path during such outbreaks. When the epidemic is more infectious and fatal, the Bayesian-optimal sample size in the clinical trial is lower and the optimal statistical significance level is higher. For COVID-19 (assuming a static <i>R</i>₀ – 2 and initial infection percentage of 0.1%), the optimal significance level is 7.1% for a clinical trial of a nonvaccine anti-infective therapeutic and 13.6% for that of a vaccine. For a dynamic <i>R</i>₀ decreasing from 3 to 1.5, the corresponding values are 14.4% and 26.4%, respectively. Our results illustrate the importance of adapting the clinical trial design and the regulatory approval process to the specific parameters and stage of the epidemic.

中文摘要

在COVID-19等疫情中，治疗候选药物不太可能在疫情暴发期间完成通常需要多年的临床试验和监管审批流程。我们应用一种以患者为中心的贝叶斯自适应模型——该模型最小化假阳性和假阴性的预期危害——来优化此类疫情中的临床试验开发路径。当疫情更具传染性和致命性时，贝叶斯最优的临床试验样本量较低，而最优统计显著性水平较高。对于COVID-19（假设静态R₀为2，初始感染百分比为0.1%），非疫苗抗感染治疗临床试验的最优显著性水平为7.1%，疫苗临床试验为13.6%。对于动态R₀从3降至1.5的情况，相应值分别为14.4%和26.4%。我们的结果说明了根据疫情的具体参数和阶段调整临床试验设计和监管审批流程的重要性。
