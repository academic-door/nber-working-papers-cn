The Distributional Impacts of a VMT-Gas Tax Swap

车辆里程税与汽油税互换的分配效应

Gilbert E. Metcalf

公共财政与税收 未指明区域

Abstract

More stringent fuel economy standards and increased market penetration of electric vehicles (EVs) present challenges to federal policy makers who historically have relied on motor vehicle fuel excise taxes to fund highway projects. This paper considers the distributional implications of a federal tax swap where a new vehicle miles travelled (VMT) tax is used to finance a reduction in the federal excise tax on gasoline. Whether the tax shift is progressive (relative to the pivot point) or not depends on the sign of the income elasticity of demand for fuel intensity. If it is negative (higher-income households demand more fuel efficient cars), then the tax shift is progressive around the pivot point. Conversely, if it is positive, then the tax shift is regressive around the pivot point. Where the pivot point occurs and how progressive a shift occurs is an empirical matter.\nUsing data from the 2017 National Household Travel Survey (NHTS), I find that the income elasticity of fuel intensity is negative and that this revenue-neutral tax swap to be mildly progressive for all household incomes below $\$200,000$. This is driven, in part, by the fact that higher income households are more likely to drive hybrid and electric vehicles and to own newer vehicles which, due to increasingly stringent fuel economy standards, tend to be more fuel efficient. How the progressivity of a tax swap changes as fuel economy standards are raised and EV market penetration increases depends on who purchases EVs and more efficient vehicles. Federal policy will likely play a role in influencing the future distribution of EV ownership. In addition, I find the tax swap benefits rural drivers and has no appreciable differential impacts on Black and Hispanic households.

中文摘要

更严格的燃油经济性标准和电动汽车市场渗透率的提高，给历史上依赖机动车燃油消费税来资助公路项目的联邦政策制定者带来了挑战。本文考虑联邦税收互换的分配效应，即用新的车辆里程税来资助联邦汽油消费税的削减。税收转移相对于支点是累进的还是累退的，取决于燃油强度的收入弹性符号。如果该弹性为负（高收入家庭需求更省油的汽车），则税收转移在支点附近是累进的。相反，如果为正，则税收转移在支点附近是累退的。支点出现的位置以及转移的累进程度是一个实证问题。利用2017年全国家庭旅行调查的数据，我发现燃油强度的收入弹性为负，并且这种收入中性的税收互换对所有收入低于20万美元的家庭而言是轻微累进的。这在一定程度上是由于高收入家庭更可能驾驶混合动力和电动汽车，并拥有更新、因日益严格的燃油经济性标准而更省油的车辆。随着燃油经济性标准提高和电动汽车市场渗透率增加，税收互换的累进性如何变化取决于谁购买电动汽车和更高效的车辆。联邦政策可能会在影响未来电动汽车拥有权的分布方面发挥作用。此外，我发现税收互换使农村司机受益，并且对黑人和西班牙裔家庭没有明显的差异化影响。
