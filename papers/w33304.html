Equilibrium Conditions for Catch-22 Situations

第二十二条军规情境的均衡条件

Joshua S. Gans

金融与资产定价 未指明区域

Abstract

This is a paper in the ``economists ruin everything'' field. It considers whether Catch-22 situations can persist as an equilibrium phenomenon. Rather than being an arbitrary rule or a set of self-serving beliefs, the focus is on the preferences of Gatekeepers who choose to create such situations in the first place. The base game-theoretic model is of a Catch-22 situation inspired by Heller's famous paradox. We consider a Requester who may be Sane or Insane and a Gatekeeper who must decide whether to grant the Requester's desired outcome or force them into a less desirable one. This is modelled as a game in which the Requester chooses whether to send a request signal before the Gatekeeper decides. We solve for the conditions under which a Catch-22 situation persists as an equilibrium and its efficiency properties. It is demonstrated that Catch-22 situations can arise, but they reflect an efficient response on the part of a Gatekeeper facing asymmetric information. An application to labour markets is also considered

中文摘要

这是一篇属于“经济学家毁掉一切”领域的论文。它探讨了第二十二条军规情境是否能够作为一种均衡现象持续存在。本文关注的焦点并非任意规则或一套自利信念，而是那些最初选择创造此类情境的守门人的偏好。基础博弈论模型受海勒著名悖论的启发，描述了一种第二十二条军规情境。我们考虑一个可能理智或疯狂的请求者，以及一个必须决定是批准请求者的期望结果还是迫使其接受较差结果的守门人。这被建模为一个博弈，其中请求者在守门人决策之前选择是否发送请求信号。我们求解了第二十二条军规情境作为均衡持续存在的条件及其效率属性。研究表明，第二十二条军规情境可能出现，但它们反映了守门人在面临信息不对称时做出的有效反应。本文还考虑了其在劳动力市场中的应用。
