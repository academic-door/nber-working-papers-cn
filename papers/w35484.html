Policy Thresholds as Growth Barriers: Theory and Evidence from a Payroll Tax Notch

作为增长障碍的政策阈值：来自工资税断点的理论与证据

Sami Jysmä, Youssef Benzarti, Jarkko Harju

宏观与货币财政 未指明区域

Abstract

Discrete policy thresholds are pervasive in tax and regulatory systems and can substantially distort behavior. We show that notches acting as barriers to mobility within a distribution generate distortions extending far beyond the threshold. The same mechanism biases conventional difference-in-differences estimators, and we propose a new methodology to recover causal effects. Applying the method to the abolition of a size-based payroll tax notch, we find that the notch reduced the number of firms above the threshold by 18 percent and lowered treated firms’ employment, capital stock, and value added by 10 percent, whereas conventional difference-in-differences estimates imply negligible effects.

中文摘要

离散的政策阈值在税收和监管体系中普遍存在，并可能显著扭曲行为。我们证明，作为分布内流动性障碍的断点所产生的扭曲效应远远超出阈值本身。相同的机制会使传统的双重差分估计量产生偏误，我们提出了一种新的方法来恢复因果效应。将该方法应用于废除基于规模的工资税断点，我们发现该断点使阈值以上的企业数量减少了18%，并使受处理企业的就业、资本存量和增加值降低了10%，而传统的双重差分估计则意味着影响可以忽略不计。
