The Macroeconomic Effects of Climate Policy Uncertainty

气候政策不确定性的宏观经济效应

Konstantinos Gavriilidis, Diego R. Känzig, Ramya Raghavan, James H. Stock

环境与健康 美国

Abstract

We develop a measure of climate policy uncertainty based on newspaper coverage. Our index spikes during key U.S. climate policy events—including presidential announcements on international agreements, congressional debates, and regulatory disputes—and shows a recent upward trend. Using a novel instrument for plausibly exogenous uncertainty shifts, we find that higher climate policy uncertainty decreases output and emissions while raising commodity and consumer prices, acting as supply rather than demand shocks. Faced with this trade-off, monetary policy does not accommodate climate policy uncertainty shocks, shaping their transmission. Firm-level analyses show stronger declines in investment and R&D when firms have higher climate change exposure.

中文摘要

我们基于报纸报道构建了一个气候政策不确定性指标。该指标在美国关键气候政策事件期间（包括总统关于国际协议的声明、国会辩论和监管争议）出现峰值，并显示出近期的上升趋势。利用一个用于识别合理外生不确定性冲击的新工具变量，我们发现气候政策不确定性上升会降低产出和排放，同时推高大宗商品和消费价格，其作用类似于供给冲击而非需求冲击。面对这种权衡，货币政策并未对气候政策不确定性冲击做出适应性调整，从而塑造了其传导机制。企业层面的分析表明，当企业对气候变化的暴露程度更高时，其投资和研发的下降幅度更大。
