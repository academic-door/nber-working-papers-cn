Loneliness, Mental Health and the Work-From-Home Revolution

孤独感、心理健康与居家办公革命

Benjamin W. Cowan, Joe M. Spearing

环境与健康 英国

Abstract

The large increase in remote work since 2020 has prompted concerns about adverse effects on population loneliness and mental health. We show that any such adverse effects were transitory, in a UK context. We use panel data from UKHLS and differences-in-differences estimators that flexibly control for a rich set of covariates to compare changes in key variables amongst two groups: those who worked in teleworkable occupations in 2019, and those who worked in non-teleworkable occupations in 2019. While the former experience large and persistent increases in their probability of working remotely compared to the latter, adverse relative changes in self-reported loneliness or adverse mental health symptoms during the pandemic had disappeared by the year 2023.

中文摘要

自2020年以来远程工作的大幅增加引发了对其对人口孤独感和心理健康产生不利影响的担忧。我们表明，在英国背景下，任何此类不利影响都是暂时的。我们使用来自UKHLS的面板数据以及灵活控制丰富协变量集的双重差分估计量，比较两组人群关键变量的变化：2019年从事可远程办公职业的人群，以及2019年从事不可远程办公职业的人群。尽管与后者相比，前者远程工作的概率经历了巨大且持续的增长，但在疫情期间，自我报告的孤独感或不良心理健康症状方面的相对不利变化到2023年已经消失。
