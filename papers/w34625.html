Employment Impacts of the CHIPS Act

《芯片法案》的就业影响

Bilge Erten, Joseph E. Stiglitz, Eric Verhoogen

其他主题 美国

Abstract

The CHIPS and Science Act, enacted in August 2022, is a key element of the revival of U.S. industrial policy. We examine the short-term employment effects of the act. Drawing on quarterly industry-by-county data from the Quarterly Census of Employment and Wages (QCEW), we implement two county-level difference-in-difference designs, the first comparing counties with pre-existing semiconductor facilities to other counties with high-tech industries and the second comparing counties with semiconductor fabrication facilities (which were targeted for the bulk of the CHIPS funding) to counties with non-fabrication semiconductor facilities. Using both approaches, we find robust, positive employment impacts in affected counties. The effects began at the time of the passage in the Senate of a precursor bill, in anticipation of the signing of the CHIPS Act. Our preferred estimates suggest an increase of 110 jobs per affected county in the first design and 180 jobs per affected county in the second design. We also find robust positive impacts on local construction employment. Evidence on total employment and GDP at the county level, as well as on employment in upstream input sectors, is mixed. Simple back-of-the-envelope calculations (which come with caveats) suggest national direct employment effects of approximately 15,000-16,000 jobs in the core semiconductor sector and indirect effects of 15,000-30,000 jobs in related sectors.

中文摘要

2022年8月颁布的《芯片与科学法案》是美国产业政策复兴的关键要素。我们考察了该法案的短期就业效应。利用来自《季度就业与工资普查》（QCEW）的季度行业-县数据，我们实施了两种县级双重差分设计，第一种是比较拥有现有半导体设施的县与其他拥有高科技产业的县，第二种是比较拥有半导体制造设施（这些设施是《芯片法案》大部分资金的目标）的县与拥有非制造类半导体设施的县。使用这两种方法，我们发现在受影响的县中，就业影响稳健且为正。这些效应始于参议院通过一项前身法案之时，早于《芯片法案》的签署。我们的首选估计表明，在第一种设计中，每个受影响的县增加了110个工作岗位，在第二种设计中，每个受影响的县增加了180个工作岗位。我们还发现对当地建筑就业有稳健的正向影响。关于县级总就业和GDP，以及上游投入部门就业的证据则喜忧参半。简单的粗略计算（附带说明）表明，核心半导体部门的全国直接就业效应约为15,000-16,000个工作岗位，相关部门的间接效应为15,000-30,000个工作岗位。
