Identification of Time-Inconsistent Models: The Case of Insecticide Treated Nets

时间不一致模型的识别：以杀虫剂处理蚊帐为例

Aprajit Mahajan, Christian Michel, Alessandro Tarozzi

理论与方法 印度

Abstract

Time-inconsistency may play a central role in explaining inter-temporal behavior, particularly among poor households. However, little is known about the distribution of time-inconsistent agents, and time-preference parameters are typically not identified in standard dynamic choice models. We formulate a dynamic discrete choice model in an unobservedly heterogeneous population of possibly time-inconsistent agents. We provide conditions under which all population type probabilities and preferences for both time-consistent and sophisticated agents are point-identified and sharp set-identification results for naïve and partially sophisticated agents. Estimating the model using data from a health intervention providing insecticide treated nets (ITNs) in rural Odisha, India, we find that about two-thirds of our sample comprises time-inconsistent agents and that both sophisticated and naïve agents are considerably present-biased. Counterfactuals show that the under-investment in ITNs attributable to present-bias leads to substantial costs that are about four times the price of an ITN.

中文摘要

时间不一致性可能在解释跨期行为中发挥核心作用，尤其是在贫困家庭中。然而，关于时间不一致行为主体的分布知之甚少，且时间偏好参数在标准动态选择模型中通常无法识别。我们构建了一个动态离散选择模型，研究对象是可能具有时间不一致性的不可观测异质性人群。我们提供了条件，在这些条件下，所有人群类型概率以及时间一致和精明行为主体的偏好都可以点识别，而对于天真和部分精明行为主体则得到尖锐集识别结果。利用来自印度奥里萨邦农村一项提供杀虫剂处理蚊帐的健康干预措施的数据估计该模型，我们发现样本中约三分之二的人属于时间不一致行为主体，并且精明和天真行为主体都表现出显著的现时偏向。反事实分析表明，由现时偏向导致的杀虫剂处理蚊帐投资不足带来了巨大成本，约为杀虫剂处理蚊帐价格的四倍。
