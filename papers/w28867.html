Evidence and Lessons on the Health Impacts of Public Health Funding from the Fight against HIV/AIDS

公共卫生资金对健康影响的证据与教训：来自抗击HIV/AIDS的经验

Marcus Dillender

环境与健康 美国

Abstract

A key approach used by federal governments to address public health issues is to allocate federal funds to support local responses, but little is known about the effectiveness of this approach for improving health. This study examines the impact of federal public health funds allocated to U.S. cities through the U.S. government’s primary mechanism for combating HIV/AIDS for the past three decades. The empirical approach identifies the impact of this funding by studying funding variation that comes from policy features that resulted in large funding differences among cities that were originally on parallel HIV/AIDS trajectories. The findings indicate that an HIV/AIDS death has been prevented for every $\$334,000$ allocated through the city-level funding and that the $\$19$ billion allocated to cities through this program through 2018 has saved approximately 57,000 lives, which represents over $\$560$ billion of value in terms of lives saved assuming a value of a statistical life of $\$10$ million. The findings also indicate that funding differences across cities have been a major contributor to the uneven progress in combating HIV/AIDS currently observed across the United States that has alarmed public health leaders. Thus, while this analysis supports allocating federal funds to local areas as part of a public health strategy, these funds being effective demonstrates how sustained differences in areas’ receipt of federal public health funds can contribute to the development of health disparities across areas, as has occurred with HIV/AIDS.

中文摘要

联邦政府应对公共卫生问题的一个关键方法是分配联邦资金以支持地方应对措施，但关于这种方法在改善健康方面的有效性知之甚少。本研究考察了通过美国政府过去三十年来抗击HIV/AIDS的主要机制分配给美国城市的联邦公共卫生资金的影响。实证方法通过研究政策特征导致的资金差异来识别这种资金的影响，这些政策特征使得原本处于平行HIV/AIDS轨迹的城市之间出现了巨大的资金差异。研究结果表明，通过城市级资金每分配334,000美元，就预防了一例HIV/AIDS死亡；截至2018年，通过该项目分配给城市的190亿美元已挽救了约57,000人的生命，假设统计生命价值为1000万美元，这相当于超过5600亿美元的生命价值。研究结果还表明，城市间的资金差异是当前美国各地在抗击HIV/AIDS方面进展不均衡的主要因素，这种不均衡已引起公共卫生领导人的警惕。因此，尽管本分析支持将联邦资金分配给地方作为公共卫生战略的一部分，但这些资金的有效性表明，各地区在获得联邦公共卫生资金方面的持续差异如何导致地区间健康差距的发展，正如HIV/AIDS领域所发生的情况。
