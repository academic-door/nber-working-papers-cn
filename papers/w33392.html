Crisis Bargaining with Collective Decision Making

集体决策下的危机谈判

Jeongbin Kim, Thomas R. Palfrey, Jeffrey Zeidel

其他主题 未指明区域

Abstract

Crisis bargaining games are widely used to analyze bilateral conflicts, featuring strategic bluffing akin to poker. Players risk substantial losses from overplaying their hand but can secure significant gains if their opponent concedes. Since decisions in crises typically emerge from collective decision processes within organizations composed of diverse individual members, we use the team equilibrium solution concept to analyze these games, providing a framework for group strategic decision-making under collective choice rules (Kim, et al. 2022). In a team equilibrium, group members have rational expectations about opponents' strategies and share common average payoffs, with private payoff perturbations. Voting rules determine group decisions, assuming optimal voting behavior. Depending on the payoff structure, collective choice rules can lead to more or less bluffing and varying aggression compared to Perfect Bayesian Equilibrium. Our experiment varies game payoffs, group size, and voting rules. Behavior is inconsistent with Perfect Bayesian Equilibrium but broadly consistent with team equilibrium predictions.

中文摘要

危机谈判博弈被广泛用于分析双边冲突，其特点是类似于扑克牌游戏中的战略性虚张声势。玩家可能因过度出牌而遭受重大损失，但如果对手认输，则可以获得显著收益。由于危机中的决策通常源于由不同个体成员组成的组织内部的集体决策过程，我们使用团队均衡概念来分析这些博弈，为集体选择规则下的群体战略决策提供了一个框架（Kim等人，2022）。在团队均衡中，群体成员对对手的策略有理性预期，并共享共同的平均收益，但存在私人收益扰动。投票规则决定群体决策，假设投票行为是最优的。根据收益结构的不同，与完美贝叶斯均衡相比，集体选择规则可能导致更多或更少的虚张声势以及不同程度的攻击性。我们的实验改变了博弈收益、群体规模和投票规则。行为与完美贝叶斯均衡不一致，但与团队均衡预测大致相符。
