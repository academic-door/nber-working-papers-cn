We Won't be Missed: Work and Growth in the AGI World

我们不会被怀念：通用人工智能世界中的工作与增长

Pascual Restrepo

宏观与货币财政 未指明区域

Abstract

This chapter explores the long-run implications of Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) for economic growth and labor markets. AGI makes it feasible to perform all economically valuable work using compute. I distinguish between bottleneck and supplementary work—tasks that are essential versus non-essential for unhindered growth. As computational resources expand: (i) the economy automates all bottleneck work, (ii) some supplementary work may be left exclusively to humans, (iii) output becomes linear in compute and labor and its growth is driven by the expansion of compute, (iv) wages converge to the opportunity cost of computational resources required to reproduce human work, and (v) the share of labor income in GDP converges to zero.

中文摘要

本章探讨了通用人工智能（AGI）对经济增长和劳动力市场的长期影响。AGI使得利用计算资源执行所有具有经济价值的工作成为可能。我将工作区分为瓶颈性工作和补充性工作——即对无阻碍增长而言至关重要与非至关重要的任务。随着计算资源的扩张：（i）经济将自动化所有瓶颈性工作，（ii）部分补充性工作可能完全留给人类完成，（iii）产出与计算和劳动力呈线性关系，其增长由计算资源的扩张驱动，（iv）工资趋近于复制人类工作所需计算资源的机会成本，以及（v）劳动收入占GDP的份额趋近于零。
