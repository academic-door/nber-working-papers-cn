Anti-Harassment Policy and the Startup Labor Market

反骚扰政策与初创企业劳动力市场

Jun Chen, Song Ma, Feng Zhang

教育与劳动 美国

Abstract

This paper examines how anti-harassment legal reforms that weaken non-disclosure agreements (NDAs) in cases of workplace sexual harassment affect startups' hiring and organizational decisions. Using a staggered difference-in-differences design and LinkedIn data on over 50,000 U.S. venture-capital-backed startups from 2014–2022, we find that NDA reforms, although intended for employee protection, reduce female hiring by about 8%, with effects concentrated among junior women, who are statistically more prone to sexual harassment, and in small or male-dominated startups. The results apply to both the intensive and extensive margins of female hiring. Treated entrepreneurial firms also witness more departures of male managers, promote more women, and receive less VC funding. These results suggest that while NDA-weakening laws increase firms’ perceived legal risk and reduce female hiring, they also trigger internal restructuring that promotes women's advancement into leadership and may, over time, foster more accountable and inclusive organizational cultures.

中文摘要

本文研究了在工作场所性骚扰案件中削弱保密协议（NDA）的反骚扰法律改革，如何影响初创企业的招聘和组织决策。利用交错双重差分设计以及2014-2022年间超过5万家美国风险投资支持初创企业的领英数据，我们发现，尽管NDA改革旨在保护员工，但它使女性招聘减少了约8%，这种影响集中在初级女性员工（她们在统计上更容易遭受性骚扰）以及小型或男性主导的初创企业中。该结果适用于女性招聘的集约边际和广延边际。受影响的创业企业还观察到更多男性管理者的离职，提拔了更多女性，并且获得的VC资金减少。这些结果表明，虽然削弱NDA的法律增加了企业感知的法律风险并减少了女性招聘，但它们也引发了内部重组，促进了女性晋升至领导岗位，并且随着时间的推移，可能培育出更负责任和更具包容性的组织文化。
