The Price of Political Ties: Reputational Risk and the Strategic Divestment of Corporate PACs

政治关联的代价：声誉风险与企业政治行动委员会的战略性撤资

Eric A. Baldwin, Takuma Iwasaki, John J. Donohue, Charlie Grabois

公共财政与税收 美国

Abstract

Political contributions generate access and influence for firms, but their public visibility exposes firms to reputational risk. We study how firms adjust their political spending when the reputational cost of political ties rises sharply. To identify this response, we exploit fatal school shootings and mass shootings as exogenous shocks to the salience of gun violence in the United States. We implement a staggered difference-in-differences design on a panel of corporate PAC contributions to U.S. House candidates, 2000–2024. We find that corporate PACs divest selectively and temporarily following gun violence shocks. Contributions to Republican candidates fall by 70.3% after fatal school shootings in competitive districts and return to baseline within nine months, with no substitution toward Democratic candidates. A parallel penalty of 60.9% emerges for Republicans in safe Democratic districts but is absent in safe Republican districts. Mass shootings generate similar perturbations in corporate contributions. These patterns indicate that firms withdraw only where the reputational risk of continued association is high or its strategic value is low. The observed divestment appears across industries and is stronger for firms with greater consumer exposure. Our findings reveal that firms treat political ties as time-varying liabilities, temporarily withdrawing visible financial support when issue salience spikes and resuming once public attention fades, leaving firms’ long-run political investment intact. Since public pressure is limited and dissipates quickly, and corporate divestment is transient rather than durably reallocated, the underlying financial incentives facing policymakers remain unchanged in the long run, sustaining the policy status quo.

中文摘要

政治献金为企业带来准入和影响力，但其公开性也使企业面临声誉风险。我们研究当政治关联的声誉成本急剧上升时，企业如何调整其政治支出。为识别这一反应，我们利用美国校园枪击案和大规模枪击案作为枪支暴力凸显度的外生冲击。我们在2000–2024年企业政治行动委员会（PAC）对美国众议院候选人捐款的面板数据上实施交错双重差分设计。我们发现，在枪支暴力冲击后，企业PAC会选择性且暂时性地撤资。在竞争性选区，致命校园枪击案后对共和党候选人的捐款下降70.3%，并在九个月内恢复至基线水平，且未出现向民主党候选人的替代性转移。在民主党安全选区，共和党候选人面临60.9%的平行惩罚，但在共和党安全选区则不存在。大规模枪击案在企业捐款中产生类似的扰动。这些模式表明，企业仅在持续关联的声誉风险较高或战略价值较低时撤资。观察到的撤资现象跨行业存在，且在消费者曝光度更高的企业中更为显著。我们的发现揭示，企业将政治关联视为随时间变化的负债，在议题凸显度飙升时暂时撤回显性财务支持，待公众注意力消退后恢复，从而保持企业的长期政治投资不变。由于公众压力有限且迅速消散，企业撤资是暂时性的而非持久性重新配置，因此政策制定者面临的潜在财务激励在长期内保持不变，从而维持了政策现状。
