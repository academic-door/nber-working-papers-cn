Platform Information Provision and Consumer Search: A Field Experiment

平台信息提供与消费者搜索：一项实地实验

Lu Fang, Yanyou Chen, Chiara Farronato, Zhe Yuan, Yitong Wang

其他主题 中国相关

Abstract

Despite substantial efforts to help consumers search in more intuitive ways, text search remains the predominant tool for product discovery online. In this paper, we explore the effects of visual and textual cues for search refinement on consumer search and purchasing behavior. We collaborate with one of the largest e-commerce platforms in China and study its roll out of a new search tool. When a customer searches for a general term (e.g., “headphones”), the tool suggests refined queries (e.g., “bluetooth headphones” or “noise-canceling headphones”) with the help of images and texts. The search tool was rolled out with a long-run experiment, which allows us to measure its short-run and long-run effects. We find that, although there was no immediate effect on orders or total expenditures, the search tool changed customers’ search and purchasing behavior in the long-run. Customers with access to the new tool eventually increased orders and expenditures compared to those in the control group, especially for non top-selling products. 中文摘要

尽管在帮助消费者以更直观的方式搜索方面做出了巨大努力，文本搜索仍然是线上产品发现的主要工具。在本文中，我们探讨了用于搜索细化的视觉和文本线索对消费者搜索和购买行为的影响。我们与中国最大的电子商务平台之一合作，研究了其新搜索工具的推出。当客户搜索一个通用术语（例如“耳机”）时，该工具借助图片和文本建议细化的查询（例如“蓝牙耳机”或“降噪耳机”）。该搜索工具通过一项长期实验推出，这使我们能够衡量其短期和长期影响。我们发现，尽管对订单或总支出没有立即影响，但该搜索工具在长期内改变了客户的搜索和购买行为。与对照组相比，使用新工具的客户最终增加了订单和支出，尤其是对于非畅销产品。购买量的增加源于更有效的搜索。
