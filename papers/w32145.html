Climate Change, Population Growth, and Population Pressure

气候变化、人口增长与人口压力

J. Vernon Henderson, Bo Yeon Jang, Adam Storeygard, David N. Weil

环境与健康 全球/多国

Abstract

We develop a novel method for assessing the effect of constraints imposed by spatially-fixed natural resources on aggregate economic output. We apply it to estimate and compare the projected effects of climate change and population growth over the course of the 21st century, by country and globally. We find that standard population growth projections imply larger reductions in income than even the most extreme widely-adopted climate change scenario (RCP8.5). Further, climate change and population growth will have their most damaging effects in similar places. Relative to previous work on macro climate impacts, our approach has the advantages of being disciplined by a simple macro growth model that allows for adaptation and of assessing impacts via a large set of climate moments, not just annual average temperature and precipitation. Our estimated effects of climate are by construction independent of country-level factors such as institutions.

中文摘要

我们开发了一种新方法，用于评估空间固定的自然资源约束对总体经济产出的影响。我们应用该方法，按国家和全球层面，估计并比较了21世纪气候变化和人口增长的预期影响。我们发现，标准的人口增长预测意味着收入减少幅度甚至超过最极端且被广泛采用的气候变化情景（RCP8.5）。此外，气候变化和人口增长将在相似的地方产生最具破坏性的影响。与以往关于宏观气候影响的研究相比，我们的方法具有以下优势：受一个允许适应的简单宏观增长模型的约束，并通过大量气候矩（而不仅仅是年平均温度和降水）来评估影响。我们估计的气候效应，按构建方式，独立于制度等国家层面因素。
