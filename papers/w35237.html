Unlocking Mortgage Lock-In: Equilibrium Effects in a Spatial Housing Ladder Model

解锁抵押贷款锁定效应：空间住房阶梯模型中的均衡效应

Julia Fonseca, Lu Liu, Pierre Mabille

理论与方法 美国

Abstract

Mortgage borrowers are "locked in": forgoing moves to hold on to low rates. Lock-in reduces both housing supply, through households who do not sell, and demand, through households who do not buy elsewhere, evidenced by a 40% drop in U.S. existing home sales between 2022 and 2024. We show that mortgage lock-in raises net housing demand: missing downsizers stay in larger homes, particularly in expensive areas, demanding more housing and offsetting a third of the aggregate house price decline caused by higher rates. Using individual-level mortgage data, we provide causal evidence that lock-in disproportionately reduces moves down the housing ladder. We design a spatial housing ladder model with long-term mortgages, which generates a distribution of locked-in rates and a causal effect on mobility consistent with the data, and use it to study the equilibrium effects of lock-in. A temporary rate hike causes lock-in, increasing aggregate house prices by 4.4% and rents by 1.5% relative to a counterfactual without lock-in, while mortgage borrowers' mobility falls by 25%. A $\$10$k tax credit to starter-home sellers modestly increases mobility but raises trade-up home prices. We estimate a cost of $\$650$k per marginal move, indicating that demand-based housing policies are poorly targeted responses to lock-in in our model.

中文摘要

抵押贷款借款人被“锁定”：放弃搬家以维持低利率。锁定效应通过不出售的住户减少住房供给，并通过不在其他地方购买的住户减少需求，这由2022年至2024年间美国现有房屋销售下降40%所证明。我们表明，抵押贷款锁定提高了净住房需求：未搬家的换小房者留在更大的房屋中，尤其是在昂贵地区，从而需求更多住房，抵消了因利率上升导致的总体房价下跌的三分之一。利用个人层面的抵押贷款数据，我们提供了因果证据，表明锁定效应不成比例地减少了沿住房阶梯向下的搬家。我们设计了一个包含长期抵押贷款的空间住房阶梯模型，该模型生成了锁定利率的分布以及对流动性的因果效应，与数据一致，并用于研究锁定效应的均衡效应。临时加息导致锁定效应，相对于无锁定的反事实，总体房价上涨4.4%，租金上涨1.5%，而抵押贷款借款人的流动性下降25%。向首次购房者卖家提供1万美元税收抵免，适度提高了流动性，但推高了换大房价格。我们估计每增加一次搬家的成本为65万美元，这表明在我们的模型中，基于需求的住房政策对锁定效应的应对目标不精准。
