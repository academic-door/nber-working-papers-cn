The Opioid Crisis, Health, Healthcare, and Crime: A Review Of Quasi-Experimental Economic Studies

阿片类药物危机、健康、医疗保健与犯罪：准实验经济学研究综述

Johanna Catherine Maclean, Justine Mallatt, Christopher J. Ruhm, Kosali I. Simon

环境与健康 美国

Abstract

Opioid use is one of the most substantial and long-lasting public health crises faced by the United States. This crisis, which began by the mid-1990s and continues through the time of writing, causes 136 fatal opioid overdoses each day and costs the U.S. at least $\$596$ billion each year. These numbers, while incredibly large, likely do not capture the full toll of the crisis on American society. In this study, we review quasi-experimental studies that examine the relationship between opioids and health and healthcare, and crime outcomes in the U.S. We focus on the U.S., a country particularly hard hit by the crisis which has adopted a broad array of policies aimed at curbing it. \nOur findings align with the general perception that the opioid crisis has negatively impacted a range of health outcomes and increased healthcare costs, with limited evidence that opioids (which are designed to reduce chronic pain) have enhanced work capacity or other metrics that might capture intended benefits from appropriate use of these medications. While opioids have worsened many health outcomes, the healthcare system played a role in the emergence of the epidemic and its continuation. Further, studies suggest that opioids increase crime, although the link is not as strong as has been observed in previous drug epidemics; this finding is consistent with the pharmacological difference between opioids and stimulant substances (e.g., cocaine) that dominated earlier drug epidemic periods characterized by higher levels of crime. Through the provision of treatment to address underlying addiction and the development of strategies to effectively curtail access to opioids, the healthcare system potentially has an important role in attempts to end the crisis.

中文摘要

阿片类药物使用是美国面临的最重大且持久的公共卫生危机之一。这场始于1990年代中期并持续至撰写本文时的危机，每天导致136例致命性阿片类药物过量，每年给美国造成至少5960亿美元的损失。这些数字虽然巨大，但可能并未涵盖这场危机对美国社会造成的全部损失。在本研究中，我们回顾了考察美国阿片类药物与健康、医疗保健以及犯罪结果之间关系的准实验研究。我们重点关注美国，这个受危机打击尤其严重的国家，它已采取了一系列旨在遏制危机的广泛政策。我们的发现与普遍看法一致，即阿片类药物危机对一系列健康结果产生了负面影响，并增加了医疗保健成本，而有限的证据表明阿片类药物（旨在减轻慢性疼痛）增强了工作能力或其他可能捕捉到适当使用这些药物预期益处的指标。虽然阿片类药物恶化了许多健康结果，但医疗保健系统在疫情的出现及其持续中发挥了作用。此外，研究表明阿片类药物增加了犯罪，尽管这种联系不如之前药物疫情中观察到的那么强；这一发现与阿片类药物和兴奋剂物质（例如可卡因）之间的药理学差异一致，后者在以前以较高犯罪率为特征的药物疫情时期占主导地位。通过提供治疗以解决潜在的成瘾问题，并制定有效限制阿片类药物获取的策略，医疗保健系统在试图结束这场危机中可能扮演重要角色。
