Money or Monitoring: Evidence on Improving Worker Effort

金钱还是监督：关于提高工人努力程度的证据

Jing Cai, Sai Luo, Shing-Yi Wang

国际贸易 中国相关

Abstract

Higher compensation and increased monitoring are two common strategies for addressing the moral hazard problem between firms and workers. In a field experiment with new hires at an automobile manufacturing firm in China, we randomly varied both signing bonuses and monitoring intensity. Both interventions increased worker output but through different channels: signing bonuses led to longer working hours without significant gains in performance, while enhanced monitoring improved performance as evaluated by managers. Additionally, bonuses reduced quit rates, whereas monitoring raised them. These results suggest that firms should carefully consider their primary objectives and weigh these trade-offs when designing optimal labor contracts.

中文摘要

提高薪酬和加强监督是解决企业与工人之间道德风险问题的两种常见策略。在一项针对中国某汽车制造企业新员工的实地实验中，我们随机改变了签约奖金和监督强度。两种干预措施都提高了工人产出，但通过不同的渠道：签约奖金导致工作时间延长，但绩效没有显著提高；而加强监督则提高了管理者评估的绩效。此外，奖金降低了离职率，而监督则提高了离职率。这些结果表明，企业在设计最优劳动合同时应仔细考虑其主要目标并权衡这些取舍。
