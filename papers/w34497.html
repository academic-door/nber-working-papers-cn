Internal vs. External Shocks in Weakening Democracies: Evidence on Migration and Foreign Investment

民主弱化中的内部冲击与外部冲击：来自移民和外国投资的证据

Assaf Razin

宏观与货币财政 未指明区域

Abstract

This paper investigates the consequences of regime change for both migration and foreign direct investment (FDI) by employing quasi-natural experiments that exploit external and internal shocks to democratic institutions. It compares evidence from Europe, which was afflicted by the “Syrian Shock”—an external institutional stress testing administrative and fiscal capacity—and Israel, which experienced the “Corruption Shock”—an internal credibility crisis that eroded judicial independence and policy predictability. These two shocks provide a natural experiment to examine how weakening democratic institutions influence both capital mobility and people mobility, using a unified econometric framework. \nThe analysis applies Difference-in-Differences (DiD) estimation to OECD panel data spanning 1995–2023 to isolate the causal effects of institutional deterioration on FDI inflows and migration flows. The DiD approach, complemented by fixed effects at the country and year levels, captures both the short-term disruptions caused by exogenous humanitarian pressures and the long-term persistence of governance-driven uncertainty. The results demonstrate that internal shocks—such as Israel’s judicial and corruption crises—generate large and durable declines in FDI and sustained outward migration, while external shocks—such as Europe’s refugee crisis—produce more transient effect.

中文摘要

本文通过利用准自然实验来研究政权更迭对移民和外国直接投资的后果，这些实验利用了民主制度的外部冲击和内部冲击。它比较了来自欧洲和以色列的证据，欧洲遭受了“叙利亚冲击”——一种对行政和财政能力进行压力测试的外部制度冲击，而以色列经历了“腐败冲击”——一种侵蚀司法独立性和政策可预测性的内部信誉危机。这两种冲击提供了一个自然实验，使用统一的经济计量框架来检验民主制度弱化如何影响资本流动和人员流动。该分析对1995年至2023年的经合组织面板数据应用了双重差分估计，以隔离制度恶化对外国直接投资流入和移民流动的因果效应。双重差分方法辅以国家和年份层面的固定效应，捕捉了外生人道主义压力造成的短期干扰以及治理驱动不确定性的长期持续性。结果表明，内部冲击——如以色列的司法和腐败危机——导致外国直接投资大幅且持久地下降以及持续的向外移民，而外部冲击——如欧洲的难民危机——产生的影响更为短暂。
