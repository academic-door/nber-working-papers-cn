Mandated and Voluntary Social Distancing During The COVID-19 Epidemic: A Review

COVID-19疫情期间的强制与自愿社交距离：综述

Sumedha Gupta, Kosali I. Simon, Coady Wing

环境与健康 美国

Abstract

For much of 2020, the COVID-19 epidemic upended social and economic life globally. In an effort to reduce COVID-19 risks in the U.S., state and local governments issued many recommendations and regulations to induce social distancing, adding to voluntary reductions in interpersonal contact. The responses to the epidemic helped contain spread, but also lead to high unintended societal costs. In the summer months, states took steps to revive the economy and lift social distancing regulations. However, as many epidemiologists expected, the scale of the epidemic has expanded very rapidly in the fall. In the week of October 14, the US generated around 57,000 new COVID-19 cases and 700 deaths each day. By November 15, the country was generating about 151,000 new cases and 1,200 deaths per day. These rapid increases in cases and deaths raise concerns about the capacity of local healthcare systems around the country. State governments are once again facing difficult choices about whether and how to use policies to address the spread of the virus. The incoming Biden-Harris administration faces an important challenge in trying to manage the epidemic as well as a large scale vaccination campaign. Although the epidemic is less than a year old, it has generated a huge volume of research by economists, epidemiologists, and others. This body of work may help inform policy decisions facing society in the coming months.\nIn this paper, we make five broad contributions. First, we provide a concise review of economic and social science research on mobility patterns, labor market outcomes, consumer behavior, and population health during the first phase of the epidemic. Second, we sketch a simple microeconomic model that may be useful considering the determinants of social distancing and the role of different policy instruments in promoting distancing. Third, we present a simple typology of the policies that were used at the state and county levels during the closure and re-opening phases of the epidemic in the U.S.. Fourth, we review a collection of new data sources that have played an important role in monitoring and analyzing population behavior this year. Fifth, we present results from event study regressions that try to disentangle private vs. policy-induced changes in mobility patterns during the early part of the epidemic.

中文摘要

在2020年的大部分时间里，COVID-19疫情颠覆了全球的社会和经济生活。为降低美国的COVID-19风险，州和地方政府发布了大量建议和规定以诱导社交距离，这加剧了人际接触的自愿减少。对疫情的反应有助于控制传播，但也导致了高昂的非预期社会成本。夏季，各州采取措施重振经济并解除社交距离规定。然而，正如许多流行病学家所预期，秋季疫情规模迅速扩大。截至10月14日当周，美国每天新增约57,000例COVID-19病例和700例死亡。到11月15日，该国每天新增约151,000例病例和1,200例死亡。病例和死亡的快速增加引发了对全国各地地方医疗系统能力的担忧。州政府再次面临艰难选择，即是否以及如何使用政策来应对病毒传播。即将上任的拜登-哈里斯政府在管理疫情以及大规模疫苗接种运动方面面临重大挑战。尽管疫情发生不到一年，但已产生了经济学家、流行病学家和其他学者的大量研究。这些工作可能有助于为未来几个月社会面临的政策决策提供信息。本文做出五项主要贡献。第一，我们简要回顾了疫情第一阶段关于流动性模式、劳动力市场结果、消费者行为和人口健康的经济学和社会科学研究。第二，我们勾勒了一个简单的微观经济模型，该模型可能有助于考虑社交距离的决定因素以及不同政策工具在促进社交距离中的作用。第三，我们提出了一个简单的政策类型学，涵盖了美国疫情关闭和重新开放阶段在州和县层面使用的政策。第四，我们回顾了一系列新的数据来源，这些来源在今年监测和分析人口行为方面发挥了重要作用。第五，我们展示了事件研究回归的结果，试图在疫情早期区分流动性模式变化的私人因素与政策诱导因素。
