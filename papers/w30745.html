The Cost of Imbalance in Clinical Trials

临床试验中不平衡的成本

Sylvain Chassang, Rong Feng

理论与方法 未指明区域

Abstract

Clinical trials following the “gold standard” of random assignment frequently use independent lotteries to allocate patients to treatment and control arms. However, independent assignment can generate treatment and control arms that are unbalanced (i.e. treatment and control populations with significantly different demographics), which reduces power. Other assignment methods such as matched pair designs ensure balance across arms while maintaining randomization and permitting inference. \nThis paper seeks to measure the cost of imbalance with respect to gender in a sample of roughly 2000 clinical studies. We document significant imbalance: 25% of experiments have at least 26% more men in one treatment arm than in the other. In addition, clinical trials with greater imbalance have more dispersed treatment effects, indicating that imbalance reduces the informativeness of experiments. A simple structural model suggests that for a typical experiment, using a balanced random design could deliver informativeness gains equivalent to increasing the sample size by 18%.

中文摘要

遵循随机分配“金标准”的临床试验通常使用独立抽签将患者分配到治疗组和对照组。然而，独立分配可能导致治疗组和对照组不平衡（即治疗组和对照组人群的人口统计学特征显著不同），这会降低统计功效。其他分配方法，如配对设计，可以在保持随机化并允许推断的同时确保各组间的平衡。本文旨在衡量约2000项临床研究样本中性别不平衡的成本。我们记录了显著的不平衡：25%的实验中，一个治疗组的男性比例比另一个治疗组至少高出26%。此外，不平衡程度更大的临床试验具有更分散的治疗效果，表明不平衡降低了实验的信息量。一个简单的结构模型表明，对于一个典型的实验，使用平衡的随机设计可以带来相当于将样本量增加18%的信息量增益。
