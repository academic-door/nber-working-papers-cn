Patent Hunters

专利猎手

Lauren Cohen, Umit Gurun, Katie Moon, Paula Suh

环境与健康 未指明区域

Abstract

Analyzing millions of patents granted by the USPTO between 1976 and 2020, we find a pattern where specific patents only rise to prominence after considerable time has passed. Amongst these late-blooming influential patents, we show that there are key players (patent hunters) who consistently identify and develop them. Although initially overlooked, these late-blooming patents have significantly more influence on average than early-recognized patents and are associated with significantly more new product launches. Patent hunters, as early detectors and adopters of these late-blooming patents, are also associated with significant positive rents. Their adoption of these overlooked patents is associated with a 6.4% rise in sales growth (t = 3.02), a 2.2% increase in Tobin’s Q (t = 3.91), and a 2.2% increase in new product offerings (t = 2.97). We instrument for patent hunting, and find strong evidence that these benefits are causally due to patent hunting. The rents associated with patent hunting on average exceed those of the original patent creators themselves. Patents hunted are closer to the core technology of patent hunters, more peripheral to writers, and in less competitive spaces. Lastly, patent hunting appears to be a persistent firm characteristic and to have an inventor-level component.

中文摘要

通过分析美国专利商标局在1976年至2020年间授予的数百万项专利，我们发现了一种模式，即特定专利只有在相当长的时间过去后才变得突出。在这些大器晚成的影响力专利中，我们展示了存在关键参与者（专利猎手），他们持续识别并开发这些专利。尽管最初被忽视，但这些大器晚成的专利平均而言比早期被认可的专利具有显著更大的影响力，并且与显著更多的新产品发布相关。专利猎手作为这些大器晚成专利的早期发现者和采用者，也与显著的正租金相关。他们采用这些被忽视的专利与销售额增长6.4%（t=3.02）、托宾Q值增加2.2%（t=3.91）以及新产品供应增加2.2%（t=2.97）相关。我们为专利猎手行为寻找工具变量，并发现强有力的证据表明这些收益是由专利猎手行为因果导致的。与专利猎手相关的租金平均而言超过了原始专利创造者自身的租金。被猎取的专利更接近专利猎手的核心技术，对原始撰写者而言更边缘化，并且处于竞争较少的领域。最后，专利猎手似乎是一个持久的公司特征，并且具有发明人层面的组成部分。
