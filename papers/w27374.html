Socioeconomic Network Heterogeneity and Pandemic Policy Response

社会经济网络异质性与大流行政策应对

Mohammad Akbarpour, Cody Cook, Aude Marzuoli, Simon Mongey, Abhishek Nagaraj, Matteo Saccarola, Pietro Tebaldi, Shoshana Vasserman, Hanbin Yang

环境与健康 未指明区域

Abstract

We develop a heterogeneous-agents network-based model to analyze alternative policies during a pandemic outbreak, accounting for health and economic trade-offs within the same empirical framework. We leverage a variety of data sources, including data on individuals' mobility and encounters across metropolitan areas, health records, and measures of the possibility to be productively working from home. This combination of data sources allows us to build a framework in which the severity of a disease outbreak varies across locations and industries, and across individuals who differ by age, occupation, and preexisting health conditions. \nWe use this framework to analyze the impact of different social distancing policies in the context of the COVID-19 outbreaks across US metropolitan areas. Our results highlight how outcomes vary across areas in relation to the underlying heterogeneity in population density, social network structures, population health, and employment characteristics. We find that policies by which individuals who can work from home continue to do so, or in which schools and firms alternate schedules across different groups of students and employees, can be effective in limiting the health and healthcare costs of the pandemic outbreak while also reducing employment losses.

中文摘要

我们开发了一个基于异质性主体网络模型，用于分析大流行爆发期间的不同政策选择，并在同一实证框架内权衡健康与经济。我们利用了多种数据来源，包括个人在大都市区域内的流动与接触数据、健康记录，以及在家有效工作的可能性指标。这些数据源的结合使我们能够构建一个框架，其中疾病爆发的严重程度因地点、行业以及年龄、职业和既有健康状况不同的个体而异。我们利用该框架，在美国各都市区COVID-19爆发的背景下，分析了不同社交距离政策的影响。我们的结果突显了结果如何因地区而异，这与人口密度、社交网络结构、人口健康和就业特征的潜在异质性相关。我们发现，那些允许可在家工作的人继续在家工作，或者学校和企业在不同学生和员工群体间交替安排的政策，能够有效限制大流行爆发的健康与医疗成本，同时减少就业损失。
