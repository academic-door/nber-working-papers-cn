A Child Support Framework Accounting for Parenting Time and Half-Siblings

考虑探视时间和同父异母/同母异父兄弟姐妹的子女抚养费框架

Martin Kulldorff, Jay Bhattacharya

其他主题 美国

Abstract

In the United States, child support guidelines sometimes generate surprising and presumably unintentional child support amounts, especially in situations with extended visitation, shared parenting, and half-siblings. These are consequences of the ad-hoc mathematical formulas that are in common use to account for such situations. This paper provides ten such surprising examples from ten randomly selected states.\nA child support calculation framework is constructed that takes as inputs the subjective/normative decisions that the public and legislators must make regarding children's expenditures, the progressivity of the contributions between parents, and other matters. Our goal is to derive mathematical formulas for child support amounts that achieve those normative goals while satisfying basic desiderata such as supporting children in both of their families, equity between siblings, neutral medical decisions, and not requiring higher contributions from parents with a lower salary.

中文摘要

在美国，子女抚养费指南有时会产生令人惊讶且可能非故意的子女抚养费金额，尤其是在涉及延长探视、共同抚养和同父异母/同母异父兄弟姐妹的情况下。这些是用于处理此类情况的常见临时数学公式的后果。本文提供了来自随机选择的十个州的十个此类令人惊讶的例子。我们构建了一个子女抚养费计算框架，该框架将公众和立法者必须就子女支出、父母之间贡献的累进性以及其他事项做出的主观/规范性决策作为输入。我们的目标是推导出子女抚养费金额的数学公式，这些公式能够实现这些规范性目标，同时满足基本要求，例如支持子女在两个家庭中的生活、兄弟姐妹之间的公平、中立的医疗决策，以及不要求收入较低的父母做出更高贡献。
