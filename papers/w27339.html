An Event Study of COVID-19 Central Bank Quantitative Easing in Advanced and Emerging Economies

COVID-19时期发达与新兴经济体央行量化宽松的事件研究

Jonathan S. Hartley, Alessandro Rebucci, Daniel Jiménez

宏观与货币财政 全球/多国

Abstract

This paper conducts an event study of 30 quantitative easing (QE) announcements made by 21 central banks on daily government bond yields and bilateral US dollar exchange rates in March and April 2020, in the midst of the global financial turmoil triggered by the COVID-19 outbreak. The chapter also investigates the transmission of innovations to long-term interest rates in a standard GVAR model estimated with quarterly pre-COVID-19 data. The authors find that QE has not lost effectiveness in advanced economies and that its international transmission is consistent with the working of long-run uncovered interest rate parity and a large dollar shortage shock during the COVID-19 period. In emerging markets, the QE impact on bond yields is much stronger and its transmission to exchange rates is qualitatively different than in advanced economies. The GVAR evidence that the authors report illustrates the Fed’s pivotal role in the global transmission of long-term interest rate shocks, but also the ample scope for country-specific interventions to affect local financial market conditions, even after controlling for common factors and spillovers from other countries. The GVAR evidence also shows that QE interventions can have sizable real effects on output driven by a very persistent impact on long-term interest rates.

中文摘要

本文对2020年3月和4月（即COVID-19疫情引发的全球金融动荡期间）21家央行发布的30次量化宽松公告进行了事件研究，考察其对每日政府债券收益率和美元双边汇率的影响。本章还利用COVID-19前的季度数据，在一个标准全球向量自回归模型中研究了长期利率创新的传导机制。作者发现，量化宽松在发达经济体中并未失效，其国际传导与长期无抛补利率平价的作用以及COVID-19期间大规模美元短缺冲击相一致。在新兴市场，量化宽松对债券收益率的影响更强，且其对汇率的传导在性质上与发达经济体不同。作者报告的全球向量自回归证据表明，美联储在全球长期利率冲击传导中发挥关键作用，但即使在控制了共同因素和其他国家的溢出效应后，各国特定干预措施仍有很大空间影响本地金融市场状况。全球向量自回归证据还显示，量化宽松干预措施可以通过对长期利率的持续影响，对产出产生显著的实际效应。
