A Supply Curve for Forest-Based CO₂ Removal

基于森林的二氧化碳清除供给曲线

Sergio L. Franklin Jr., Robert S. Pindyck

其他主题 未指明区域

Abstract

Forestation is viewed as an important means of removing CO₂ from the atmosphere and thereby reducing net CO₂ emissions. But how much CO₂ can be removed, and at what cost? Focusing on forested and forestable areas in South America, and using spatially disaggregated data, we estimate a supply curve for forest-based atmospheric CO₂ removal. The supply curve traces out the marginal cost of removing a metric ton of CO₂ as a function of total annual CO₂ removal. Each point on the curve corresponds to a specific location, and accounts for land opportunity costs as well as costs of tree planting and maintenance. We show that over a billion tons of CO₂ can be removed annually via forestation at a cost below $\$45$ per ton, and about 2.5 billion tons can be removed at a cost below $\$90$ per ton. The supply curve applies to only South America, but with sufficient data could be extended to the entire world.

中文摘要

植树造林被视为从大气中清除二氧化碳从而减少净二氧化碳排放的重要手段。但可以清除多少二氧化碳，成本又是多少？我们聚焦于南美洲的森林和可造林区域，利用空间分解数据，估计了一条基于森林的大气二氧化碳清除供给曲线。该供给曲线描绘了清除一公吨二氧化碳的边际成本，作为年总二氧化碳清除量的函数。曲线上的每一点对应一个特定地点，并考虑了土地机会成本以及植树和养护成本。我们表明，每年可以通过植树造林以低于每吨45美元的成本清除超过十亿吨二氧化碳，而以低于每吨90美元的成本可以清除约25亿吨。该供给曲线仅适用于南美洲，但如果有足够的数据，可以扩展到整个世界。
