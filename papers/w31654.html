The Importance of Sound Monetary Policy: Some Lessons for Today from Canada’s Experience with Floating Exchange Rates Since 1950

稳健货币政策的重要性：从加拿大自1950年以来浮动汇率经验中汲取的今日教训

Michael D. Bordo, Pierre Siklos

宏观与货币财政 加拿大

Abstract

In this paper we revisit the Canadian experience with floating exchange rates since 1950. Canada was a pioneer in successfully adopting a floating exchange rate during the Bretton Woods pegged exchange rate regime. Since then, most advanced countries have followed the Canadian example.\nA key finding of our paper based on historical narrative and econometric analysis is that economic performance under floating depended on its monetary policy performance as Milton Friedman originally argued in his seminal 1953 article. Canadian monetary policy achieved low and stable inflation once it adopted inflation targeting as a nominal anchor. Also, as Friedman argued, Canada’s floating exchange rate provided it with a modicum of insulation from external shocks, especially commodity price shocks that influenced both the level and volatility of the real exchange rate over the past three decades. The Canadian experience with floating (along with that of other small open economies such as Australia, New Zealand and Sweden) and inflation targeting became a model for the conduct of monetary policy in emerging countries.

中文摘要

在本文中，我们重新审视了加拿大自1950年以来的浮动汇率经验。加拿大是在布雷顿森林体系固定汇率制度下成功采用浮动汇率的先驱。此后，大多数发达国家都效仿了加拿大的做法。基于历史叙述和计量经济学分析，我们论文的一个关键发现是，正如米尔顿·弗里德曼在其1953年的开创性文章中所论证的，浮动汇率下的经济表现取决于其货币政策表现。一旦加拿大采用通胀目标作为名义锚，其货币政策就实现了低且稳定的通胀。此外，正如弗里德曼所论证的，加拿大的浮动汇率为其提供了一定程度的外部冲击隔离，特别是影响了过去三十年实际汇率水平和波动性的商品价格冲击。加拿大的浮动汇率经验（以及澳大利亚、新西兰和瑞典等其他小型开放经济体的经验）和通胀目标制成为了新兴国家货币政策操作的典范。
