NBER Working Paper w31864所在周报所在月度合集

Financial Windfalls, Portfolio Allocations, and Risk Preferences

意外之财、资产配置与风险偏好

Joseph S. Briggs, David Cesarini, Sean Chanwook Lee, Erik Lindqvist, Robert Östling

理论与方法 未指明区域

Abstract

We investigate the impact of financial windfalls on household portfolio choices and risk exposure. Exploiting the randomized assignment of lottery prizes in three Swedish lotteries, we find a windfall gain of $\$100$K leads to a 5-percentage-point decrease in the risky share of household portfolios. We show theoretically that negative wealth effects are consistent with both constant and decreasing relative risk aversion and analyze how our empirical estimates help distinguish between competing models of portfolio choice. We further show our results are quantitatively aligned with the predictions of a calibrated dynamic portfolio choice model with nontradable human capital and consumption habits.

中文摘要

我们研究了意外之财对家庭投资组合选择和风险敞口的影响。利用瑞典三种彩票中奖的随机分配，我们发现10万美元的意外之财导致家庭投资组合中风险资产份额下降5个百分点。我们从理论上证明，负财富效应与常数相对风险厌恶和递减相对风险厌恶都是一致的，并分析了我们的实证估计如何帮助区分竞争性的投资组合选择模型。我们进一步表明，我们的结果与一个包含不可交易人力资本和消费习惯的校准动态投资组合选择模型的预测在数量上一致。
