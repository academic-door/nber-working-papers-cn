No-Poach Agreements

互不挖角协议

Axel Gottfries, Gregor Jarosch

教育与劳动 未指明区域

Abstract

We study no-poach cartels using a wage-posting model that puts worker poaching at the center of labor market competition. Even when just two out of ten employers in a local labor market collude, wages fall by almost 5% market-wide. The reason is that no-poach agreements suppress competition along the job ladder, with spillovers to non-colluding firms. We then compare no-poach agreements to two other forms of anti-competitive conduct in the labor market: noncompete agreements and wage fixing. No-poach agreements have by far the largest negative wage effects.

中文摘要

我们利用一个将员工挖角置于劳动力市场竞争核心的工资报价模型来研究互不挖角卡特尔。即使当地劳动力市场中十家雇主中只有两家合谋，市场范围内的工资也会下降近5%。原因是互不挖角协议抑制了职业阶梯上的竞争，并产生向非合谋企业的溢出效应。然后，我们将互不挖角协议与劳动力市场中另外两种反竞争行为进行比较：竞业限制协议和固定工资协议。互不挖角协议对工资的负面效应迄今为止最大。
