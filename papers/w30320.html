Do Funding Agencies Select and Enable Risky Research: Evidence from ERC Using Novelty as a Proxy of Risk Taking

资助机构是否选择并支持高风险研究：以新颖性作为风险承担代理变量的欧洲研究理事会证据

Reinhilde Veugelers, Jian Wang, Paula Stephan

其他主题 未指明区域

Abstract

Concern exists that public funding of science is increasingly risk averse. Funders have addressed this concern by soliciting the submission of high-risk research to either regular or specially designed programs. Little evidence, however, has been gathered to examine the extent to which such programs and initiatives accomplish their stated goal. This paper sets out to study this using data from the European Research Council (ERC), a program within the EC, established in 2007 to support high-risk/high-gain research. We examine whether the ERC selected researchers with a track record of conducting risky research. We proxy high-risk by a measure of novelty in the publication records of applicants both before and after the application, recognizing that it is but one dimension of risk. We control and interact the risk measure with high-gain by tracking whether the applicant has one or more top 1% highly cited papers in their field. We find that applicants with a history of risky research are less likely to be selected for funding than those without such a history, especially early career applicants. This selection penalty for high-risk also holds among those applicants with a history of high-gain publications. To test whether receiving a long and generous prestigious ERC grant promotes risk taking, we employ a diff-in-diff approach. We find no evidence of a significant positive risk treatment effect for advanced grantees. Only for early career grantees do we find that recipients are more likely to engage in risky research, but only compared to applicants who are unsuccessful at the second stage. This positive treatment effect is in part due to unsuccessful applicants cutting back on risky research. We cautiously interpret this as a “lesson learned” that risk is not rewarded.

中文摘要

人们担忧公共科学资助日益规避风险。资助者通过征集高风险研究提交至常规或专门设计的项目来应对这一担忧。然而，很少有证据检验这些项目和举措在多大程度上实现了其既定目标。本文利用欧洲研究理事会（ERC）的数据进行研究，ERC是欧盟委员会于2007年设立的一个项目，旨在支持高风险/高回报研究。我们考察ERC是否选择了有从事高风险研究记录的申请者。我们以申请者申请前后发表记录中的新颖性度量作为高风险的代理变量，同时认识到这只是风险的一个维度。我们通过追踪申请者是否在其领域内拥有一篇或多篇前1%高被引论文，来控制风险度量并与高回报进行交互分析。我们发现，有高风险研究历史的申请者比没有此类历史的申请者更不可能获得资助，尤其是早期职业申请者。这种对高风险的选拔惩罚同样存在于有高回报发表历史的申请者中。为了检验获得长期且慷慨的著名ERC资助是否促进风险承担，我们采用了双重差分方法。我们没有发现对高级受资助者存在显著正向风险处理效应的证据。仅在早期职业受资助者中，我们发现受资助者更可能从事高风险研究，但这仅与在第二阶段未成功的申请者相比。这种正向处理效应部分源于未成功申请者减少了高风险研究。我们谨慎地将此解释为“经验教训”，即风险并未得到回报。
