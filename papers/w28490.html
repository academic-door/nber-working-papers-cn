Do Required Minimum Distribution 401(k) Rules Matter, and For Whom? Insights from a Lifecycle Model

最低提款401(k)规则是否重要，对谁重要？来自生命周期模型的见解

Vanya Horneff, Raimond Maurer, Olivia S. Mitchell

理论与方法 美国

Abstract

Tax-qualified vehicles helped U.S. private-sector workers accumulate $\$25$Tr in retirement assets. An often-overlooked important institutional feature shaping decumulations from these retirement plans is the “Required Minimum Distribution” (RMD) regulation, requiring retirees to withdraw a minimum fraction from their retirement accounts or pay excise taxes on withdrawal shortfalls. Our calibrated lifecycle model measures the impact of RMD rules on financial behavior of heterogeneous households during their worklives and retirement. We show that proposed reforms to delay or eliminate the RMD rules should have little effects on consumption profiles but more impact on withdrawals and tax payments for households with bequest motives.

中文摘要

税收合格工具帮助美国私营部门工人积累了25万亿美元的退休资产。一个常被忽视但重要的制度特征塑造了这些退休计划的资产提取，即“最低提款”规定，要求退休人员从其退休账户中提取最低比例，否则需对提款不足部分缴纳消费税。我们校准的生命周期模型衡量了最低提款规则对异质性家庭在工作生涯和退休期间财务行为的影响。我们表明，提议的延迟或取消最低提款规则的改革对消费概况影响很小，但对有遗赠动机的家庭的提款和税收支付影响更大。
