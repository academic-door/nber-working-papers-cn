The Effects of Social Insurance Benefits on Leaving Employment at Older Ages in the Netherlands

荷兰社会保障福利对老年离职的影响

Adriaan Kalwij, Arie Kapteyn

金融与资产定价 未指明区域

Abstract

In the Netherlands, from 1989 to 2013, in the age group 55-63 the annual exit rate from employment to receiving social insurance benefits in the following year decreased from around 17 percent to 7 percent for men, and from 14 percent to 5 percent for women. We found that less generous social insurance benefits have had small but significant negative effects on these exit rates: The annual exit rate to social insurance benefit receipt next year (at ages 56-64) would have been about 14 percent higher for both men and women in 2013 should social insurance benefits schemes of 1989 still have been in place. This increase amounts to staying, on average, three months longer in employment from age 55 onwards in 2013 than in 1989. These findings are driven to some extent by the reduction in the maximum duration of unemployment insurance benefits in 2007, but predominantly by making (early) retirement schemes actuarially fair from 2006 onwards. The increase in disability insurance’s income replacement rate in 2006 has led to a slight increase in the exit rate from employment, conditional on eligibility. As the estimated effects of changes in the social insurance benefits from 1989 to 2013 on working beyond age 55 are relatively small, they suggest the importance of other factors such as changes in workers’ skills, improved health (on which we provide some evidence), and social insurance’s tighter eligibility criteria.

中文摘要

在荷兰，从1989年到2013年，在55-63岁年龄组中，男性从就业状态转为在下一年领取社会保障福利的年退出率从约17%下降到7%，女性从14%下降到5%。我们发现，较不慷慨的社会保障福利对这些退出率产生了虽小但显著的负面影响：如果1989年的社会保障福利计划仍然存在，2013年男性和女性在56-64岁期间下一年转为领取社会保障福利的年退出率将高出约14%。这一增长相当于在2013年，从55岁起平均比1989年多工作三个月。这些发现在一定程度上是由2007年失业保险福利最长期限的缩短所驱动的，但主要是由2006年起使（提前）退休计划在精算上公平所驱动的。2006年残疾保险收入替代率的提高，导致在符合条件的情况下，就业退出率略有上升。由于估计的1989年至2013年社会保障福利变化对55岁以上就业的影响相对较小，这表明其他因素的重要性，例如工人技能的变化、健康状况的改善（我们对此提供了一些证据）以及社会保障更严格的资格标准。
