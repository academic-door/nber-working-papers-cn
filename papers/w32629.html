Social Comparisons and Adolescent Body Misperception: Evidence from School Entry Cutoffs

社会比较与青少年身体认知偏差：来自入学截止日期的证据

Christopher S. Carpenter, Brandyn F. Churchill

教育与劳动 未指明区域

Abstract

We provide novel evidence on the role of social comparisons in shaping adolescent body misperception. Using an instrumental variables approach leveraging variation in relative age generated by school entry cutoff months and data from the Health Behaviour in School-Aged Children study, we show that relatively older students are more likely to misperceive their weight harshly relative to their BMIs compared to their same-age counterparts who are relatively younger within their classrooms. Meanwhile, relatively younger students are more likely to misperceive their weight leniently relative to their BMIs. We then show that relatively older students are less likely to be overweight or obese, consume more low-calorie foods, and report higher levels of physical activity. Overall, our results suggest that relatively older students base their weight-related expectations and behaviors on their younger peers, while relatively younger students compare themselves to their older peers.

中文摘要

我们提供了关于社会比较在塑造青少年身体认知偏差中作用的新证据。利用基于入学截止月份产生的相对年龄差异作为工具变量，并结合“学龄儿童健康行为”研究的数据，我们表明，与同班级中相对较年轻的同龄人相比，相对年长的学生更可能相对于其身体质量指数（BMI）严厉地误判自己的体重。与此同时，相对年轻的学生更可能相对于其BMI宽松地误判自己的体重。我们还发现，相对年长的学生超重或肥胖的可能性更低，摄入更多低热量食物，并报告更高水平的身体活动。总体而言，我们的结果表明，相对年长的学生基于其较年轻的同龄人来设定与体重相关的期望和行为，而相对年轻的学生则与较年长的同龄人进行比较。
