The Social and Statistical Construction of Race

种族的社会建构与统计建构

Anjali Adukia, Richard Hornbeck, Daniel Keniston, Benjamin Lualdi

其他主题 美国

Abstract

We examine the social construction of race in the United States, from Reconstruction to the modern day, and show how this affects statistical differences across and within racial categories. During the Reconstruction era, people with the same light brown skin tones, recorded by the Freedman’s Bank (1865-1874), were more likely racialized as White or Mulatto in the 1870 Census if they were wealthier or literate. Racial assignment of descendants remained fluid through the 1940 Census. In modern data, self-reported race also varies with income and college completion among people with light brown skin tones. This endogenous racial assignment increases cross-race wealth gaps, which we show can be adjusted using sub-samples with data on skin tone or ancestry. Endogenous race also induces selection when controlling for race that, for example, attenuates the relationship between wealth and skin tone.\n

中文摘要

我们考察了美国从重建时期至今种族的社会建构，并展示了这如何影响种族类别内部和之间的统计差异。在重建时期，根据自由民储蓄银行（1865-1874年）的记录，具有相同浅棕色肤色的人，如果更富有或更有文化，在1870年人口普查中更有可能被归类为白人或混血儿。其后代的种族归属直到1940年人口普查仍具有流动性。在现代数据中，在浅棕色肤色人群中，自我报告的种族也随收入和大学完成情况而变化。这种内生的种族归属增加了跨种族的财富差距，我们表明可以使用具有肤色或血统数据的子样本进行调整。当控制种族时，内生种族也会引起选择偏差，例如，这会削弱财富与肤色之间的关系。
