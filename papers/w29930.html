Intimate Partner Violence and Income: Quasi-Experimental Evidence from the Earned Income Tax Credit

亲密伴侣暴力与收入：来自劳动所得税抵免的准实验证据

Resul Cesur, Núria Rodriguez-Planas, Jennifer Roff, David Simon

公共财政与税收 未指明区域

Abstract

We estimate the impact of an exogenous increase in income on the prevalence and counts of intimate partner violence (IPV). Using the National Crime Victimization Survey data from 1992 to 2000, we exploit time and family-size variation in the Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC) by comparing IPV victimization of women with one or more children (our “treated” group) to that of women with no children (our comparison group) before and after OBRA-93. The OBRA-93 expansion reduces both reports of any physical or sexual assault and counts of physical or sexual assaults per 100 women surveyed, with the effects being strongest for those groups more likely to both experience IPV and be eligible for EITC: unmarried women and unmarried Black women. If increased income (rather than changes in employment) is the only channel by which the EITC decreases IPV, an additional $\$1,000$ of after-tax income decreases the prevalence of physical or sexual violence of unmarried low-educated women by 9.73% and the counts of physical or sexual violence by 21%. We explore potential mechanisms behind these findings.

中文摘要

我们估计了外生收入增加对亲密伴侣暴力（IPV）发生率和数量的影响。利用1992年至2000年的全国犯罪受害调查数据，我们通过比较OBRA-93前后有一个或多个孩子的女性（我们的“处理”组）与没有孩子的女性（我们的对照组）的IPV受害情况，来利用劳动所得税抵免（EITC）在时间和家庭规模上的变化。OBRA-93的扩张减少了每100名受访女性中报告的任何身体或性侵犯以及身体或性侵犯的次数，这种效应对于那些更可能经历IPV且更有资格获得EITC的群体最为强烈：未婚女性和未婚黑人女性。如果增加收入（而非就业变化）是EITC减少IPV的唯一渠道，那么额外1000美元的税后收入会使未婚低学历女性遭受身体或性暴力的发生率降低9.73%，身体或性暴力的次数降低21%。我们探讨了这些发现背后的潜在机制。
