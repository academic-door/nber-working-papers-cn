NBER Working Paper w35092所在周报所在月度合集

The Medical Expansion, Life Expectancy, and Endogenous Directed Technical Change

医疗扩张、预期寿命与内生定向技术变革

Leon Huetsch, Dirk Krueger, Alexander Ludwig

环境与健康 未指明区域

Abstract

We build a unified quantitative theory of increasing adult life expectancy and income growth in the last two centuries, and the emergence of a modern health sector in the 20th century. We interpret the data as three phases of a dynamic equilibrium in which households are initially poor, the price of health goods is prohibitively high, and life expectancy is stagnant. As technological progress fuels income growth, households commence consuming basic health goods and life expectancy rises in the first half of the 19th century. 100 years later, further directed technological progress leads to the emergence of a modern health sector. \nThrough the lens of the model, the quality-adjusted relative price of modern health goods declined by about 2.5% per year between 1940 and 2020 while the model-implied relative price that lacks quality adjustment increases in line with the BEA health price index. Counterfactual analyses suggest that almost one fourth of adult life expectancy gains between 1940 and 2020 are attributed to the emergence and expansion of modern health and that public spending on health R&D during World War II played an important role in the kickoff of the modern health sector.

中文摘要

我们构建了一个统一的量化理论，用以解释过去两个世纪中成人预期寿命和收入增长的同时增加，以及20世纪现代健康部门的出现。我们将数据解释为动态均衡的三个阶段：最初家庭贫困，健康商品价格高得令人望而却步，预期寿命停滞不前。随着技术进步推动收入增长，家庭在19世纪上半叶开始消费基本健康商品，预期寿命上升。100年后，进一步的定向技术进步导致了现代健康部门的出现。根据该模型的视角，1940年至2020年间，经质量调整的现代健康商品相对价格每年下降约2.5%，而模型隐含的未经质量调整的相对价格则与BEA健康价格指数同步上升。反事实分析表明，1940年至2020年间成人预期寿命的增长中，近四分之一归因于现代健康的出现和扩张，而二战期间公共健康研发支出在现代健康部门的启动中发挥了重要作用。
