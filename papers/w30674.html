Does War Foster Cooperation or Parochialism? Evidence from a Natural Experiment among Turkish Conscripts

战争促进合作还是狭隘主义？来自土耳其应征入伍者自然实验的证据

Arzu Kibris, Resul Cesur

其他主题 未指明区域

Abstract

Exploiting a natural experiment and an innovative survey design, we study the causal impact of armed conflict exposure (ACE) on the sociopolitical attitudes and behaviors of the average male randomly picked from the population. Contrary to the arguments that war fosters cooperation, we find little evidence of social or political participation in exposed individuals. Instead, we document compelling evidence that ACE promotes opposition to peaceful means of conflict resolution, animosity towards minorities, and adherence to right-wing ideology. Further analyses show war-driven grievances, the normalization of violence in everyday life, and changes in conservative norms and preferences as the transmitting pathways.

中文摘要

利用一项自然实验和创新的调查设计，我们研究了武装冲突暴露（ACE）对从人口中随机挑选的普通男性的社会政治态度和行为的因果影响。与战争促进合作的观点相反，我们发现暴露个体在社会或政治参与方面几乎没有证据。相反，我们记录了令人信服的证据，表明ACE促进了反对和平解决冲突的方式、对少数群体的敌意以及对右翼意识形态的坚持。进一步分析表明，战争驱动的怨恨、暴力在日常生活中的正常化以及保守规范和偏好的变化是传导途径。
