Are Government Bonds Safe in Times of War and Pandemic?

政府债券在战争和疫情期间是否安全？

Zhengyang Jiang, Hanno Lustig, Stijn Van Nieuwerburgh, Mindy Z. Xiaolan

宏观与货币财政 美国

Abstract

We analyze real returns on U.S. and U.K. government debt during major wars and the COVID-19 pandemic over the past three centuries. Wars are associated with sharply negative real returns on outstanding government debt, with returns falling far below economic growth, in contrast to peacetime periods when returns exceed growth. Elevated surprise inflation and financial repression account for a cumulative 30% wedge between returns and growth over four years of war, implying that bondholders bear a substantial share of wartime fiscal costs. During wartime, government bonds also systematically underperform risky assets.

中文摘要

我们分析了在过去三个世纪的主要战争和COVID-19疫情期间，美国和英国政府债务的实际回报率。战争与未偿政府债务的急剧负实际回报率相关，回报率远低于经济增长，这与和平时期回报率超过增长的情况形成对比。在四年的战争期间，高企的意外通胀和金融抑制共同导致了回报率与增长率之间累计30%的差距，这意味着债券持有人承担了战时财政成本的很大一部分。在战争期间，政府债券的表现也系统性逊于风险资产。
