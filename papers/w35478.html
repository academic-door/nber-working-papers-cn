Better Together? The Impact of Cash Transfers and Couples' Financial Planning on Household Dynamics and Economic Outcomes

携手共进？现金转移与夫妻财务规划对家庭动态和经济结果的影响

Sarika Gupta, Jessica Leight, Daniel W. Maggio, Simone G. Schaner

其他主题 利比里亚

Abstract

Households' most consequential economic decisions are usually made jointly, yet behavioral interventions are typically targeted to individuals and optimized to overcome intra-personal constraints. We use a randomized controlled trial to study whether behavioral interventions targeted to couples can overcome interpersonal constraints related to intra-household decision-making. In rural Liberia, we layer a facilitated joint financial planning exercise atop a large unconditional cash transfer, assessing effects relative to cash only and a pure control. Relative to cash only, adding planning increases the amount of the transfer spent on productive investment and consequently improves economic outcomes by 0.16 standard deviation units. However, for households with the highest ex-ante conflict risk, these gains come at the cost of additional intimate partner violence.

中文摘要

家庭最具后果的经济决策通常是共同做出的，然而行为干预通常针对个人，并优化以克服个人内部的约束。我们使用一项随机对照试验来研究针对夫妻的行为干预能否克服与家庭内部决策相关的人际约束。在利比里亚农村地区，我们在一次大规模无条件现金转移之上叠加了一项有引导的联合财务规划练习，评估其相对于仅现金转移和纯对照组的效果。与仅现金转移相比，增加规划提高了用于生产性投资的转移金额比例，从而使经济结果改善了0.16个标准差单位。然而，对于事前冲突风险最高的家庭，这些收益是以增加亲密伴侣暴力为代价的。
