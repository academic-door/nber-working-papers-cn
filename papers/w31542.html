Who Benefits from Remote Schooling? Self-Selection and Match Effects

谁从远程教育中受益？自我选择与匹配效应

Jesse M. Bruhn, Christopher Campos, Eric Chyn, Anh Tran

教育与劳动 未指明区域

Abstract

We study the distributional effects of remote learning using a novel approach that combines preference data from a conjoint survey experiment with administrative student records. The experimentally derived preference data allow us to account for selection into remote learning while also studying selection patterns and treatment effect heterogeneity. We validate the approach using random variation from school choice lotteries. Our analysis of the average impacts of remote learning finds negative effects on reading (–0.13σ) and math (–0.14σ) achievement. Notably, we find evidence of positive learning effects for children whose parents have the strongest demand for remote learning. Parental concerns related to bullying appear to be an important driver of the demand for remote learning. Moreover, we find that across-the-board positive impacts of remote learning on bullying outcomes operate as a compensating differential for negative impacts on learning. Our results suggest that an important subset of students who currently sort into post-pandemic remote learning benefit from expanded choice.

中文摘要

我们利用一种结合联合调查实验偏好数据与行政学生记录的新方法，研究远程学习的分配效应。实验得出的偏好数据使我们能够解释远程学习的自我选择，同时研究选择模式和处理效应异质性。我们利用择校抽签产生的随机变异验证了该方法。对远程学习平均影响的分析发现，其对阅读成绩（-0.13σ）和数学成绩（-0.14σ）有负面效应。值得注意的是，我们发现对于父母对远程学习需求最强的儿童，存在积极的学习效应。与欺凌相关的家长担忧似乎是远程学习需求的重要驱动因素。此外，我们发现远程学习对欺凌结果的全面积极影响，作为对学习负面影响的补偿性差异发挥作用。我们的结果表明，在疫情后时期选择远程学习的学生中，有一个重要的子集从扩大的选择中受益。
