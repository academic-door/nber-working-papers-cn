Does Increasing Financial Access to Contraception in the U.S. Reduce Unintended Pregnancies? Evidence from the M-CARES Randomized Controlled Trial at Two Years

在美国增加避孕药具的经济可及性能否减少非意愿妊娠？来自M-CARES随机对照试验两年的证据

Martha J. Bailey, Emilia Brito Rebolledo, Deniz Gorgulu, Kelsey Figone, Vanessa W. Lang, Alexa Prettyman, Vanessa Dalton

环境与健康 美国

Abstract

We use a randomized controlled trial to examine how out-of-pocket costs affect contraceptive method choice, pregnancy, abortion, and childbirth among uninsured U.S. women. The study recruited women seeking care through Title X—a national family planning program subsidizing reproductive health services for low-income Americans—and randomized vouchers, making the full spectrum of contraception highly discounted or free. We find that subsidizing contraception has large and persistent effects on the choice of contraceptive method, resulting in significantly fewer pregnancies and abortions, but no effect on births, at 26 months.

中文摘要

我们利用一项随机对照试验，考察自付费用如何影响未参保美国女性的避孕方法选择、怀孕、堕胎和生育。该研究招募了通过Title X——一项为低收入美国人提供生殖健康服务补贴的国家计划生育项目——寻求护理的女性，并随机发放代金券，使全系列避孕药具大幅折扣或免费。我们发现，补贴避孕药具对避孕方法的选择产生了巨大且持续的影响，导致在26个月时怀孕和堕胎显著减少，但对生育没有影响。
