Estimating Trends In Mental Distress Using Survey Data

利用调查数据估计心理困扰趋势

Christopher J. Ruhm

环境与健康 美国

Abstract

One way of examining how mental health has changed over time is to use information available in large national surveys. While these survey-based measures are valuable in principle, and have been widely employed in prior research, a number of challenges may limit their practical usefulness or require considerable care in their application. This study uses data on U.S. 18-64 year olds from five major household surveys over the period 1999-2019 to investigate three specific questions. First, do different measures or the same indicator from different data sets consistently show how mental distress has changed over time? Second, how does the performance of single-item measures compare to those of psychometrically validated screeners based on multiple questions? Third, what are the benefits or potential costs of using different functional forms of these measures? The answers to these questions are as follows: 1) the estimated trends vary, often dramatically, across measures and, for the same indicator, across data sets and, as a result, some proxies provide a misleading understanding of how mental health has changed over time; 2) the single-item measures do as well or better than those based on multiple questions; 3) no single functional form demonstrates clear superiority over the others. After demonstrating these points, lessons are elucidated for researchers attempting to use survey data to examine trends in mental health.

中文摘要

考察心理健康随时间变化的一种方法是利用大型全国性调查中的可用信息。虽然这些基于调查的指标原则上很有价值，并已在先前研究中被广泛使用，但若干挑战可能限制其实用性，或需要在其应用中格外谨慎。本研究利用1999-2019年期间来自五项主要家庭调查的美国18-64岁人群数据，探讨三个具体问题。第一，来自不同数据集的、不同的衡量指标或同一指标是否一致地显示了心理困扰随时间的变化？第二，单项指标的表现与基于多个问题的、经过心理测量学验证的筛查工具相比如何？第三，使用这些指标的不同函数形式有哪些益处或潜在成本？对这些问题的回答如下：1）估计的趋势在不同指标之间，以及对于同一指标在不同数据集之间，往往存在显著差异，有时甚至差异巨大，因此，一些代理变量会提供关于心理健康随时间如何变化的误导性理解；2）单项指标的表现与基于多个问题的指标相当或更好；3）没有单一的函数形式显示出明显优于其他形式。在论证了这些要点之后，本文为试图利用调查数据研究心理健康趋势的研究者阐明了经验教训。
