Fallow Lengths and the Structure of Property Rights

休耕期与产权结构

Etienne Le Rossignol, Sara Lowes, Eduardo Montero

环境与健康 全球/多国

Abstract

We study a fundamental institution in many societies: the structure of property rights over land. Across societies, communal land rights have been more common than private land rights. We test the hypothesis that longer fallow requirements – the time needed to leave land uncultivated to restore fertility based on ecological features of the soil and climate – led to a higher prevalence of communal property rights. Longer fallow requirements generate higher protection costs and therefore make communal rights more beneficial. We construct an ecological measure of the optimal fallow length for the most suitable staple crop across grid cells globally based on soil type, temperature, and climate. We find that places where land needs to be fallowed for longer periods are more likely to have communal property rights both historically and presently. We then examine the implications for efforts to title land. Using World Bank project ratings and academic impact evaluations, we find that land interventions are less effective and positive estimated impacts are systematically less common in settings with longer fallow requirements, suggesting a mismatch between development policy and the underlying context. Our results provide insights into the origins of property rights structures and the importance of aligning development policies with the cultural and institutional setting.

中文摘要

我们研究了许多社会中的一项基本制度：土地产权结构。在不同社会中，共有土地权利比私有土地权利更为普遍。我们检验了以下假设：更长的休耕要求——即根据土壤和气候的生态特征，土地需要闲置以恢复肥力的时间——导致了共有产权制度的更高普及率。更长的休耕要求会产生更高的保护成本，从而使共有权利更加有利。我们基于土壤类型、温度和气候，构建了一个全球网格单元上最适合主粮作物的最优休耕长度的生态指标。我们发现，历史上和当前，土地需要休耕更长时间的地区更可能拥有共有产权制度。然后，我们考察了这对土地确权努力的影响。利用世界银行项目评级和学术影响评估，我们发现，在休耕要求更长的环境中，土地干预措施效果较差，且正面估计影响系统性较少，这表明发展政策与基本背景之间存在错配。我们的结果为产权制度的起源以及使发展政策与文化制度环境保持一致的重要性提供了见解。
