The Effect of E-cigarette Taxes on Substance Use

电子烟税对物质使用的影响

Dhaval M. Dave, Yang Liang, Johanna Catherine Maclean, Caterina Muratori, Joseph J. Sabia

公共财政与税收 未指明区域

Abstract

Public health advocates warn that the rapid growth of legal markets for electronic nicotine delivery systems (ENDS) may generate a “gateway” to marijuana and harder drug consumption, particularly among teenagers. This study explores the effects of ENDS taxes on substance use. We find that a one-dollar increase in ENDS taxes (2023$) is associated with a 1.0 to 1.5 percentage point decline in teen marijuana use and in co-use of ENDS and marijuana. This result is consistent with e-cigarettes and marijuana being economic complements. We also find that youth responses to ENDS taxes, in terms of their ENDS use and spillovers into marijuana use, appear to moderate over the longer term. We find no evidence that ENDS taxes affect drug treatment admissions or consumption of illicit drugs other than marijuana such as cocaine, methamphetamine, or opioids.

中文摘要

公共卫生倡导者警告称，电子尼古丁输送系统合法市场的快速增长可能为青少年吸食大麻和更烈性毒品创造“门户”。本研究探讨了电子烟税对物质使用的影响。我们发现，电子烟税每增加1美元（按2023年美元计算），青少年大麻使用以及电子烟与大麻共同使用的比例下降1.0至1.5个百分点。这一结果与电子烟和大麻是经济互补品的结论一致。我们还发现，青少年对电子烟税的反应，即其电子烟使用及向大麻使用的溢出效应，似乎在长期内趋于缓和。我们没有发现证据表明电子烟税会影响药物滥用治疗入院率或除大麻以外的非法药物（如可卡因、甲基苯丙胺或阿片类药物）的消费。
