Do Wage Floors Increase Employment Risk for Workers with Disabilities? Evidence from Minimum Wage Increases and Subminimum Wage Repeal

工资下限是否增加残疾劳动者的就业风险？来自最低工资上调与次最低工资废除的证据

Roisin O'Neill, Shailee Manandhar, Douglas L. Kruse

其他主题 全球/多国

Abstract

People with disabilities are disproportionately represented in low-wage work, raising concerns that higher wage floors may reduce their employment opportunities, particularly for workers with more severe disabilities. We examine the effects of state minimum wage increases and state subminimum wage terminations on employment outcomes for people with disabilities using American Community Survey data from 2010–2023. We find little evidence that minimum wage increases reduce employment or labor force participation among people with disabilities, including those with more severe disabilities. While many estimates are statistically imprecise, confidence intervals generally rule out economically meaningful negative employment effects. We likewise find no evidence that terminating subminimum wages reduces employment opportunities for affected workers, and some estimates suggest positive employment effects for groups most likely to have been employed under subminimum wage arrangements. These gains may reflect complementary policies that often accompany repeal, such as Employment First initiatives. Overall, the results provide little support for the view that higher wage floors create disproportionate employment barriers for people with disabilities.

中文摘要

残疾人在低工资工作中占比过高，这引发担忧：更高的工资下限可能减少其就业机会，尤其对重度残疾劳动者而言。我们利用2010-2023年美国社区调查数据，考察州最低工资上调与次最低工资终止对残疾人就业结果的影响。几乎没有证据表明最低工资上调会减少残疾人的就业或劳动参与，包括重度残疾者。尽管许多估计在统计上不精确，但置信区间通常排除了经济意义上显著的负面就业效应。同样，我们没有发现终止次最低工资会减少受影响工人就业机会的证据，且部分估计表明，最可能受次最低工资安排影响的群体出现了正面就业效应。这些收益可能反映了废除政策常伴随的补充性措施，如“就业优先”倡议。总体而言，结果不支持更高工资下限会为残疾人制造不成比例就业障碍的观点。
