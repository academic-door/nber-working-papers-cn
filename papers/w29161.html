Demographics, Wealth, and Global Imbalances in the Twenty-First Century

二十一世纪的人口结构、财富与全球失衡

Adrien Auclert, Hannes Malmberg, Frederic Martenet, Matthew Rognlie

公共财政与税收 全球/多国

Abstract

We use a sufficient statistic approach to quantify the general equilibrium effects of population aging on the returns to wealth, wealth accumulation, and global imbalances. Combining population forecasts with household survey data from 25 countries, we measure the compositional effect of aging: how a changing age distribution affects wealth-to-GDP, holding the age profiles of wealth and labor income fixed. In a baseline overlapping generations model this statistic, in conjunction with cross-sectional information and two standard macro parameters, pins down general equilibrium outcomes. Since the compositional effect is positive, large, and heterogeneous across countries, our model predicts that population aging will lower returns to wealth, increase wealth-to-GDP ratios, and widen global imbalances through the twenty-first century. These conclusions extend to a richer model in which bequests, individual savings, and the tax-and-transfer system all respond to demographic change.

中文摘要

我们使用一个充分统计量方法来量化人口老龄化对财富回报率、财富积累和全球失衡的一般均衡效应。结合来自25个国家的家庭调查数据与人口预测，我们衡量了老龄化的构成效应：在保持财富和劳动收入的年龄分布不变的情况下，年龄分布的变化如何影响财富与GDP之比。在一个基准的代际交叠模型中，该统计量结合横截面信息和两个标准宏观参数，可以确定一般均衡结果。由于构成效应为正、规模较大且在不同国家间存在异质性，我们的模型预测，人口老龄化将在整个二十一世纪降低财富回报率、提高财富与GDP之比，并加剧全球失衡。这些结论可以推广到一个更丰富的模型，在该模型中，遗产、个人储蓄以及税收与转移支付体系都会对人口结构变化做出反应。
