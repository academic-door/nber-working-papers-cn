The Design of Defined Contribution Plans

固定缴款计划的设计

Vivek Bhattacharya, Gastón Illanes

理论与方法 未指明区域

Abstract

Defined contribution plans are a major vehicle for retirement savings in the US, holding almost $\$10$ trillion in assets under management. In recent years, the quality and availability of these plans has been the subject of active policy attention and of several major lawsuits. This paper studies how employers and plan providers design these plans. We argue that low plan quality and limited provision can come from two sources. First, employer willingness to pay may be misaligned with that of workers or of regulators. Second, the market for plan provision may be imperfectly competitive. We propose a model of plan design and estimate that while both frictions are at play, significant changes to plan quality require modifying employer preferences. Accordingly, we evaluate proposed policies and conclude that only direct quality regulation can lead to significant quality improvements, although such regulation reduces plan provision.

中文摘要

固定缴款计划是美国退休储蓄的主要工具，管理着近10万亿美元的资产。近年来，这些计划的质量和可用性一直是政策关注和几起重大诉讼的主题。本文研究了雇主和计划提供者如何设计这些计划。我们认为，低计划质量和有限的提供可能来自两个来源。首先，雇主的支付意愿可能与工人或监管机构的支付意愿不一致。其次，计划提供市场可能是不完全竞争的。我们提出了一个计划设计模型，并估计虽然两种摩擦都在起作用，但计划质量的显著改变需要修改雇主的偏好。因此，我们评估了拟议的政策，并得出结论，只有直接的质量监管才能带来显著的质量改进，尽管这种监管会减少计划的提供。
