The Persistence of Power: How Family Origins Shape Political Representation and Policy

权力的持久性：家庭出身如何塑造政治代表性和政策

Eric Chyn, Katherine Cohen, Kareem Haggag, Bryan A. Stuart

环境与健康 美国

Abstract

In the United States, long hailed as the land of opportunity, is access to political office truly open across society, or do the most privileged children disproportionately rise to enter political life? This question speaks to a longstanding concern that elite families may entrench themselves in positions of power, reproducing a form of hereditary privilege within a democratic system. We study the family backgrounds of U.S. politicians over the late nineteenth and early twentieth centuries and show that children from wealthy and privileged households have been substantially overrepresented in elected office. This imbalance has changed little over time and, at the highest levels of office, varies little across political parties. To test whether political access depends on family resources, we exploit the sudden economic shock caused by the end of slavery. Despite the large and concentrated losses at the top of the wealth distribution, the children of slaveholders continued to enter government at high rates. Finally, we examine whether politicians' socioeconomic origins shape policy by constructing a new sample of close elections linked to detailed information on U.S. House candidates' family backgrounds. Comparing otherwise similar districts in which a candidate from a high socioeconomic status family narrowly wins rather than loses, we find that districts represented by higher status candidates are less likely to support pro-tax positions in roll-call voting. Together, the evidence across our analyses shows that family background strongly predicts entry into political office and has measurable consequences for policy choices.

中文摘要

在美国这个长期被誉为机遇之地的国家，进入政治职位的途径是否真正向社会各阶层开放，还是最特权的子女不成比例地崛起进入政治生活？这个问题涉及一个长期存在的担忧，即精英家庭可能固守权力职位，在民主制度内复制一种世袭特权形式。我们研究了19世纪末20世纪初美国政治家的家庭背景，并表明来自富裕和特权家庭的子女在民选职位中一直占据显著过高的比例。这种不平衡随着时间的推移变化甚微，并且在最高级别的职位中，不同政党之间差异很小。为了检验政治准入是否取决于家庭资源，我们利用了奴隶制终结造成的突然经济冲击。尽管财富顶层遭受了巨大且集中的损失，但奴隶主的子女继续以高比例进入政府。最后，我们通过构建一个新的与美国家庭背景详细信息相关联的众议院候选人紧密选举样本，检验了政治家的社会经济出身是否塑造政策。比较那些来自高社会经济地位家庭的候选人以微弱优势获胜而非失败的原本相似的选区，我们发现由地位较高候选人代表的选区在唱名投票中支持支持税收立场的可能性较低。综合来看，我们分析中的证据表明，家庭背景强烈预测进入政治职位，并对政策选择产生可衡量的影响。
