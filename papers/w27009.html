Covid-19 Infection Externalities: Trading Off Lives vs. Livelihoods

COVID-19感染外部性：生命与生计的权衡

Zachary A. Bethune, Anton Korinek

理论与方法 未指明区域

Abstract

We analyze the externalities that arise when social and economic interactions transmit infectious diseases such as COVID-19. Individually rational agents do not internalize that they impose infection externalities upon others when the disease is transmitted. In an SIR model calibrated to capture the main features of COVID-19 in the US economy, we show that private agents perceive the cost an additional infection to be around $\$80$k whereas the social cost including infection externalities is more than three times higher, around $\$286$k. This misvaluation has stark implications for how society ultimately overcomes the disease: for a population of individually rational agents, the precautionary behavior by the susceptible flattens the curve of infections, but the disease is not overcome until herd immunity is acquired. The resulting economic cost is high; an initial sharp decline in aggregate output followed by a slow recovery over several years. By contrast, the socially optimal approach in our model focuses public policy measures on the infected in order to contain the disease and quickly eradicate it, which produces a much milder recession. If targeting the infected is impossible, the optimal policy in our model is still to aggressively contain and eliminate the disease, and the social cost of an extra infection rises to $\$586$k.

中文摘要

我们分析了当社会和经济互动传播传染病（如COVID-19）时产生的外部性。当疾病传播时，个体理性代理人没有内化他们强加给其他人的感染外部性。在一个校准以捕捉美国经济中COVID-19主要特征的SIR模型中，我们表明私人代理人认为额外感染的成本约为8万美元，而包括感染外部性在内的社会成本则高出三倍多，约为28.6万美元。这种错误估值对社会最终如何战胜疾病具有显著影响：对于个体理性代理人群体，易感者的预防行为使感染曲线趋平，但疾病直到获得群体免疫才被克服。由此产生的经济成本很高；总产出最初急剧下降，随后是持续数年的缓慢复苏。相比之下，我们模型中的社会最优方法将公共政策措施集中于感染者，以控制疾病并迅速根除它，这会产生温和得多的衰退。如果无法针对感染者，我们模型中的最优政策仍然是积极控制和消除疾病，此时额外感染的社会成本上升至58.6万美元。
