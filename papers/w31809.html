China’s Nationwide CO2 Emissions Trading System: A General Equilibrium Assessment

中国全国性二氧化碳排放交易体系：一般均衡评估

Lawrence H. Goulder, Xianling Long, Chenfei Qu, Da Zhang

环境与健康 中国相关

Abstract

China’s recently launched CO2 emissions trading system, already the world’s largest, aims to contribute importantly toward global reductions in greenhouse gas emissions. The system, a tradable performance standard (TPS), differs importantly from cap and trade (C&T), the principal emissions trading approach used in other countries. This paper presents the structure and results from a multi-sector, multi-period equilibrium model tailored to evaluate China’s TPS. The model incorporates distinctive features of China’s economy, including state-owned enterprises and electricity market regulation. It distinguishes between the TPS and C&T and considers a wide range of potential future TPS designs.\nKey findings include the following. The TPS’s environmental benefits exceed its costs by a factor of five when only the climate-related benefits are considered and by a significantly higher factor when health benefits from improved air quality are included. The TPS’s interactions with China’s fiscal system substantially affect its costs relative to those of C&T. Employing a single benchmark (standard) for the electricity sector would lower costs by 34 percent relative to the four-benchmark system that is actually in place but increase the standard deviation of percentage income losses across provinces by more than 60 percent. Introducing an auction as a complementary source of allowance supply can lower economy-wide costs by at least 30 percent.

中文摘要

中国最近启动的二氧化碳排放交易体系，已是全球规模最大的此类体系，旨在为全球温室气体减排做出重要贡献。该体系是一种可交易绩效标准，与世界上其他国家主要采用的排放交易方法——总量控制与交易——有重要区别。本文介绍了一个为评估中国可交易绩效标准而量身定制的多部门、多时期均衡模型的结构和结果。该模型纳入了中国经济的独特特征，包括国有企业和电力市场监管。它区分了可交易绩效标准与总量控制与交易，并考虑了未来可交易绩效标准设计的多种可能方案。主要发现包括：仅考虑气候相关效益时，可交易绩效标准的环境效益是其成本的五倍；若纳入空气质量改善带来的健康效益，则倍数显著更高。可交易绩效标准与中国财政体系的相互作用对其相对于总量控制与交易的成本有显著影响。对电力部门采用单一基准，相对于实际运行的四基准体系，可将成本降低34%，但会使各省份收入损失百分比的标准差增加超过60%。引入拍卖作为配额供给的补充来源，可将整个经济体的成本降低至少30%。
