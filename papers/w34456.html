The Consequences of Faculty Sexual Misconduct

教师性行为不端的后果

Sarah R. Cohodes, Katherine B. Leu

教育与劳动 未指明区域

Abstract

Faculty sexual misconduct targeted at students is a widespread problem. The consequences of such incidents include direct harm to victims and may also entail a loss to science if students who encounter misconduct become discouraged from continuing their studies in their chosen field. We link publicly available information on degree completion by institution, academic field, and gender to a database of faculty sexual misconduct incidents verified in the media or court cases covering the years from 1983 to 2023. Then, we employ a stacked event study approach to document the extent to which publicly reported faculty sexual misconduct decreases in-field degree completion. Exposure to a field-specific faculty sexual misconduct public report decreases degree completion in that field by 4.7 percent four years after the incident. This decline is driven by reports occurring in 2015 and after, among which we observe a 7.9 percent decline in in-field degree completion. Students exit majors dominated by men, but this shift has little effect on predicted earnings because students shift away from both high- and low-earning majors.

中文摘要

针对学生的教师性行为不端是一个普遍存在的问题。此类事件的后果包括对受害者的直接伤害，也可能导致科学损失，如果遭遇不端行为的学生因气馁而不再继续在其所选领域学习。我们将按机构、学术领域和性别划分的学位完成公开信息与1983年至2023年间经媒体或法庭案件核实的教师性行为不端事件数据库相关联。然后，我们采用堆叠事件研究方法，记录公开报道的教师性行为不端在多大程度上减少了领域内的学位完成。接触特定领域的教师性行为不端公开报告，会使该领域四年后的学位完成率下降4.7%。这种下降是由2015年及之后发生的报告驱动的，在这些报告中，我们观察到领域内学位完成率下降了7.9%。学生退出男性主导的专业，但这种转变对预测收入影响甚微，因为学生从高收入和低收入专业中都有转移。
