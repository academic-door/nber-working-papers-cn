ICE Arrests across Trump’s First and Second Terms: Variation in Targeting, Method, and Geography

特朗普第一和第二任期内的ICE逮捕行动：目标、方法和地理位置的差异

Chloe N. East, Caitlin Patler, Elizabeth Cox

其他主题 未指明区域

Abstract

Deportation is often framed as a necessary tool to protect public safety by removing people who commit crimes. We use newly available, and externally validated, administrative data containing US Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) arrests from September 2015-October 2025. Beyond demonstrating national trends in immigration arrests by method and composition over time, we are able to compare, for the first time, apprehensions spanning the start of the two Trump administrations, both of which focused on mass immigration enforcement. Our results reveal that the reality of immigration enforcement diverges sharply from the public narrative: while arrests spiked at the outset of both Trump presidencies, there were significant declines in the percentage of arrested individuals with criminal convictions, with especially marked declines in 2025. Examining potential mechanisms reveals that this is partly driven by a change in ICE tactics, but even conditional on tactic, as arrests rose, the percent with a criminal record declined. Moreover, we find substantial heterogeneity over time and across ICE Areas of Responsibility. Taken together, our results highlight a substantial gap between political rhetoric and reality.

中文摘要

驱逐出境通常被描述为通过驱逐犯罪者来保护公共安全的必要工具。我们使用了新近可获得且经过外部验证的行政数据，其中包含2015年9月至2025年10月期间美国移民和海关执法局（ICE）的逮捕记录。除了按方法和构成展示全国范围内移民逮捕随时间变化的趋势外，我们首次能够比较特朗普两届政府任期开始时的逮捕情况，这两届政府都专注于大规模移民执法。我们的结果显示，移民执法的现实与公众叙事存在显著差异：尽管在特朗普两届总统任期之初逮捕人数激增，但被逮捕者中有刑事定罪的比例显著下降，尤其是在2025年下降更为明显。考察潜在机制发现，这在一定程度上是由ICE策略的改变所驱动，但即使在策略不变的情况下，随着逮捕人数上升，有犯罪记录者的比例也在下降。此外，我们发现不同时期和不同ICE责任区之间存在显著的异质性。综合来看，我们的结果突显了政治言论与现实之间的巨大差距。
