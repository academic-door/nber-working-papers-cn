Planning Capital and Discovery-Based Learning-by-Doing: Investment as Staged Discovery in the Hyperscale Era

规划资本与基于发现的干中学：超大规模时代的投资作为分阶段发现

Andrew Caplin

环境与健康 未指明区域

Abstract

This paper treats frontier investment as staged discovery. Firms invest through sequences of costly inquiry stages before deciding whether and how to realize value. At each stage, a firm may sharpen its diagnostic understanding, choose what question to ask, embody that question in a costly action, and interpret the answer that action produces. The answer changes what the firm knows, what it can do next, and how costly future inquiry will be. Successful histories can also carry over to later related problems, making the firm a better learner beyond the current investment path.\nThe paper calls the resulting asset planning capital in firm form. Planning capital is the accumulated diagnostic understanding a firm has built through costly inquiry and operation: understanding of technologies, processes, customers, tools, partners, failures, constraints, and opportunities, together with the capacity to keep learning. It becomes valuable because it helps the firm choose what to investigate, make the question answerable, interpret what happened, and carry what it learned into later investment stages. Building on classical investment theory, R&D models, real-options theory, venture-capital models, classical learning-by-doing, the paper develops a model of investment as staged discovery. \nThe AI and semiconductor frontier makes this asset visible. Firms are being valued and funded for more than the physical capacity they already own. Chips, fabs, data centers, cloud platforms, power commitments, and model systems matter because they carry traces of accumulated learning and because their use generates more learning. They let firms train models, test demand, expose bottlenecks, improve yields, qualify customers, integrate tools, and discover which complements are missing. The current buildout is therefore investment in capacity and in the institutional learning system that makes capacity more valuable over time.\nThe framework explains why production systems, tool systems, research paths, sovereign capability programs, and infrastructure sponsorship belong in the same analysis. They are different institutional forms through which planning capital is accumulated. The paper treats terminal realization value in reduced form and focuses on the discovery process that precedes it: how institutions build capacity, learn from using it, and turn accumulated diagnostic understanding into an advantage in the next round of investment.

中文摘要

本文将前沿投资视为分阶段发现。企业在决定是否以及如何实现价值之前，通过一系列代价高昂的探究阶段进行投资。在每个阶段，企业可能深化其诊断性理解，选择要提出的问题，将该问题体现在代价高昂的行动中，并解读该行动产生的答案。答案改变了企业所知、下一步能做什么以及未来探究的成本。成功的历史还可以延续到后续相关问题，使企业在当前投资路径之外成为更好的学习者。 本文将由此产生的资产称为企业形式的规划资本。规划资本是企业通过代价高昂的探究和运营积累的诊断性理解：对技术、流程、客户、工具、合作伙伴、失败、约束和机会的理解，以及持续学习的能力。它之所以有价值，是因为它帮助企业选择要调查的内容、使问题可回答、解读发生的情况，并将所学带入后续投资阶段。基于经典投资理论、研发模型、实物期权理论、风险投资模型和经典干中学，本文构建了投资作为分阶段发现的模型。 人工智能和半导体前沿使这一资产变得可见。企业的估值和融资已超越其已拥有的物理产能。芯片、晶圆厂、数据中心、云平台、电力承诺和模型系统之所以重要，是因为它们承载了累积学习的痕迹，且其使用能产生更多学习。它们让企业训练模型、测试需求、暴露瓶颈、改进良率、认证客户、集成工具，并发现哪些互补品缺失。因此，当前的扩建既是对产能的投资，也是对使产能随时间更具价值的制度性学习系统的投资。 该框架解释了为何生产系统、工具系统、研究路径、主权能力项目和基础设施赞助应纳入同一分析。它们是规划资本积累的不同制度形式。本文以简化形式处理终端实现价值，并聚焦于其前的发现过程：机构如何建设产能、通过使用产能学习，并将累积的诊断性理解转化为下一轮投资中的优势。
