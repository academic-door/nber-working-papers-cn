Diagnostic Thresholds, Written Consent Requirements, and the Utilization of Scarce Organs

诊断阈值、书面同意要求与稀缺器官的利用

Hannah Bae, Jeffrey Clemens

环境与健康 美国

Abstract

This paper explores how diagnostic thresholds and a written consent requirement affect decision making between patients and transplant surgeons regarding the use of deceased donor kidneys. Combining administrative data on the universe of organ donors and transplant recipients in the United States, we show that age-based discontinuities within the Expanded Criteria Donor system (ECD), which was in place from late 2002 to late 2014, led to 4.0 and 5.4 percentage point differences in the rates at which otherwise indistinguishable kidneys were discarded. A discontinuity within the subsequent presentation of the Kidney Donor Profile Index (KDPI) led to a 2.6 percentage point difference. We present evidence that diagnostic thresholds have nuanced effects on decision making and information acquisition; procurement biopsy rates, for example, rose substantially at the ECD system’s age-based discontinuities. We further show that written consent requirements triggered within the ECD and KDPI labeling systems substantially reduced the number of potential recipients to whom kidneys were offered prior to being discarded. This channel can explain the majority of the increased rate at which “High-KDPI” kidneys are discarded under the current system.

中文摘要

本文探讨诊断阈值和书面同意要求如何影响患者与移植外科医生关于使用 deceased donor kidneys 的决策。结合美国所有器官捐献者和移植受者的行政数据，我们表明，在2002年底至2014年底实施的扩展标准供体（ECD）系统中，基于年龄的不连续性导致原本无法区分的肾脏被丢弃的比率出现4.0和5.4个百分点的差异。随后在肾脏供体概况指数（KDPI）呈现中的不连续性导致了2.6个百分点的差异。我们提供的证据表明，诊断阈值对决策和信息获取具有细微影响；例如，在ECD系统的年龄不连续性处，采购活检率大幅上升。我们进一步表明，在ECD和KDPI标签系统中触发的书面同意要求，显著减少了肾脏在被丢弃前被提供给潜在受体的数量。这一渠道可以解释在当前系统下“高KDPI”肾脏被丢弃比率上升的大部分原因。
