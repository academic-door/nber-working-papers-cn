Do Environmental Markets Improve Allocative Efficiency? Evidence from U.S. Air Pollution

环境市场能提高配置效率吗？来自美国空气污染的证据

Kyle C. Meng, Vincent Thivierge

环境与健康 美国

Abstract

The central appeal of environmental markets – efficient allocation of emission reductions – has been difficult to validate empirically. We develop a framework linking the theoretical change in allocative efficiency following a market-based policy to a quasi-experimental estimator. We apply this framework with administrative data to two major U.S. air pollution markets. We find allocative efficiency for pollution improved by 3.3 percentage points annually under California’s RECLAIM program but do not detect a change under the U.S.’ NOₓ Budget Trading Program. These results are supported by greater heterogeneity in baseline facility characteristics under RECLAIM and differences in program implementation.

中文摘要

环境市场的核心吸引力——减排量的有效配置——一直难以通过实证验证。我们构建了一个框架，将市场导向政策后配置效率的理论变化与准实验估计量联系起来。我们利用行政数据将该框架应用于美国两个主要的空气污染市场。我们发现，在加利福尼亚州的RECLAIM计划下，污染配置效率每年提高了3.3个百分点，但在美国的NOₓ预算交易计划下未检测到变化。这些结果得到了RECLAIM计划下设施基线特征更大异质性以及计划实施差异的支持。
