Innovation Spillovers across U.S. Tech Clusters

美国科技集群间的创新溢出

Xavier Giroud, Ernest Liu, Holger Mueller

理论与方法 美国

Abstract

The vast majority of U.S. inventors work for firms that also have inventors and plants in other tech clusters. Using merged USPTO–U.S. Census Bureau plant-level data, we show that larger tech clusters not only make local inventors more productive but also raise the productivity of inventors and plants in other clusters, which are connected to the focal cluster through their parent firms’ networks of innovating plants. Cross-cluster innovation spillovers do not depend on the physical distance between clusters, and plants cite disproportionately more patents from other firms in connected clusters, across large physical distances. To rationalize these findings, and to inform policy, we develop a tractable model of spatial innovation that features both within- and cross-cluster innovation spillovers. Based on our model, we derive a sufficient statistic for the wedge between the social and private returns to innovation in a given location. Taking the model to the data, we rank all U.S. tech clusters according to this wedge. While larger tech clusters exhibit a greater social-private innovation wedge, this is not because of local knowledge spillovers, but because they are well-connected to other clusters through fims’ networks of innovating plants. In counterfactual exercises, we show that an increase in the interconnectedness of U.S. tech clusters raises the social-private innovation wedge in (almost) all locations, but especially in tech clusters that are large and well-connected to other clusters.

中文摘要

绝大多数美国发明家受雇于那些在其他科技集群也拥有发明家和工厂的企业。利用合并的美国专利商标局（USPTO）与美国人口普查局工厂级数据，我们表明，较大的科技集群不仅提高了本地发明家的生产率，还提高了其他集群中发明家和工厂的生产率，这些其他集群通过其母公司的创新工厂网络与焦点集群相连。跨集群创新溢出不依赖于集群间的物理距离，并且工厂引用来自相连集群中其他企业的专利比例更高，即使物理距离遥远。为合理化这些发现并为政策提供信息，我们构建了一个可处理的空间创新模型，该模型兼具集群内和跨集群创新溢出特征。基于我们的模型，我们推导出一个充分统计量，用于衡量给定地点创新的社会回报与私人回报之间的楔子。将模型应用于数据，我们根据这一楔子对所有美国科技集群进行排序。虽然较大的科技集群表现出更大的社会-私人创新楔子，但这并非由于本地知识溢出，而是因为它们通过企业的创新工厂网络与其他集群紧密相连。在反事实模拟中，我们表明，美国科技集群互联性的提高会提升（几乎所有）地点的社会-私人创新楔子，尤其是在那些规模大且与其他集群联系紧密的科技集群中。
