The Limits of onetary Economics: On Money as a Latent Medium of Exchange

货币经济学的局限：论货币作为潜在交换媒介

Ricardo Lagos, Shengxing Zhang

宏观与货币财政 未指明区域

Abstract

We formulate a generalization of the traditional medium-of-exchange function of money in contexts where there is imperfect competition in the intermediation of credit, settlement, or payment services used to conduct transactions. We ﬁnd that the option to settle transactions with money strengthens the stance of sellers of goods and services vis-à-vis intermediaries, and show this mechanism is operative even for sellers who never exercise the option to sell for cash. These latent money demand considerations imply that in general, in contrast to current conventional wisdom in policy-oriented research in monetary economics, monetary policy remains eﬀective through medium-of-exchange transmission channels—even in highly developed credit economies where the share of monetary transactions is negligible.

中文摘要

我们在信贷、结算或支付服务中介存在不完全竞争的交易环境中，对货币的传统交换媒介功能进行了概括。我们发现，用货币结算交易的选择增强了商品和服务卖方相对于中介机构的地位，并表明即使对于从未行使过现金销售选择权的卖方，这一机制也起作用。这些潜在的货币需求考虑意味着，总体而言，与当前货币政策导向研究中货币经济学的传统观点相反，货币政策通过交换媒介传导渠道仍然有效——即使在货币交易份额微不足道的高度发达的信用经济中也是如此。
