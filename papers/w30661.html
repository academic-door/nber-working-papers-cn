Who Benefits from Hazardous Waste Cleanups? Evidence from the Housing Market

谁从危险废物清理中受益？来自住房市场的证据

Alecia W. Cassidy, Elaine L. Hill, Lala Ma

其他主题 未指明区域

Abstract

The Resource Conservation and Recovery Act (RCRA) manages cleanup of hazardous waste releases at over 3,500 sites across the US, which covers approximately 17.9 million of all developed land in the country. We use publicly available Census survey data (Decennial and American Communities Survey) to evaluate the national and distributional housing market impacts of these cleanups. We find that cleanups near residential properties yield significant, yet localized, increases in home prices, and that impacts are concentrated in the lower deciles of the price distribution. Importantly, we find fairly weak evidence of sorting along sociodemographic dimensions in response to cleanup. Our findings suggest that cleanup benefits accrue to the residents who are the original ‘hosts’ of pollution and could correct pre-existing disparities in exposure to land-based contamination.

中文摘要

《资源保护与回收法案》（RCRA）管理着美国超过3500个场地的危险废物释放清理工作，覆盖了全国约1790万英亩的所有已开发土地。我们利用公开可用的人口普查调查数据（十年一次的人口普查和美国社区调查）来评估这些清理工作对全国及不同分布区域的住房市场影响。我们发现，住宅物业附近的清理工作带来了显著但局部化的房价上涨，且影响集中在价格分布的低十分位数组。重要的是，我们发现关于清理后按社会人口维度进行居住分层的证据相当薄弱。我们的研究结果表明，清理带来的好处归于最初遭受污染的居民，并可能纠正先前存在的土地污染暴露方面的不平等。
